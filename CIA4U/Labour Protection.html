Labour Protection

According to McMillan LLP, “Although in many ways similar, there is a considerable difference in focus between the United States and Canada regarding employment and labor relations. In essence, Canadian law presumes the vulnerability of employees and provides protections and minimum standards with which all employers must comply. 

American eyes show Canada has a strong employee or labor bias that is less evident in the United States. American law stresses a higher level of contractual liberty and fewer prescribed standards instead.” Canada is also more union-friendly than the USA, with an average total unionization rate between 2006 and 2010 in the U.S. is 13.1%, compared to 31.5%.
