Privatization

is transferring a business, industry, or service from public to private ownership and control. The reason for government-owned and regulated businesses, also known as crown corporations, such as transportation or electricity, is often because of economies of scale.

One historical example of privatization is Petro Canada.

 

Timeline

  • 1975

    Created by the Trudeau Liberals to protect and develop a Canadian presence in the energy sector

  • 1979

    The company discovers (with partners Chevron, Mobil, and Gulf) the Hibernia oil field off Newfoundland

  • 1982

    Discovers oil at Valhalla, Alberta

  • 1984

    Discovers Terra Nova oil field off Newfoundland

  • 1990

    Governing Mulroney Tories announce privatization of Petro-Canada

  • 1991

    First shares of Petro-Canada sold to the public

  • 1995

    Ottawa reduces its interest in the company to 20 percent

  • 2004

    Martin Liberals announce they will sell off the remaining stake in the company.
