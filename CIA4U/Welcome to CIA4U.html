Unit Title

Description

Unit 1: Fundamentals of Economics

Students learn about basic ideas like scarcity, opportunity cost, and supply and demand. They also explore different economic systems, such as capitalism and socialism, and learn the basics of microeconomics and macroeconomics. This unit helps students understand how people and societies decide how to use their resources. 

Unit 2: Firms, Markets, and Economic Stakeholders 

"Firms, Markets, and Economic Stakeholders," looks at how businesses work in different markets, like competitive ones and monopolies. It studies the roles of consumers, companies, and governments in the economy and talks about how prices and job markets change. 

Unit 3: Macroeconomics 

In the "Macroeconomics" unit, the course looks at the entire economy. It covers topics like the total national income (GDP), job rates, and inflation. This unit also discusses important government policies related to spending and money supply and examines why economies grow or go into recession. 
Unit 4: Global Interdependence and Inequalities

The unit "Global Interdependence and Inequalities" explores how the world's economies are connected. It looks at globalization, international trade, and the roles of organizations like the IMF and World Bank. The unit also focuses on the differences in wealth within and between countries, helping students understand the varied economic situations around the world.
