Perfect Competition

Characteristics: Examples:
  • many buyers and many sellers 
  • buyers and sellers have to know the prices
  • sell identical products
  • sellers are price takers
  • firms can enter/exit without restrictions
  • grain producers 
  • Stock market
  • tuna fishermen

Monopolistic Competition

Characteristics: Examples: 
  • many sellers and buyers
  • producers are selling differentiated but close substitute products
  • differentiate through product differences, branding differences, marketing differences, or distribution differences
  • no one business has complete control over market prices, have some control over price
  • there is the freedom to enter/exit the market
  • coffee shops
  • grocery stores
  • pharmacies
  • gas stations
  • hotels
  • hardware/home improvement stores
  • automotive service
  • fast food restaurants

Monopolies and Anti-Competitive

Characteristics: Examples:
  • one seller, no close substitutes; many buyers
  • price control
  • barriers to entry
  • natural gas
  • electricity
  • some medications (for which patents have not expired)

Oligopoly 

Characteristics: Examples:
  • Few sellers exist in this market structure, and those that do dominate it (oligopolies exist if four or fewer companies own more significant than 50% of the total market share)
  • Entry and exit barriers are usually very high.
  • Oligopolies are created in several ways, although noncompetitive practices, government mandates, or advanced technology are usually precursors.
  • some control over the price
  • barriers to entry
  • Apple, 
  • Amazon,
  • Netflix, 
  • Air Canada, 
  • OPEC
  • Automakers
