Monetary Policy

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will explore monetary policy, a fundamental aspect of macroeconomic management. Monetary policy involves controlling the money supply and interest rates to achieve economic goals. We'll delve into its objectives, such as price stability and full employment, and the tools central banks use, like open market operations and discount rates, to influence these factors. We'll also discuss how these decisions impact financial markets and the broader economy. By the end of this lesson, you'll have a comprehensive understanding of monetary policy and its significance in shaping economic conditions and financial markets.

Overall Expectations

D3 Monetary Policy: analyze various aspects of monetary policy in Canada and their impact on the economy. 

Specific Expectations

D3.1 analyze the role of the Bank of Canada, with a particular emphasis on monetary policy (e.g., with reference to the money supply, interest rates, reserve ratio, exchange rates, and its role in responding to economic challenges such as recession or inflation).

D3.2 analyze the form and function of money in Canada’s economy and how monetary policy affects the Canadian economy.

D3.3 analyze the role of financial institutions in Canada.
