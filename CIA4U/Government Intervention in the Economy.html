Government Intervention in the Economy

Sometimes, governments decide to pass legislation to impact the market. The two most common methods are a price floor and a price ceiling. A price floor is a government- or group-imposed price control or limit on how low a price can be charged for a product. A price floor must be higher than the equilibrium price to be effective. One example is the minimum wage.

A price ceiling is a government-imposed price control or limit on how much a product is charged. Governments intend price ceilings to protect consumers from conditions that could make commodities prohibitively expensive.

One common effect of price ceilings is black markets. Because of the shortage, some people will pay “under the table” to get the product. Examples are rent.
