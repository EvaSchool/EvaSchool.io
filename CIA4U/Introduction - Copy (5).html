Economic Trade-Offs and Decisions

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we'll delve into "Economic Trade-Offs and Decisions." We'll explore how scarcity drives decision-making, leading to trade-offs and opportunity costs. We'll investigate how consumers and producers make choices, using economic models and tools. We'll also touch on global decisions and trade's role in the international arena, helping us make more informed economic choices in our daily lives.

Overall Expectations

C2 Economic Trade-offs and Decisions: Analyse economic trade-offs from the perspective of different stakeholders, including those in different countries, and how trade-offs influence economic decisions.

Specific Expectations

C2.1 demonstrate an understanding of economic trade-offs (e.g., whether to buy a car or a transit pass, to rent an apartment or buy a house, to send a child from an impoverished family to school or to work, to pay down government debt or increase government spending on infrastructure or social services) and explain their significance for individuals and society.

C2.2 analyse how different stakeholders view the trade-off between economic growth and concerns for the environment.

C2.3 evaluate the impact of some key socioeconomic trends on economic policy in different countries.

C2.4 assess some responses, or possible responses, to current economic issues, using cost/benefit analysis.
