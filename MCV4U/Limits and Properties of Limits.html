Properties of Limits

  • The statement above says that the values of become closer and closer to L as x gets closer and closer to a (from either side of a), but x is never equal to a.
  • This means that when finding the limit of as x approaches a, there is no need to consider x= a.  In fact, as we have seen, f(a) may not even defined.

The only thing that matters is the behavior of f(x) near a.
