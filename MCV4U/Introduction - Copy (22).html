Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will acquire the ability to articulate the concept of vectors and provide real-world examples illustrating their application.

They will familiarize themselves with the algebraic representation of vectors and develop the proficiency to perform vector addition and subtraction.

The lesson will also involve a review of trigonometric ratios, emphasizing the Sine and Cosine Laws.

Furthermore, students will enhance their skills in solving unknowns related to acute and right triangles.

Overall Outcomes

C1. Demonstrate an understanding of vectors in two-space and three-space by representing them algebraically and geometrically and by recognizing their applications.

Specific 

Outcomes

C1.1 Recognize a vector as a quantity with both magnitude and direction, and identify, gather, and interpret information about real-world applications of vectors.


C1.2 Represent a vector in two-space geometrically as a directed line segment, with directions expressed in different ways, and algebraically, and recognize vectors with the same magnitude and direction but different positions as equal vectors.

Learning Goals

  • Students will be reviewing trigonometric ratios and the Sine and Cosine Laws.
  • Students can solve unknowns about acute and right triangles.
  • Students will be able to explain what vectors are and give examples of vectors in the real world.
  • Students will know how vectors are written algebraically and can use this information to add and subtract vectors.
