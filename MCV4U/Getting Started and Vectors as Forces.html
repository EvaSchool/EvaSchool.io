Vector versus Scalar Quantity

A Vector is a mathematical quantity having both magnitude and direction.

A Scalar is a mathematical quantity having only magnitude for example speed.

Vector Subtraction

When two opposite vectors are added, the resultant is the zero vector.
The zero vector has a magnitude of 0 and no definite direction.
