Introduction to The Slope of a Tangent and Rates of Change

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will embark on a comprehensive exploration of calculus fundamentals. They will first gain a solid understanding of the concept of derivatives by delving into the definition through the limit function. As the lesson progresses, students will acquire the skills to utilize tangents' slopes to determine derivatives, a fundamental technique in calculus. Additionally, students will learn to calculate the average velocity of an object, contributing to their real-world problem-solving abilities. Furthermore, they will delve into the important distinction between average and instantaneous rates of change. By the end of this lesson, students will have built a strong foundation in calculus concepts and practical applications.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate an understanding of the rate of change by making connections between the average rate of change over an interval and the instantaneous rate of change at a point, using the slopes of secants and tangents and the concept of the limit.

 

Specific Expectations

A1.1 describe examples of real-world applications of rates of change, represented in a variety of ways.

A1.2 describe connections between the average rate of change of a function that is smooth over an interval and the slope of the corresponding secant and between the instantaneous rate of change of a smooth function at a point and the slope of the tangent at that point.

A1.3 make connections, with or without graphing technology, between an approximate value of the instantaneous rate of change at a given point on the graph of a smooth function and average rates of change over intervals containing the point.

A1.5 make connections, for a function that is smooth over the interval a ≤ x ≤ a + h, between the average rate of change of the function over this interval and the value of the expression and between the instantaneous rate of change of the function at x = a and the value of the limit.

A1.6 compare, through investigation, the calculation of instantaneous rates of change at a point (a, f(a)) for polynomial functions [e.g., f(x) = xn, f(x) = xm ], with and without simplifying the expression before substituting values of h that approach zero.

Learning Goals

  • Students will know the definition of a derivative using the limit function. 
  • Students will know how to use the slopes of a tangent to find the derivative
  • Students know how to calculate the average velocity of an object.
  • Students can differentiate between average and instantaneous rates of change.
