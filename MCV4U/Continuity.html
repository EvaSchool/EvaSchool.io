Continuity

The idea of continuity may be thought of informally as the idea of being able to draw a graph without lifting one’s pencil.
The concept arose from the notion of a graph “without breaks or jumps or gaps.”
A graph that is not continuous at a point (discontinuous at a point) has a break of some type at the point.

The precise definition of continuity at a point is given on the right.

To test if a function f(x) is continuous at a point x=a, check the following:

  • Does f(a) exist?
  • Does the limit at a exist?
  • Does f(a) and the limit of f(x) at a equal each other?

If the answer is no to any of these, the function is discontinuous at x = a.
