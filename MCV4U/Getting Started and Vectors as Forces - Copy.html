What is a VECTOR?

A Vector is a mathematical quantity having both magnitude and direction.

A vector can be represented by a directed line segment, a directed line segment has a length, called its magnitude, and a direction indicated by an arrowhead.

Callout

What is a Force

On Earth, a force is defined as the product between the mass of an object and the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s2. This unit of measure is called a newton and is abbreviated as N.

In problems involving forces, it is often the case that two or more forces act on an object.

Composition of Forces

To better understand the effect of these forces, it is useful to be able to find a single force that would produce exactly the same effect as all the forces acting together and this single force is called the RESULTANT or SUM

It is important to determine the direction and magnitude of this single force. The process of finding the resultant of all the force acting on an object is called the COMPOSITION OF FORCES

Callout

Equilibrant of Several Forces

The equilibrant of a number of forces is the single force that opposes the resultant of the forces acting on an object. When an equilibrant is applied to the object, this force maintains the objects in a state of equilibrium.

Two children, James and Fred, are pushing on a rock. James pushes with a vector force of 80N in an easterly direction, and Fred pushes with a vector force of 60N in the same direction. Determine the resultant and equilibrant of these two forces
