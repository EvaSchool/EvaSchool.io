Relationships Between Points, Lines, and Planes

Intersections of Lines and Planes  The Intersection of Two Planes The Intersection of Three Planes  The Distance from a Point to a Line in R2 and R3 The Distance from a Point to a Plane

Lesson Description

Students are capable of determining the intersection behavior of lines with other lines, as well as lines with planes, both in R2 and R3.

Overall Outcomes

C3. Distinguish between the geometric representation of a single linear equation or a system of two linear equations in two-space and three-space and determine different geometric configurations of lines and planes in three-space.

Specific Outcomes

C3.1 Recognize that the solution points (x, y) in two-space of a single linear equation in two variables form a line and that the solution points (x, y) in two-space of a system of two linear equations in two variables determine the point of intersection of two lines, if the lines are not coincident or parallel.


C3.2 Determine, through investigation with technology and without technology, that the solution points (x, y, z) in three-space of a single linear equation in three variables form a plane and that the solution points (x, y, z) in three-space of a system of two linear equations in three variables form the line of intersection of two planes, if the planes are not coincident or parallel.

Learning Goals

  • Students can find the intersection behavior of lines with lines, and lines with planes, in R2 and R3.

Lesson Description

Students possess the ability to analyze and articulate the intersection of two planes.

Overall Outcomes

C3.2 Determine, through the investigation with technology and without technology, that the solution points (x, y, z) in three-space of a single linear equation in three variables form a plane and that the solution points (x, y, z) in three-space of a system of two linear equations in three variables form the line of intersection of two planes, if the planes are not coincident or parallel.

Specific Outcomes

C3.3 determine, through investigation using a variety of tools and strategies, different geometric configurations of combinations of up to three lines and/or planes in three-space; organize the configurations based on whether they intersect and, if so, how they intersect.


C4.7 solves problems relating to lines and planes in three-space that are represented in a variety of ways and involving distances or intersections and interprets the result geometrically.
