Radical Expressions - Rationalizing Denominators

A rational number is a number that can be expressed as a fraction (quotient) containing integers.

The process of changing a denominator from a radical (square root) to a rational number (integer) is called rationalizing the denominator. 

For example:

The conjugate of (2 + 3) is (2 - 3).

If your expression only has one term, the expression is the same as its conjugate.  

For example the conjugate of 4 is 4.   

There are two situations we will need to consider:

  • The denominator has one term in it.
  • The denominator has two terms in it.

We handle both situations in the same way:  We use conjugates.

A conjugate is formed by changing the sign between two terms in an expression.

Why do we need to rationalize the denominator? 

The reason that we rationalize denominators is that dividing by an integer is preferable to dividing by a radical number.
