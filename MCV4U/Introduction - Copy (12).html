The Derivative of Composite Functions         

 

Lesson Description

In this engaging lesson, students will explore the world of calculus, focusing on the important concept of finding derivatives for composite functions. They will learn to use the Chain Rule, which helps them understand how functions within functions work together.

As students progress, they will have the chance to put their knowledge into practice. They will apply all the rules they've learned to find derivatives for various functions, boosting their problem-solving skills and confidence.

By the end of the lesson, students will be well-prepared to handle calculus with ease, having mastered the art of finding derivatives and understanding composite functions.

Overall Expectations

A3. Verify graphically and algebraically the rules for determining derivatives; apply these rules to determine the derivatives of polynomial, sinusoidal, exponential, rational, and radical functions, and simple combinations of functions; and solve related problems.

 

Specific Expectations

A3.4 Verify that the power rule applies to functions of the form f(x) = xn, where n is a rational number, and verify algebraically the chain rule using monomial functions and the product rule using polynomial functions.

A3.5 Solve problems, using the product and chain rules, involving the derivatives of polynomial functions, sinusoidal functions, exponential functions, rational functions, radical functions, and other simple combinations of functions.

Learning Goals

  • Students will know how find derivatives of composite functions by applying the chain rule.
  • Students will know how to apply all the rules learned to find the derivatives of functions.
