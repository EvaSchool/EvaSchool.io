Optimization in Economics

Optimization techniques are used throughout industry, since the goal is to maximize profits. 

A company’s profit is the amount left over after the costs of production have been discounted from the company’s total income (called revenue). 

In other words, profit is the difference between revenues and costs, If the profit is represented by p(x), revenues by r(x) and costs by c(x), then    p(x) = r(x) - c(x)

The cost of manufacturing fishing poles can be modelled by the function c(x) = 7x + 3, where x is the number of batches (each containing 1000 poles) manufactured. Revenue is modelled by r(x) = x3 - 10x2 + 20x. A small company has enough workers to produce a maximum of 1200 fishing poles. Find the number of fishing poles that the company should manufacture to maximize profits.
