Continuity                 

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will sharpen their ability to recognize and differentiate between the continuity and discontinuity of functions over various intervals. They will explore the characteristics and key indicators that signify when a function maintains a smooth and unbroken path, and when it experiences interruptions or breaks. By the end of this lesson, students will have a clear grasp of the distinction between continuous and discontinuous behavior in functions, equipping them with valuable insights for their mathematical journey. 

Overall Outcomes

A1. Demonstrate an understanding of the rate of change by making connections between the average rate of change over an interval and the instantaneous rate of change at a point, using the slopes of secants and tangents and the concept of the limit.

 

Specific Outcomes

A1.4 recognize, through investigation with or without technology, graphical and numerical examples of limits, and explain the reasoning involved.

Learning Goals

Students can distinguish between a function being continuous or discontinuous over intervals.
