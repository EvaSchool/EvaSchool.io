Introduction to Radical Expressions - Rationalizing Denominators

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will be introduced to The Slope of a Tangent. Students will grasp the definition of a derivative through the lens of the limit function, gaining a deep understanding of this crucial concept. Furthermore, students will learn how to leverage slopes of tangents to uncover derivatives, a vital skill in calculus. Beyond that, they will explore the calculation of an object's average velocity and develop the ability to distinguish between average and instantaneous rates of change. This lesson equips students with essential knowledge and skills for their journey through calculus.

Overall Expectations

A3. Verify graphically and algebraically the rules for determining derivatives; apply these rules to determine the derivatives of polynomial, sinusoidal, exponential, rational, and radical functions, and simple combinations of functions; and solve related problems.

 

Specific Expectations

A3.4 verify that the power rule applies to functions of the form f(x) = xn, where n is a rational number, and verify algebraically the chain rule using monomial functions and the product rule using polynomial functions.

Learning Goals

  • Students will know how to rationalize denominators.
  • Students will learn that writing fractions with whole number denominators is a lot easier to approximate, and this skill will help them later in the unit.
  • Students will be able to explain the value of knowing how to rationalize denominators.
