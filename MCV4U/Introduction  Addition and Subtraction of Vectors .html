Introduction: Addition and Subtraction of Vectors 

In this lesson, students will develop a solid understanding of vectors and their practical applications. They will learn to define vectors and provide real-world examples, recognizing the significance of these mathematical entities in our everyday lives. Additionally, students will become proficient in expressing vectors algebraically, empowering them to effectively add and subtract vectors, a crucial skill in various scientific and engineering fields. As part of their comprehensive review, students will revisit trigonometric ratios and delve into the Sine and Cosine Laws, providing them with essential tools to solve problems involving acute and right triangles. By the end of this lesson, students will be well-equipped to tackle unknowns related to these fundamental geometric shapes with confidence and precision.

Overall Outcomes:

C1. Demonstrate an understanding of vectors in two-space and three-space by representing them algebraically and geometrically and by recognizing their applications.

Specific Outcomes:

C1.1 recognizes a vector as a quantity with both magnitude and direction, and identifies, gathers, and interprets information about real-world applications of vectors.

C1.2 represents a vector in two-space geometrically as a directed line segment, with directions expressed in different ways, and algebraically, and recognizes vectors with the same magnitude and direction but different positions as equal vectors.
