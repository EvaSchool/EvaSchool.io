Introduction to Limits and Properties of Limits

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will delve into the concept of limits as x approaches a specific value, denoted as "a." They will become acquainted with the properties that govern limits, gaining a comprehensive understanding of their characteristics and significance. Furthermore, students will develop the ability to apply these properties to calculate the limits of polynomial functions, a valuable skill in calculus. Additionally, students will explore various techniques for calculating limits, recognizing that a graph or a table of values can be essential tools for verifying their results. This lesson equips students with the knowledge and tools to handle limits effectively in their mathematical journey.

Overall Outcomes

A1. Demonstrate an understanding of the rate of change by making connections between the average rate of change over an interval and the instantaneous rate of change at a point, using the slopes of secants and tangents and the concept of the limit.

 

Specific Outcomes

A1.4 recognize, through investigation with or without technology, graphical and numerical examples of limits and explain the reasoning involved.

A1.5 make connections, for a function that is smooth over the interval a ≤ x ≤ a + h, between the average rate of change of the function over this interval and the value of the expression and between the instantaneous rate of change of the function at x = a and the value of the limit.

Learning Goals

  • Students are familiar with the idea of the limit of a function as x approaches a certain value (“a”).
  • Students are familiar with the properties of limits.
  • Students can use the properties of limits to calculate the limits of polynomials.
  • Students know that there are a number of techniques for calculating limits, but in any case a graph or table of values can help to check results.
