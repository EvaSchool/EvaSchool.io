Welcome to Functions – Grade 11 (MCR3U)

This course introduces students to the mathematical concept of the function by extending their experiences with linear and quadratic relations. Students will investigate properties of discrete and continuous functions, represent functions numerically, algebraically, and graphically, and solve problems involving applications of functions.

Students will also develop an understanding of rates of change, explore transformations of functions, and build the algebraic skills necessary for future success in courses such as Advanced Functions (MHF4U) and Calculus and Vectors (MCV4U).

Unit Title Description
Chapter 1: Introduction to Functions Students will review prior knowledge and explore the definition of a function, including function notation, domain and range, and different ways to represent functions (graphical, numerical, algebraic, and verbal).
Chapter 2: Algebraic Expressions This chapter focuses on algebraic manipulation and simplification, including factoring, expanding, and solving equations. These foundational skills are necessary for success in later units.
Chapter 3: Quadratic Functions Students will investigate the properties of quadratic functions, explore transformations of quadratic graphs, and solve quadratic equations using a variety of methods including factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.
Chapter 4: Exponential Functions This unit introduces exponential relationships and their applications. Students will explore exponential growth and decay and compare exponential functions with linear and quadratic models.
Chapter 5: Trigonometric Ratios Students will study right triangle trigonometry, including primary trigonometric ratios, solving triangles, and applications in real-world contexts.
Chapter 6: Sinusoidal Functions Building on their trigonometric understanding, students will graph and interpret sinusoidal functions and analyze their key characteristics such as amplitude, period, phase shift, and vertical displacement.
Chapter 7: Sequences and Series This unit introduces students to arithmetic and geometric sequences and series. They will model and solve problems involving recursive and explicit formulas.
Chapter 8: Financial Applications Students will apply their knowledge of exponential functions and series to real-life financial contexts such as compound interest, annuities, and loans.
