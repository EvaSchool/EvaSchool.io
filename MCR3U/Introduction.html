Lesson Description

Identify the features of a relation that make it a function.

Describe relations that are not functions.

Overall Expectations

A1. demonstrate an understanding of functions, their representations, and their inverses, and make connections between the algebraic and graphical representations of functions using transformations.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 explain the meaning of the term function, and distinguish a function from a relation that is not a function, through investigation of linear and quadratic relations using a variety of representations and strategies.

A1.3 explain the meanings of the terms domain and range, through investigation using numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of the functions f(x) = x, f(x) = x2, f(x) = √x, and f(x) = 1/x; describe the domain and range of a function appropriately; and explain any restrictions on the domain and range in contexts arising from real-world applications.

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will:

Identify and use correct function notation.

Represent linear and quadratic functions with proper function notation.

Explore the most common parent functions.

Compare parent functions using the main characteristics of their graphs.

Use visuals including graphs to determine the domain and range of functions.

Use tables of values and equations to algebraically determine the domain and range of functions.
