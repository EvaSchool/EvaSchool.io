a) Determine a formula that defines the arithmetic sequence 3, 12, 21, 30, ….

 Solution: 

For the arithmetic sequence 3, 12, 21, 30, ..., the formula for the (n)th term, (a_n), can be expressed without using multiplication and division symbols:

1. The first term (a_1) of the sequence is 3.
2. The common difference d between consecutive terms is 9, determined by subtracting any term from the next one in the sequence (for example, 12 - 3).

The formula for the n th term of an arithmetic sequence is given by:
[ a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d ]

Substituting the values (a_1 = 3) and (d = 9):
[ a_n = 3 + 9n - 9 ]
Simplifying this, we get:
[ a_n = 9n - 6 ]

So, the formula defining this arithmetic sequence is,
[ a_n = 9n - 6 ]

b) State a formula that defines each term of any arithmetic sequence.

 Solution:

The formula that defines each term of any arithmetic sequence can be stated as follows:

For an arithmetic sequence, the n th term, denoted as a_n, is given by the formula:
[ a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d ]

Here:
- (a_1) represents the first term of the sequence.
- (d) is the common difference between consecutive terms.
- (n) is the term number.

This formula calculates the value of any term in the sequence based on its position n. The term a_n is obtained by adding the product of the common difference d and one less than the term's position n - 1 to the first term a_1.

c. Find the 20th term of the arithmetic sequence where the first term is 7 and the common difference is 5.

 Solution:

To find the 20th term of the arithmetic sequence, we use the formula for the \(n\)th term of an arithmetic sequence:

 a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d 

Where:
-a_n is the nth term.
- a_1 is the first term.
- d is the common difference.
- n is the term number.

Given:
- a_1 = 7 (first term)
- d = 5 (common difference)
- n = 20 (we need to find the 20th term)

Substitute these values into the formula:
[ a_{20} = 7 + (20 - 1)5 ]
[ a_{20} = 7 + 19*5]
[ a_{20} = 7 + 95 ]
[ a_{20} = 102 ]

Therefore, the 20th term of the arithmetic sequence is 102.
