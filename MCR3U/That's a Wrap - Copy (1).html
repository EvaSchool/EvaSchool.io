Learning Goals

Students will be able to:

  • Identify the reciprocal trigonometric identities.
  • Use the primary and reciprocal trigonometric identities to solve problems.
  • Explore trigonometric ratios of any angle.
  • Find the quadrants where trigonometric ratios
  • Explore trigonometric identities.
  • Simplify trigonometric expressions to prove trigonometric identities.
  • Review the Cosine Law.
  • Solve real-world problems using the Cosine Law.
  • Solve three-dimensional problems using the Sine and Cosine laws.
  • Solve real-world problems involving angles and triangles.

Success Criteria

Students will be successful if they can:

  1. List the reciprocal trigonometric identities and identify them.
  2. Solve problems using the primary and reciprocal trigonometric identities.
  3. Identify the 4 quadrants
  4. Use the Cartesian plane to evaluate the primary trigonometric ratios for angles between 0° and 360°
  5. Prove simple trigonometric identities
  6. Solve three-dimensional problems using the Sine and Cosine laws and solve real-world problems involving angles and triangles.
