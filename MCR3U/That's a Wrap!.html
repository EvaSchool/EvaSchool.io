Learning Goals

Students can explain what a function is and know the difference between a function and a relation.

Students can use function notation to represent linear and quadratic functions given their graphs, table of values, or equations.

Students can explain domain and range, and can identify it for the basic functions given their graphs, tables of values, or equations.

 Students can find the inverse of linear and quadratic functions.

Students can graph the inverse of linear and quadratic functions and make connections between the graph of the original function and its inverse.

 Students know that the domain and range of a function are the range and domain of the inverse of that function.

 Students can describe the roles of a, k, c, and d in transforming the basic functions and can write correct equations when given transformations in words.

 Students can describe the roles of a, k, c, and d in transforming the basic functions and can write correct equations when given transformations in words.

Success Criteria

You will know that you are successful if you can: 

  • Describe what a relation is.
  • Describe what a function is.
  • Recognize different forms of functions and relations.
  • Describe what a “parent function” is.
  • Can recognize a variety of different functions from its graph and its equation.
  • Use tables, graphs, and equations to find domains and ranges of functions
  • Explain how various transformations affect the graph of the parent function.
  • Perform transformations on parent functions.
