Q 1. Expand and simplify (x - 2)5

1. Understanding the Exponent: The expression ((x - 2)^5) means ((x - 2)) multiplied by itself five times. 

2. Applying the Binomial Theorem: The binomial theorem is used for expansion. It states that ((a + b)^n) is equal to the sum of binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k, where binom{n}{k} are binomial coefficients.

3 Expanding (x - 2)^5: 
    - For (n = 5), (a = x), and (b = -2), the expansion is:
    - ((x - 2)^5 = binom{5}{0} x^5(-2)^0 + binom{5}{1} x^4(-2)^1 + binom{5}{2} x^3(-2)^2 + binom{5}{3} x^2(-2)^3 + binom{5}{4} x^1(-2)^4 + binom{5}{5} x^0(-2)^5

4. Calculating the Binomial Coefficients:
    -(binom{5}{0}) is 1, (binom{5}{1}) is 5, (binom{5}{2}) is 10, (binom{5}{3}) is 10, (binom{5}{4}) is 5, (binom{5}{5}) is 1.

5. Putting It All Together:
    - ((x - 2)^5) becomes (1 x^5 1 - 5 x^4 2 + 10 x^3 4 - 10 x^2 8 + 5 x 16 - 1 32)
    - Simplifying this, we get (x^5 - 10x^4 + 40x^3 - 80x^2 + 80x - 32).

Thus, the expanded form of ((x - 2) ^5) is (x^5 - 10x^4 + 40x^3 - 80x^2 + 80x - 32).
