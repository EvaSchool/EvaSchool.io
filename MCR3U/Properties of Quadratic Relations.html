Properties of Quadratic Functions 

Key Ideas

Graphs of quadratic functions with no domain restrictions are parabolas.

Quadratic functions have constant nonzero second differences. If the second difference is positive, the parabola opens up and the coefficient is positive. If the second difference is negative, the parabola opens down and the coefficient is negative.

Callout

Need to Know

Quadratic functions can be represented by equations in function notation, by tables of values, or by graphs.

Quadratic functions have a degree of 2.
