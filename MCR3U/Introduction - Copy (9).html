The Inverse of a Quadratic Function

Lesson Description

I can explain what the inverse of a function is.
I can graph the inverse of quadratic functions.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate an understanding of functions, their representations, and their inverses, and make connections between the algebraic and graphical representations of functions using transformations.

Specific Expectations

A1.4 Relate the process of determining the inverse of a function to their understanding of reverse processes (e.g., applying inverse operations).

A1.5 Determine the numeric or graphical representation of the inverse of a linear or quadratic function, given the numeric, graphical, or algebraic representation of the function, and make connections, through investigation using a variety of tools (e.g., graphing technology, Mira, tracing paper), between the graph of a function and the graph of its inverse (e.g., the graph of the inverse is the reflection of the graph of the function in the line y = x).

A1.6 determine, through investigation, the relationship between the domain and range of a function&nbsp;and the domain and range of the inverse relation, and determine whether or not the inverse relation is a function.

A1.7 Determine, using function notation when appropriate, the algebraic representation of the inverse of a linear or quadratic function, given the algebraic representation of the function [e.g., f(x) = (x – 2) – 5], and make connections, through investigation using a variety of tools (e.g., graphing technology, Mira, tracing paper), between the algebraic representations of a function and its inverse (e.g., the inverse of a linear function involves applying the inverse operations in the reverse order).

Learning Goals

  • Students will explore the concept of the inverse of a function.
  • Students will graph the inverse of quadratic functions.
