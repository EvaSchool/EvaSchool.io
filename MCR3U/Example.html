Example

Two students are riding their bikes. A pebble is stuck in the tire of each bike. The two graphs show the heights of the pebbles above the ground in terms of time. What information about the bikes can you gather from the graphs of these functions?

COMPARING PEAKS OF A SINUSOIDAL FUNCTION

For Bike A, the pebble was initially at its highest height of 60 cm. For Bike B, the pebble was initially at its lowest height of 0 cm.

The wheels have different diameters. What is the diameter of the wheel on Bike A? How about on Bike B?

The diameter of the wheel on Bike A is 60 cm. The diameter of the wheel on Bike B is 50 cm.

COMPARING PERIODS

The wheel on Bike A takes 0.6 s to complete one revolution. The wheel on Bike B takes 0.5 s to complete one revolution.

The period of Bike A is 0.6 s. The period of Bike B is 0.5 s.

COMPARING EQUATIONS OF AXES

The axis is halfway between a peak (or maximum) and a trough (or minimum). Add the maximum and the minimum and then divide it by 2.
The axle for the wheel on Bike A is 30 cm above the ground. The axle for the wheel on Bike B is 25 cm above the ground.

COMPARING SPEED

Speed is equal to distance divided by time, so we had to figure out how far each bike travels when the wheel completes one revolution. This distance is the circumference. Calculate the two circumferences.

What are the circumferences of Bike A and Bike B?

To calculate the speed, divide each circumference by the time taken to complete one revolution.

What can you tell about the speed of the two bikes?
