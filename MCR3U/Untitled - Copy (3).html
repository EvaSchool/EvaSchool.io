Key Ideas

  • Graphs of quadratic functions with no domain restrictions are parabolas.
  • Quadratic functions have constant nonzero second differences. If the second differences are positive, the parabola opens up and the coefficient of is positive. If the second differences are negative, the parabola opens down and the coefficient of is negative.

Need to Know

  • Quadratic functions can be represented by equations in function notation, by tables of values, or by graphs.
  • Quadratic functions have a degree of 2.
