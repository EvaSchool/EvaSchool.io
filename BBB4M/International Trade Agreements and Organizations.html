International Trade Agreements and Organizations  

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will engage in discussions about the advantages and disadvantages of globalization. They will also explore the trade agreements Canada has engaged in and examine the motivations behind Canada's active pursuit of such agreements. Furthermore, students will research the impact and influence of different trade organizations on international trade. Lastly, they will discuss how the United Nations can provide support to international business.

Overall Expectations

B3. Assess the effects of current trends in global business activity and economic conditions.

Specific Expectations

B3.1 Explain, drawing on information from a variety of sources, including the Internet, how Canadian and international companies, industries, and markets are being affected by increased global business activity.


B3.2 identify and analyze international business trends and their influences on companies, industries, and career opportunities in the global economy.


B3.3 Assess, considering factors such as markets, financing, and labor, how trends in the global marketplace have changed how an individual might run a business.


B3.4 Explain how globalization creates the need for standardization of products, services, and processes.

Learning Goals

  • Students will explore the positive and negative effects of globalization.
  • Students will explore the trade agreements Canada has negotiated and the reasons Canada actively pursues trade agreements.
  • Students will research how various trade organizations impact and influence international trade.
  • Students will learn how the United Nations can assist international business.
