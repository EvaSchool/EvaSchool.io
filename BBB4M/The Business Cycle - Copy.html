The Business Cycle

The business cycle refers to the fluctuation in economic activity of a country or the world over some time. It is characterized by repetitive patterns of expansion and contraction, and it can be observed across various economic indicators such as GDP, employment rates, inflation, and consumer confidence. 

It has four main stages:

  • Recession
  • Trough
  • Expansion
  • Peak

Expansion

During the expansion phase, the economy experiences relatively rapid growth, interest rates tend to be low, production increases, and inflationary pressures build.

Peak

The peak of a cycle is reached when growth hits its maximum rate. Peak growth typically creates some imbalances in the economy that need to be corrected. 

Recession

This correction from the peak occurs through a period of contraction (recession) when growth slows, employment falls, and prices stagnate.

Trough

The trough of the cycle is reached when the economy hits a low point and growth begins to recover.

Leading Indicators

Leading indicators are economic measurements or trends that are used to forecast or predict the future direction of an economy. They are considered early signals or signals that occur before changes in the overall economy take place. Investors, policymakers, and businesses closely monitor leading indicators to make informed decisions about future economic conditions. These indicators are crucial for planning and developing strategies to mitigate potential risks or take advantage of emerging opportunities.

Lagging Indicators

Lagging indicators refer to economic factors or variables that do not respond immediately to changes in the overall economy but instead adjust or reflect them after a certain period. These indicators are considered to follow or lag behind the performance of the economy, and their changes are observed and analyzed retrospectively.

Coincident Indicators

Coincident indicators are economic factors or variables that move in conjunction with the business cycle. Unlike leading indicators, which predict future economic trends, coincident indicators provide a real-time snapshot of the current state of the economy. They provide immediate information about the business conditions at a particular moment and often move in sync with the overall economic performance.

Monetary Policy

Monetary policy refers to the set of decisions and actions taken by a country's central bank or monetary authority to control and regulate the money supply and credit conditions in the economy. The primary goal of monetary policy is to maintain price stability, promote economic growth, and ensure the stability of the financial system.

Fiscal Policy

Fiscal policy refers to the decisions and actions undertaken by a government regarding its revenue collection (taxation) and expenditure. It involves the way the government collects and spends money at all levels, including municipal, provincial, and federal governments.

What is trade?

Trade is a basic economic concept involving the buying and selling of goods and services, with compensation paid by a buyer to a seller, or the exchange of goods or services between parties. Trade can take place within an economy between producers and consumers.
