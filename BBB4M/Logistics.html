Logistics                    

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will delve into the fundamental concepts of customers and competition, exploring their interplay and impact on marketing decision-making processes. They will learn how to identify and analyze target customers' needs, preferences, and behaviors, and how to develop effective marketing campaigns tailored to meet those needs. Additionally, students will gain insights into competitive analysis, understanding the competitive landscape, assessing competitors' strengths and weaknesses, and leveraging that information to gain a competitive advantage.

Overall Expectations

D3. Demonstrate an understanding of the logistics of, and challenges associated with, distribution to local, national, and international markets.

Specific Expectations

D3.1 Compare the logistics of delivering a product to a local, a national, and an international market.


D3.2 Describe the key factors that influence how a company may deliver its product to an international market.


D3.3 Compare the advantages and disadvantages of different modes of transportation for distributing a product to different world markets.


D3.4 Identify, drawing on a variety of sources, including the Internet, information to facilitate the import/export process.

Learning Goals

  • Students will compare the logistics of delivering a product to a local, a national, and an international market.
  • Students will describe the key factors that influence how a company may deliver its product to an international market.
