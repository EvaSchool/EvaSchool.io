Elements of International Trade

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will explore the foundational elements that shape and facilitate international trade. International trade refers to the exchange of goods, services, and resources between countries. Students will start by examining the concept of comparative advantage and how it drives specialization and trade between nations. Next, students will dive into an exploration of trade barriers, including tariffs, quotas, and non-tariff barriers, and their impact on international trade. 

Overall Expectations

A3. Demonstrate an understanding of how international business and economic activities increase the interdependence of nations.

Specific Expectations

A3.1 Identify the types of international businesses

A3.2 Describe ways in which international business activity develops interdependence among nations.

Learning Goals

  • Students will know the different types of international business.
  • Students will understand and apply the different ways companies can do business internationally.
  • Students will describe the effects of barriers and obstacles to international business activity.
  • Students will explain how changes in the value of the Canadian dollar can affect business opportunities.
