Advantages and Disadvantages of International Trade

International Trade

International trade is a key driver of economic growth and development, facilitating the movement of resources and fostering global interconnectedness. It involves the buying and selling of goods, services, and capital across different countries or territories. The need or want for goods or services in one country often leads to the importation of those products from another country that can provide them more efficiently or at a lower cost.

1. Meeting Our Needs

  • Trade is always balanced if it is "FAIR".
  • Businesses can create a surplus inventory of goods and services.
  • Canadian farms produce more food than Canadians can eat, Canadian manufacturers make more products than Canadians use, and Canadian service providers can provide service to other countries.
  • Canadians cannot produce fruits like bananas and oranges, and some products we cannot make.
  • These products are imported. Both trading partners get something they need by trading something they don’t need.

2. Job Creation

  • Trade creates employment opportunities directly as well as indirectly.
  • International trade results in an increase in production and due to increased production the demand for labor increases which creates employment opportunities.
  • It helps in generating more employment through the establishment of newer industries to cater to the demands of various countries.
  • Export-oriented industries provide employment opportunities such as forwarding agents and clearing agents.
  • This helps countries to bring down their unemployment rates.

3. Diverse Products and Services

  • New services such as banking, travel, and consultation are also available now.
  • Competition is no longer on a city scale; businesses compete worldwide resulting in better quality goods, lower prices, and functional design.

4. New Technology and Materials

  • New technology promotes competitiveness and profitability. Businesses could create a machine that works better, faster, or cheaper (or all three), and then the business will have produced a more competitive product. The biotechnology industry in Canada is second only to the U.S.

1. Support of Non-Democratic System

  • People make poor decisions about land use or surplus production for export and do not consider the general population’s welfare.
    • Example :
      • Landowners in Nicaragua & Salvador want farmers to grow coffee beans because it is a very profitable cash crop, however, the farmers would like to use the land to grow more food for their families.
      • The farmer’s wishes are ignored because they do not own the land.

2. Cultural Identity Issues

  • Culture is a major export in the world.
  • The "culture consumer" in other countries is sometimes overwhelmed by North American ideas. “Ethnocentrism"
    • Example :
      • Coca-Cola, McDonald's, Nike, and Microsoft all sell products that symbolize American values and symbolize and reflect American corporate culture

3. Social Welfare Issues

  • Safety standards, minimum wages, worker’s compensation, and Health benefits are all social welfare cost business money.
  • The downside to this is that substandard safety conditions cause death and injury in the workplace.

4. Environmental Issues

  • In Canada, businesses are urged by the government and environmental groups through laws and regulations to keep our air, land, and water clean.
  • This is a costly process so businesses decide to move their operations to countries; i.e. Mexico, where it is less regulated.

5. Political Issues

  • Precious commodities such as gold, diamond, oil, or farmland are so important for countries to have control that wars have been started and as a result, people are killed.
  • Trade of these items has caused political alliances that do not help the people in the trading nation but only the powerful corporations that control the commodity.
