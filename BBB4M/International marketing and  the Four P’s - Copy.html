International Marketing and the Four P’s

Market research is a crucial aspect of the marketing process, as it involves gathering and analyzing data to gain insights into consumer behavior, market trends, and competitive landscapes. This data is then used to make informed decisions and solve marketing problems. Two types of data commonly used in market research are secondary data and primary data.

Secondary data refers to information that has been previously gathered by someone other than the user or the organization conducting the research. This data can be sourced from various external sources such as government agencies, research organizations, industry reports, and publications. Examples of secondary data include census data, survey data conducted by other organizations, market research reports, and historical sales data. Secondary data can provide a wealth of information and serve as a starting point for research efforts, as it often provides a broad overview of the market.

On the other hand, primary data refers to original data that is collected specifically to address a business's particular needs or problems. This data is observed or collected firsthand by the organization conducting the research. The methods used to gather primary data can vary and include techniques such as surveys, interviews, focus groups, and observational studies. Primary data is considered more specific and tailored to the organization's needs, allowing for a deeper understanding of consumer preferences, motivations, and behaviors.

Advertising and Promotion

Advertising and promotion are needed to convince the customer to buy the product. Businesses must identify the best way to reach their target market, taking into consideration the cost of different methods. For example, an Internet ad or brochure is much less expensive than a magazine ad or television commercial. 

Pricing

The price of a product must take into account the amount of labor, the cost of materials, and overhead (such as electricity). Items that are sold in stores are marked up, as the retailer needs to make money on the items it sells.

Sales

Businesses must determine the best way to sell their product. Sales methods or venues may include:
•    Selling products to retailers
•    Opening their retail store
•    Selling online

Logistics

Logistics consists of the flow of goods and services both into and out of an organization. It consists of transportation, inventory management, warehousing and storage, and packaging.
