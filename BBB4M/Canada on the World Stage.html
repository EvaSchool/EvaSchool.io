Canada on the World Stage

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will be able to discuss the characteristics of a multinational enterprise participating in global business with those of a Canadian company focused on domestic business activity.  And also how the workplace, occupations, the nature of work, and working conditions in Canada have changed as a result of the growth of a global economy.

Overall Expectations

B1. Demonstrate an understanding of the factors that influence a country’s ability to participate in international business.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Compare the characteristics of a multinational enterprise participating in global business with those of a Canadian company focused on domestic business activity.

B1.2 Analyse ways in which the workplace, occupations, the nature of work, and working conditions in Canada have changed as a result of the growth of a global economy.

Learning Goals

  • Students will compare the characteristics of a multinational enterprise participating in global business with those of a Canadian company focused on domestic business activity.
  • Students will analyze ways in which the workplace, occupations, the nature of work, and working conditions in Canada have changed as a result of the growth of a global economy.
  • Students will explore how global productivity is measured and can be improved and how workplaces are changing.

Success Criteria

  • Determine the diseases and disorders of the circulatory system.
  • Explain the mechanism of the circulatory system.
