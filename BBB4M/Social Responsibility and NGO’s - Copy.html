Social Responsibility and NGO’s

Corporate Social Responsibility

The duty of a company’s management to work in the best interests of the society it relies on for its resources (human, material, and environmental), to advance the welfare of society, and to act as a good global citizen through its policies.

Corporate social responsibility can take many forms, including:

  • Making charitable donations
  • Treating employees ethically
  • Being environmentally conscious
  • Ensuring safe working environments
  • Sponsoring local sports teams
  • Creating and promoting diverse workplaces

Benefits of CSR

  • Can be used as a marketing tool.
  • Dissuades governments from implementing regulations that could interfere with business.
  • Helps companies attract and retain excellent employees.

Criticisms of CSR

  • Costs money, and detracts from profits.
  • Uses employees’ time and energy.
  • Can distract customers from problems a company creates.
  • A company may act ethically in one country, but not in another.

Stakeholder Analysis

  • Used to determine which group’s interests are most important when a company is faced with an ethical dilemma.
  • Primary stakeholders directly affect the company and its profitability.
  • Secondary stakeholders have an impact on the company but do not directly influence its success or contribute to profitability.

Business Ethics

  • It is a set of rules or guidelines that management or individual follows to make their decisions for the company.
  • It includes:
    • domestic and international laws,
    • Company code of ethics corporate governance, and
    • personal values of the individual making the decision.

 Global Ethical Reasoning

  1. Ethical imperialism
    a.     It suggests that certain universal truths or values are standard across cultures.
    b. When something is considered wrong in one country, it is
    c. also wrong in all other countries.
    It includes the following:
    • Domestic and international laws,
    • company’s code of ethics, and
    • Personal values of the individual making the decision
  2. Cultural relativism
    a.     It suggests that the values of different cultures should be respected, as the ethics of one culture are not better than those of another
    b. For companies following the idea of cultural relativism, decisions are made based on the culture of the countries in which they are doing business

Environmental Issues

  • Sustainable development is defined as the ability to meet human consumption while maintaining the environment.
  • It is a critical issue that all businesses need to consider to ensure their futures.
  • Pollution- It is the presence or introduction of any substance or object that may harm the environment.
  • The top priorities that should be addressed are pollution and resource depletion.
  • Resource Depletion- It is the consumption of resources faster than it can be replenished.

Sweatshops

Factories in underdeveloped and developing countries where employees work in unsafe environments, are treated unfairly and have no chance to address these conditions.

  • Often employ children, as young as 14 or 15, who work alongside adults
  • Pay poverty wages that provide only one-quarter to one-half of a living wage in the community.
  • Unrealistic production quotas, harassment, and overtime without compensation.

Solution: 

  • Companies that can outsource their production need to implement strict policies to improve working conditions and protect workers’ rights.
