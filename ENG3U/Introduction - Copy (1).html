Irony

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn about the three types of Irony (verbal, situational, and dramatic).

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.

B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

Specific Expectations

B1.3 identify the most important ideas and supporting details in texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts.

B2.3 identify a variety of elements of style in texts and explain how they help communicate meaning and enhance the effectiveness of the texts.

Learning Goals

  • Identify the three types of irony in a short story.
  • Develop a deeper understanding of literary and poetic devices, as well as figurative language (metaphor, simile, personification, etc).
  • Explain interpretations of “The Necklace” using evidence from the text.
  • Effectively describe the oral and visual cues used in the text.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can identify the three different forms of irony in a short story
  • I can explain the examples of irony in the story.
  • I can develop a deeper understanding of literary devices, as well as figurative language (metaphor, simile, personification, etc)
  • I can explain interpretations of “The Necklace” using evidence from the text
  • I can effectively discuss the oral and visual cues used in the text
