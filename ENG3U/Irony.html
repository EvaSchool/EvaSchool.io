Irony

  • Irony is a literary device used to say the opposite of what it meant
  • There are three types of irony:
    -Verbal
    -Dramatic
    -Situational

Examples:

  • When a person is exhausted and says, “I’m having a terrific day!”
    -This is ironic because obviously it’s not a good day if you are extremely tired
  • If it were a cold, rainy gray day, you might say, “What a beautiful day!”
    -This is ironic because obviously the weather of the day is not “beautiful”

Example:

  • A fire station is burning and it can’t be saved by the firefighters.
    -It’s ironic because the firefighters who are usually the rescuers from fire, end up falling victim to the very thing they fight.
