Communication and Media

 

Communication
  • Is the act of conveying information with meaning from one person or group to another.
  • Usually follows a process.
  • Has five major types: verbal, non-verbal, visual, written, and listening.

Verbal Communication

Any message of information that is conveyed through speaking.

Non-Verbal Communication

In this form, the speakers use facial expressions, body language, and gestures to send their message of information.

Written Communication

When you write, text, or type something, you are communicating information through the written form.

Visual Communication

 

This type of communication refers to messages sent through photos, images, artworks, signs, and more.

Listening

Most people forget that listening is also a type of communication. It focuses on the receiver understanding the message in order to give feedback.

   

The Communication Process Components in the Communication Process
  • The process starts when the source sends a message through a channel to a receiver.
  • The communication process starts again when the receiver gives feedback to the source of the original message.

  • Source - initial sender of the message
  • Encoding - the act of sending a message of information
  • Channel - a type of method for presenting information
  • Decoding - the act of understanding the message/information
  • Receiver - the individual who is intended to get the message
  • Message - the information being sent
  • Feedback - the response sent back to the source

Channels

These are the means through which communication travels. More specifically, we call these media.

 

Media

There are 4 major types: print, broadcast, outdoor, and online.

 

 

 

Print Media

A form of publication where the materials are formed or made through printing that can be used for advertisement or spreading news, entertainment, and information.

 

Broadcast Media

A type of media that gives information to the public through television and radio and also used for advertisement and entertainment purposes.

Outdoor Media

  • A media that is focused on advertisement outdoors.
  • Examples: posters seen on buses or at bus stops, billboards, etc.

Online Media

  • This is media located on the internet.
  • Examples of these are social media apps such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, and Messenger
  • The purpose is still that these are channels used for exchanging messages and entertainment.
