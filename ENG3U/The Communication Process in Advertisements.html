The Communication Process in Advertisements

Recall: The Communication Process

The process starts when the source sends a message through a channel to a receiver. The communication process starts again when the receiver gives feedback to the source of the original message.

  

Open All Panels

The Source (or the Sender)

The source is where the communication starts.

Message

This is the information that the source intends to send.

Encoding

  • The source sends the information through encoding.
  • This also determines what means/channel the communication goes through.

Channel

  

This is the medium or media the message goes through

Decoding

 

  • The process where the receiver begins to interpret the message is called decoding.
  • The manner in which the receiver gets the message affects how he/she views the message.

The Receiver

The receiver is the intended person to get the message. Their background also determines how they will interpret the message.

Feedback

This is the response of the receiver. When the receiver responds to the message, the communication process becomes dynamic.

  

Interference with Communication Process

  • Also considered as “NOISE”
  • This is anything that protrudes the success of communication.
  • Examples: faulty signal, loud sounds, etc.
