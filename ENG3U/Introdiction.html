Independent Novel Analysis Guidelines 

Lesson Description

You will be walked through the course’s final rich summative task in this lesson. You will also be given an opportunity to manage your timetable in completing this task.

Overall Expectations

A2 Speaking to Communicate: Use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate with different audiences for a variety of purposes.

B1 Reading for Meaning: Read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.

D3 Creating Media Texts: Create a variety of media texts for different purposes and audiences, using appropriate forms, conventions, and techniques.

Specific Expectations

A2.1 Communicate orally for a range of purposes, using language appropriate for the intended audience.

A2.7 Use a variety of audio-visual aids effectively to support and enhance oral presentations and to engage an audience.

B1.3 Identify the most important ideas and supporting details in texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts. 

B1.4 Make and explain inferences of increasing subtlety about texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts, supporting their explanations with well-chosen stated and implied ideas from the texts.

D3.4 Produce media texts, including increasingly complex texts, for a variety of purposes and audiences, using appropriate forms, conventions, and techniques.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about the guidelines and conventions for independent novel analysis presentation.
  • Students will choose their novel for independent study
  • Students will plan their presentation.
  • Students will identify the PACTS in their chosen novel.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  

  • I will understand the guidelines and conventions required for my independent novel analysis presentation.
  • I will select my novel for independent study
  • I will begin planning my presentation.
  • I will identify PACTS in my chosen novel.
