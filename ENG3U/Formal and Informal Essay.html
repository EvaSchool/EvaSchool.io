Formal and Informal Essay

The Informal Essay

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Author’s viewpoint

  • Usually in first person
  • Directly addresses the reader

Subject/Content (sources of evidence)

  • Frequently drawn from life of the writer and everyday events.

Tone & Diction

  • Frequently more personal and subjective
  • May be ironic, amusing, thoughtful, angry or serious (etc.)
  • Vocabulary tends to be drawn from everyday usage

Structure

  • Appears to be more loosely structured

Location of Thesis

  • May appear anywhere in the essay
  • May also not be explicitly stated

The Formal Essay

 

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Author’s viewpoint

  • Usually uses third person
  • Does not address the reader

Subject/Content (sources of evidence)

  • More commonly drawn from shared historical events or literature or other forms of knowledge (ie. research)

Tone & Diction

  • Tends to be removed from the subject and appears to be objective
  • May be ironic, amusing, thoughtful, angry, or serious (etc.), but tends to hold emotions in check and express concerns through strong arguments and powerful rhetorical devices
  • No contractions
  • Vocabulary tends to be more academic and may contain some unfamiliar words

Structure

  • Follows a fairly rigid structure that focuses on the development of one clear argument at a time to support a clearly stated thesis.

Location of Thesis

  • Usually stated explicitly
  • Usually located in the 1st paragraph
