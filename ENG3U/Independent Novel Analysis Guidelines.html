Independent Novel Analysis Guidelines

At this point in our course, our roles will be reversed. This time, it is you who will educate your teacher about the novel that you chose in Unit 1. It is expected that you have gone through most of the novel.

Your Tasks to Complete - Unit 5 Summative Task

  • You will read and demonstrate an understanding of your independent novel through a quote analysis.
  • You will draft and revise your writing to create a quote analysis.
  • You will use knowledge of language conventions, to refine expression, and present the quote analysis effectively.

Your Tasks to Complete - Rich Summative Task

  • You will create a video presentation with voice over about your chosen novel.
  • In the video presentation, you will discuss a brief background on the novel you chose (author, time it was made, context).
  • Using PACTS, you will discuss the most important details of the novel.
  • Then, you will choose a literary theory to focus your analysis of the novel.
  • Your video should be at least 5 minutes long.

Let’s identify the PACTS in the novel you chose.

The PACTS chart is a tool used for analyzing stories, it is made up of the plot, atmosphere, characterization, theme, and setting.

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Plot

Guided Questions: 

What is the basic story line:

  • inciting events
  • Climax
  • falling action 

What is the nature of the conflict?

  • person vs person
  • person vs self
  • person vs society
  • person vs nature

Atmosphere

Guided Questions: 

  • What is the dominant mood of the story?
  • Does it change? 
  • How does the mood influence the theme?

Characterization

Guided Questions: 

  • How were the characters portrayed in the story?
  • Who is the protagonist? 
  • Who is the antagonist?
  • Are the characters well-rounded or flat?

Theme

Guide Questions: 

  • What is the main idea of the story?
  • What is the author trying to show us?

Setting

Guide Questions: 

  • Where and when does the story take place?
  • How does this affect the theme, characters, plot or atmosphere?
