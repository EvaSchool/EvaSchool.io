Methods of Persuasion

PERSUASION is the act of causing people to do a particular action, or to believe 

RHETORIC: THE ART OF PERSUASION

According to the philosopher Aristotle, there are three main strategies for developing arguments:

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

LOGOS

Logic and Reason

“Believe me because my reasoning is logical and sensible.”
“I believe you because your argument is valid and supported by evidence.”

ETHOS

Credibility and Ethics

“Believe me because I am a credible person and I am trustworthy.”

“I believe in you because you are an expert in the field and has lots of experience in the matter.”

PATHOS

Emotions and Feelings

“Believe me because my statements will make everyone happy.”

“I believe what you say because it makes me feel at ease and comfortable.”

 

Advertisements use Ethos, Pathos and Logos to persuade customers to buy their product.

What type of persuasion was shown in the video?

ETHOS

Colin Kaepernick is an American football player who oozes good ethics. He bravely protested against racial injustice and systematic oppression in the USA by kneeling during the US national anthem, rather than standing. It resulted in him not having a team to play for (and a barrage of angry comments against him on social media)

Kaepernick sacrificed everything. So when he says the line, “Believe in something, even if it means sacrificing everything,” it’s a powerful use of ETHOS. It leaves the audience thinking that if they trust the character of the speaker (Kaepernick), then they can trust Nike as a credible source, too.
