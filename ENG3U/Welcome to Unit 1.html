Welcome to Unit 1

In Unit 1, students delve into the intricacies of literary analysis and sharpen their writing skills through a series of engaging lessons. The unit begins with exploring the PACTS Short Story Analysis and the RACES strategy for tackling comprehension questions. Students not only grasp the fundamental components of short story analysis but also practice applying these techniques independently. Following this, the focus shifts to characterization in short stories, with an emphasis on understanding character development. Reading "The Lottery" and employing the RACES technique in answering comprehension questions enable students to connect theory with practical application. Additionally, they are encouraged to apply characterization concepts to their prior knowledge, fostering a deeper understanding of character dynamics within narratives.

The unit then moves on to the exploration of irony in literature, covering verbal, situational, and dramatic irony. Students apply their newfound knowledge by reading "The Necklace" and identifying examples of irony through a graphic organizer. As the lessons progress, students are introduced to literary devices in pop culture, promoting a connection between academic concepts and real-world applications. The unit also includes a practical lesson on integrating quotes into essays using MLA style, culminating in a comprehensive assessment that involves reading "An Ounce of Cure" and crafting a well-structured essay.

The final lesson in Unit 1 focuses on metaphors and extended metaphors, introducing students to the nuanced use of figurative language. Reading "The Metaphor" by Budge Wilson serves as a foundation for collaborative analysis with peers. The lesson concludes with students answering comprehension questions and, critically, crafting their own extended metaphor. Overall, Unit 1 provides a well-rounded approach to literary analysis and writing, equipping students with the skills necessary to navigate complex texts and express their insights effectively.
