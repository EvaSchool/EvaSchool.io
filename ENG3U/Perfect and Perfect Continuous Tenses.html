The Focus Today is on Perfect Tenses

Past Perfect or Present Perfect Structure

  • [had/has/have + past participle]

Future Perfect Structure

  • [will + have+ past participle]

Note: When we discuss the perfect tense, always remember that we are talking about two actions.

   

Past Perfect Tense Present Perfect Tense Horizontal Tab 3 of 3

We use the past perfect tense when we focus on a completed action that happened before another completed action in the past.

Example:

  • April had worked diligently before she made the entrance exam.

We use the present perfect tense when an action started in the past and is still happening in the present.

Example:

  • Lady has played the piano since she was 7 years old.

We use the future perfect tense when talking about an action that must happen first so that another action can happen.

Example:

  • Rey will have been with Karen for 6 years by the time they get married.

The Last Verb Tense is the Perfect Continuous Tense

   

Key Notes About This Tense

The Perfect Continuous tense follows this structure:

Past/ Present Perfect Continuous:

  • [ had/has/have + been + base form of the verb+ -ing]

Future Perfect Continuous:

  • [will + have + been + base form of the verb + -ing]

When we use the perfect continuous tense, we focus on the duration it took for one action to happen before another action.

    

Past Perfect Continuous Present Perfect Continuous Future Perfect Continuous

To use the past perfect continuous tense, we need to focus on the duration one action took before another action happened.

Example:

  • He had been working at that company for three years when it went out of business.
  • James had been teaching at the university for more than a year before he left for Asia.

When we use the present perfect continuous tense, we focus on the duration of an action that started in the past and is still continuing in the present.

Example:

  • She has been crying all evening.
  • She has been playing tennis since she was 8.

When using the future perfect continuous tense, we focus on the duration a future action must take before another action in the future can happen.

Example:

  • We will have been cleaning the house all day by the time she returns.
