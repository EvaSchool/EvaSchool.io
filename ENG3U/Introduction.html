PACTS and RACES  

Lesson Description

In the lesson, you will learn about the PACTS Short Story Analysis. You will also learn about the RACES strategy in answering comprehension questions. Then, you will apply these concepts independently.

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning;

B3 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading

B1.2 select and use the most appropriate reading comprehension strategies to understand texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts

B1.3 identify the most important ideas and supporting details in texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts

B3.1 automatically understand most words in a variety of reading contexts

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn and develop an understanding of the basic elements of the short story: plot, character, conflict, setting, theme, symbol, irony and foreshadowing
  • Students will develop and learn the skills needed to read for meaning and to analyze.
  • Students will make connections to short stories
  • Students will identify the most important ideas and supporting details in “The Secret Life of Walter Mitty”

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can understand the basic elements of the short story: plot, character, conflict, setting, theme, symbol, irony and foreshadowing
  • I am developing the skills needed to read for meaning and to analyze.
  • I can make connections to short stories.
  • I can identify the most important ideas and supporting details in “The Secret Life of Walter Mitty.”
