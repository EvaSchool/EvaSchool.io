Basic Communication Theories

Before We Start: Don’t Be Scared of the word, “Theory!”
  • Theories exist in order to explain different phenomena.
  • It is just a particular way of looking at things.

Imagine that you are wearing sunglasses on a hot day. When you view the world through your sunglasses, you will see it in a different light depending on your type of sunglasses.

But once you remove the sunglasses, you will still see the same thing but with a different perspective (normally). In the same way, when you put on another person’s sunglasses, you view everything in a different shade.

  

 That’s what communication theories are.

   

Note!

When we talk about media here, we are not just referring to the channels. We are also referring to the press or the agencies that supply us with information.

Hypodermic Needle Theory

  • What does the ad directly say to you? 
  • Knowing the message of the ad, would you still buy the product? 

Hypodermic Needle Theory - Potential Ideas

  • The ad is trying to scare the audience into the idea that Sun Fizz drinks are promoted by a cartoon character that comes alive.
  • If I were the viewer using this theory, I would believe the ad and buy Sprite instead.

Two-Step Flow Theory

  • Who is the “influencer” in the ad?
  • If this were endorsed by an influencer, would you buy the product?

Two-Step Flow Theory - Potential Ideas

  • The influencer in the ad is the cartoon character.
  • I don’t believe in cartoon characters so I will definitely not buy this product.
  • If this were endorsed by an influencer, I would not buy Sun Fizz and buy Sprite instead.

Agenda Setting Theory

  • What is the agenda in the ad?
  • What reality does this ad shape?

Agenda Setting Theory - Potential Ideas

  • The agenda in the ad is for the viewers to trust their gut instead of a cartoon character because cartoon characters are not real.
  • The reality this ad shapes is that we should not believe in ads that use cartoon characters as influencers.

Uses and Gratifications Theory

  • What needs does the advertisement satisfy?
  • In what way will the product satisfy your needs?

Uses and Gratifications Theory - Potential Ideas

  • The advertisement satisfies the thirst of the audience.
  • Two options are shown in the video but one (Sun Fizz) doesn't give this.
  • Sun Fizz actually scares you instead, making it a bad option.
  • This makes Sprite the best choice to "obey our thirst".
