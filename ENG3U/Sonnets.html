Sonnets

Origin of the Name

From the Italian word sonneto, it means a little sound or song. It is a popular classical form of poetry that is a one-stanza, 14-line poem, written in iambic pentameter.

More Specifics About a Sonnet

  • A sonnet has 14 lines.
  • A sonnet must follow a specific rhyme scheme.
  • A sonnet can be about any subject, though they are often about love or nature.
  • A sonnet introduces a problem or question.
