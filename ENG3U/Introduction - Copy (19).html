Stereotypes, Segregation and Labelling

Lesson Description

You will explore the theme of stereotypes, segregation and labelling in this lesson. You will see how these concepts are present in poetry.

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.

B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

Specific Expectations

B1.6 analyzes texts in terms of the information, ideas, issues, or themes they explore, examining how various aspects of the texts contribute to the presentation or development of these elements.

B1.7 evaluates the effectiveness of texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts, using evidence from the text to support their opinions.

B2.1 write for different purposes and audiences using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

Learning Goals

  • Students will understand stereotypes in media.
  • Students will be able to locate and explain how stereotypes are expressed in a piece of work.
  • Students will find a piece of literature or media and share how that piece portrays segregation or a certain stereotype.
  • Students will write a poem using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

 

  • I can understand stereotypes in media.
  • I can locate and explain how stereotypes are expressed in a piece of work.
  • I can find a piece of literature or media and share how that piece portrays segregation or a certain stereotype.
  • I can write a poem using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.
