Basic Communication Theories

Lesson Description

You will learn about four basic communication theories that will help them understand media texts more. You will learn about the hypodermic needle theory, two-step flow theory, agenda setting theory, and uses and gratifications theory in this lesson. You will then apply these communication lenses to examine various advertisements.

Overall Expectations

D1 Understanding Media Texts: Demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts.

D2 Understanding Media Forms, Conventions, and Techniques: Identify some media forms and explain how the conventions and techniques associated with them are used to create meaning.

Specific Expectations

D1.1 Explain how media texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts, are created to suit particular purposes and audiences.

D1.2 Interpret media texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts, identifying and explaining the overt and implied messages they convey.

D1.4 Explain why the same media text might prompt different responses from different audiences.

D1.5 Identify the perspectives and/or biases evident in media texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts, and comment on any questions they may raise about beliefs, values, identity, and power.

D2.1 Identify general and specific characteristics of a variety of media forms and explain how they shape content and create.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about basic communication theories.
  • Students will apply the basic communication theories to some advertisements.
  • Students will identify general and specific characteristics of basic communication theories and explain how they shape content and create meaning in advertisements.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

 

  • I can explain the different basic communication theories.
  • I can apply the basic communication theories to advertisements.
  • I can identify general and specific characteristics of basic communication theories
  • I can explain how the characteristics shape content and create meaning in advertisements.
