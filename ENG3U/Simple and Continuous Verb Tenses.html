Simple and Continuous Verb Tenses

 

  

In discussing verb tenses, there are three major periods:

Then There are SIMPLE Verb Tenses

 

Simple Past Tense Simple Present Tense Simple Future Tense

We use the simple past tense when we talk about something that happened in the past.

Examples:

  • I watched a movie yesterday.
  • She studied in the library.

We use the simple present tense to talk about something that is true now. It can be a fact or a description.

Examples:

  • She starts a new diet today.
  • She lives in New York.

We use the simple future tense in talking about an uncertain or certain event in the future. We use will or shall with the verb.

Examples:

  • I will go to the gym next week.
  • I shall do my homework for today.

 

There Are Also CONTINUOUS Verb Tenses

  

Basic Structure of Continuous Tenses

  • [be verb] + [base form of the verb]+ing

For Past/Present Continuous:

  • was/is + walking

For Future Continuous:

  • will be walking

   

Past Continuous Tense Present Continuous Tense Future Continuous Tense 

We use the past continuous tense to talk about an event that was “happening in the past”. Usually, we use this tense alongside another action in the past tense.

Example:

  • Shane was doing her homework when Jenny called.
  • When Lara opened the door, Jenny was waiting outside.

We use the present continuous tense to talk about an action that is happening at the exact moment someone is speaking.

Example:

  • I am calling him right now.
  • He is answering the phone at the moment.

We use the future continuous tense to talk about something that will happen at a certain time in the future.

Example:

  • I will be joining this class in two months.
  • I will be watching the movie after dinner.
