Analyzing Non-Fiction Essay

Lesson Description

You will learn how to analyze nonfiction essays in this lesson, based on genre, audience, and purpose.

Overall Expectations

C2 Using Knowledge of Form and Style: draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience.

Specific Expectations

C2.1 writes for different purposes and audiences using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

C2.2 establishes a distinctive voice in their writing, modifying language and tone skillfully and effectively to suit the form, audience, and purpose of writing.

Learning Goals

  • Students will be able to apply comprehension monitoring strategies during and after reading. Such tactics include determining importance using theme, main idea, and supporting details in grade-level informational/expository text and/or literary/narrative text.
  • Students will be able to apply comprehension monitoring strategies for informational and technical materials, complex narratives, and expositions, by using prior knowledge.
  • Students will use different reading strategies to better understand non-fiction.
  • Students will identify the main idea and details from non-fiction texts, as well as compare and contrast topics.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

 

  • I can apply comprehension monitoring strategies during and after reading.
  • I can apply comprehension monitoring strategies for informational and technical materials, complex narratives, and expositions, by using prior knowledge.
  • I can use different reading strategies to better understand non-fiction.
  • I can identify the main idea and details from non-fiction texts, as well as compare and contrast topics.
  • I can analyze non-fiction texts and explain my reasonings.
