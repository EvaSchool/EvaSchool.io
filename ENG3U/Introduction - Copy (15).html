Welcome to Unit 3

Unit 3 provides a comprehensive exploration of poetry, covering various forms, literary devices, close reading techniques, and thematic analysis. The unit begins with an introduction to basic information and elements involved in different forms of poetry. Students engage in practical exercises, practising the essential skills of reading and analyzing poems, fostering a deeper understanding of the nuances within the poetic expression.

Building on this foundation, the lessons progress to a review of literary devices commonly found in poetry. This critical examination enhances students' ability to recognize and appreciate the artistry inherent in poetic compositions. The unit also introduces the concept of poetry close reading, guiding students on how to delve into the layers of meaning embedded in poetic works. A significant thematic exploration within Unit 3 revolves around stereotypes, segregation, and labelling, with a focus on how these concepts manifest in poetry. This thematic analysis encourages students to critically examine societal issues and perspectives presented through poetic language.

As the unit advances, students are exposed to the evolving landscape of digital poetry, providing insights into the unique elements and forms associated with this contemporary mode of poetic expression. The exploration of sonnets marks another highlight, wherein students delve into the distinctive elements and formats of both Petrarchan and Shakespearean sonnets, gaining a deeper appreciation for the structure and artistry inherent in these classical poetic forms. Overall, Unit 3 provides a rich and varied exploration of poetry, equipping students with the analytical tools and thematic insights necessary for a nuanced understanding of poetic works.
