Characterization & Theme

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn about characterization in short stories.

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning; 

B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning;

B3 Reading With Fluency: use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently;

Specific Expectations

B1.1 read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading.

B1.2 select and use the most appropriate reading comprehension strategies to understand texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts.

B2.2 identify a variety of text features and explain how they help communicate meaning.

B2.3 identify a variety of elements of style in texts and explain how they help communicate meaning and enhance the effectiveness of the texts.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn and develop an understanding of the basic elements of the short story: plot, character, conflict, setting, theme, symbol, irony and foreshadowing
  • Students will develop and learn the skills needed to read for meaning and to analyze.
  • Students will make connections to short stories.
  • Students will apply their understanding of short story elements to “The Lottery”

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can understand what I have read in “The Lottery,” by Shirley Jackson.
  • I can answer comprehension questions using the RACES technique.
  • I can understand characterization in the short story, “The Lottery.”
  • I can give examples of direct and indirect characterization.
