Locate the Metaphor

Extended Metaphors

  • These are metaphors that are developed in great detail
  • They can vary in length: from that of a sentence or a paragraph, to encompassing an entire work.
  • The author takes a single metaphor and employs it at length, using various subjects, images, ideas and situations.
  • This device is commonly used in poetry, as well as prose.

The Extended Metaphor: Lesson and Activity

Example

An author who is wanting to tell a story about a criminal might employ an extended metaphor. They could then instead tell a story about a fox who attacks a farmer’s flock of chickens. The fox would represent the criminal and the chickens would represent the victims.


The extended metaphor would be: “The assassin is a wolf in the night, silently hunting helpless and unwary chickens whose life he extinguishes under the cover of pitch black darkness.”

Effectiveness of Extended Metaphors

  • These are useful in helping your audience make more complex connections
  • They can spark the imagination of the author’s readers and help them to make new and unexpected connections.
  • They are alternative descriptive images and layered comparisons that help readers break down complex subjects and view things in new ways.
