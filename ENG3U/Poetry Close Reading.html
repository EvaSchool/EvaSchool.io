Poetry Close Reading

Poetry Analysis

  • Analysis is defined as separating something into its component elements for detailed examination.
  • We will do the same way with poetry where we will identify its component elements so we can examine them in detail.

Why do we need to analyze poetry?

To learn how to analyze poetry, we will read Emily Dickinson’s “Tell all the truth but tell it slant,” first and then identify the following components:

The Main Idea

Poetry analysis starts by giving a brief “plot summary” of the poem. This means to explain in general terms the main idea of the poem. 

The main idea should be stated in sentence form.  Additionally, you should respond to the main idea of the poem.

Main Idea

The poem, “Tell All the truth but tell it slant,” by Emily Dickinson, talks about how we should not be upfront in saying things because some people can’t handle the truth.

Response

I agree with Emily Dickinson’s stance, because sometimes we need to approach people with compassion, otherwise, we cannot get our message across.

Diction

The next step is to discuss the diction of the poem and its effectiveness in contributing to the main idea of the poem.

  • Are there words, phrases, or lines that are particularly striking?
The line, “The Truth must dazzle gradually or every man be blind,” struck me the most because it suggests a way for people to say the truth.

Literary Devices

Lastly, analyze the figurative meaning in the poem. Identify relevant figurative devices (or the absence of them).

  • How do those techniques effectively communicate the main idea of the poem?
The personification of the truth and how it is compared to lightning shows how quick the truth should be said. Ultimately, the main idea which is, “to tell the truth, but tell it slant” resonates through its last line as well. With these strategies, I believe the author was able to communicate the main point of telling the truth compassionately.

Mechanics

We then discuss the mechanics of the poem: use of rhyme and rhythm, sound devices, line length, etc. 

  • Explain how the mechanical aspects of the poem contribute to the meaning of the poem.
Dickinson’s poem is short, but meaningful. Not all the lines rhyme and I think that was done on purpose. In a way, it also applies the main idea of the poem: by presenting the main idea in a creative way but piercing the reader in the end.
