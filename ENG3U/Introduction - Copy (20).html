Digital Poetry           

Lesson Description

In the lesson, you will learn about the PACTS Short Story Analysis. You will also learn about the RACES strategy in answering comprehension questions. Then, you will apply these concepts independently.

Overall Expectations

B2 Understanding Form and Style: Recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

B3 Reading With Fluency: use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading.

B1.2 select and use the most appropriate reading comprehension strategies to understand texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts.

B1.3 identify the most important ideas and supporting details in texts, including increasingly complex or difficult texts.

B3.1 automatically understand most words in a variety of reading contexts.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about different forms of digital poetry.
  • Students will learn about elements in digital poetry.
  • Students will write a poem using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

 

  • I can explain the differences among forms of digital poetry.
  • I can identify elements within digital poetry.
  • I can write a poem using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.
