Literary Terms in Poetry

Remember!

Often in poetry, the meaning of the words isn't literal…It’s Figurative!

Alliteration

The repeating of beginning consonant sounds.

Assonance

The repetition of a vowel sound.

Repetition

The repeating of a word or phrase.

End Rhyme

The rhyming of words at the end of two or more lines of poetry.

Onomatopoeia

The use of a word that sounds like what it means.

Consonance

The repetition of consonant sounds.

Rhyme Scheme

A pattern of lines within a poem that go together.

The use of concrete details to describe what the reader should see, hear, feel, smell, taste.

Examples:

  • He felt the rough tablecloth.
  • The kitchen smelled of freshly-baked cookies.

A direct comparison between two unlike things.

 

Examples: 

  • It is the East, and Juliet is the Sun. 
  • Her home was a prison. 

A direct comparison between two unlike things using like or as. 

 

Examples: 

  • My love is like a red, red rose.
  • Jim was as thin as a pencil.

Examples:

Example:

  • I’d jump in front of a train for you.

The purposeful re-use of words and phrases for a certain effect. In music, this can often take the form of the chorus.

 

Examples: 

  • “I would walk 500 miles / And I would walk 500 more / Just to be the man that walks a thousand miles to fall down at your door.”
  • "The old man walked down the street, down the street, down the street"
