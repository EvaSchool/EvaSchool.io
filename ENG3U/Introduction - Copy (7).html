Methods of Persuasion

Lesson Description

You will learn about persuasion, the three key methods of rhetoric, and how to effectively apply this in a persuasive essay in this lesson.

Overall Expectations

C1 Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience.

C2 Using Knowledge of Form and Style: draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience.

Specific Expectations

C1.1 identify the topic, purpose, and audience for a variety of writing tasks.

C1.2 generates, expands, explores, and focuses ideas for potential writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.

C2.1 writes for different purposes and audiences using a variety of literary,    informational, and graphic forms.

Learning Goals

  • Students will be able to identify keywords; use advanced search strategies; independently locate appropriate and varied information sources; and evaluate sources as primary/secondary.
  • Students will be able to evaluate the reliability, credibility, and validity of information from a variety of sources.
  • Students will be able to understand there are various reasons for reading to identify text features and their use in helping the reader understand the text to confirm understanding of the informational text by asking and answering questions about key details to ask and answer questions to seek help, get information, or clarify something that is not understood.
  • Students will write a persuasive essay using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

 

  • I can identify keywords, use advanced search strategies, independently locate appropriate and varied information sources, and evaluate primary/secondary sources.
  • I can evaluate the reliability, credibility, and validity of information from a variety of sources.
  • I can understand that there are various reasons for reading.
  • I can write a persuasive essay using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.
