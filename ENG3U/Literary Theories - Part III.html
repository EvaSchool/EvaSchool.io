Feminism

focuses on the economic, political, social, and psychological oppression of women

Applying the Feminism Lens to “Cinderella”

  • Consider the misogynist theme of abused-girl-waiting-to-be-rescued.  
    • Can Cinderella save herself?
    • Why does she need a man to rescue her?
    • Can’t women exist independently of men?

  • Cinderella as the “Good Girl”
    • Consider the values conveyed in the portrayal of the “good girl” as physically beautiful, whereas the “wicked girls” are physically ugly.
    • Are women only valuable to men as sexualized objects?

Marxism

focuses on the representation of class conflict as well as the reinforcement of class distinctions

Applying the Marxism Lens to “Cinderella”

The Unseen Battle of Classes:

  • Consider Cinderella as being a representative of the proletariat
  • She is seen as being oppressed by her bourgeoisie stepmother and stepsisters, who have stolen her rightful inheritance and turned her into a servant in her own home
  • She desires to join the ranks of the bourgeoisie, and does so by marrying the prince

Historical / Hermeneutical

applies to text-specific historical information about the time during which an author wrote

Applying the Historical Lens to “The Black Cat”

  • In "The Black Cat," the narrator says that his wife alluded to "the ancient popular notion, which regarded all black cats as witches in disguise."
    • Poe carefully introduced this element into the story to give it another layer of possible meaning.
    • Poe brings attention to the association between cats and witches. 
    • Witches were thought to be able to assume the form of a cat, and a cat was a witch's "familiar." 
  • Also, Poe depicted a motiveless murderer whose actions cannot be sufficiently explained
    • This showed difficulties in both scientific and religious thought
    • He ironically upheld the mysterious nature of the human will in a time dominated by intellectual rationalism

Moral / Didactic

concerned with content and values, particularly with how the work imparts morality, philosophy, and religion

Applying the Moral/Didactic Lens to “The Black Cat”

  • “The Black Cat” by Edgar Allan Poe is in many ways a moral tale that deals with the tension between love and hate, and that also warns about the dangers of alcohol. 
  • The narrator appears at first to love both his wife and his pets, but by the end of the story, his fondness has turned to neglect, spite, and even hatred, particularly for Pluto and his successor.

Freudian /Psychoanalytic

centers on the psychology of the characters and analyze character motivation, behaviour and actions

Applying the Freudian Lens to “Cinderella”

Cinderella: Persecution Syndrome (A View on the Abuser)

  • In the story, the narcissistic stepmother and stepsisters persecute Cinderella, who arouses jealousy and threatens their fragile superiority.
  • They go in for the psychological kill, where they create a myth that Cinderella is selfish and arrogant and wants to outdo everybody else.
  • They bully and abuse her to fulfill their need to be better than someone else.

Cinderella Persecution Syndrome (A View on the Victim)

  • Consequently, Cinderella grows up feeling guilty and ends up with feelings of inadequacy and low self-esteem.
  • In the story, she goes to the ball with the help of her friends.
  • In reality, it would probably take years of psychotherapy to put her together again.
