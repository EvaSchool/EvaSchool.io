Characterization & Theme

RECALL: Characterization

  • This the method that authors use, so that readers can get to know a character.
  • Characterization occurs any time the author uses details to teach us about a character.
    • Characterization is done through:
    • What the characters say (and how they say it)
    • What their actions are
    • What other characters say about them

“The Lottery”

  • We will be reading this short story written by Shirley Jackson for the New Yorker on June 26, 1948
  • "The Lottery" is a chilling and thought-provoking short story.
  • Since being published, it has become one of the most widely anthologized and discussed works of American literature.
  • It has a unique setting: in a seemingly idyllic small town on a warm summer day, the story explores the darkness of human nature and the dangers of blind reliance to tradition. 

Analysing “The Lottery”

  • We typically see traditions as something positive.
  • Usually, a tradition benefits almost everyone in the specific group of people.
  • However, after reading the story, we can clearly see that some of these traditions pose more harm than good.
  • As a society, we have developed past these types of traditions and we have reached morality and maturity
