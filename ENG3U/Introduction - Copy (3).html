Integrating Quotes in Essays

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn how to integrate quotes in Essays using MLA style.

Overall Expectations

C1 Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience;

Specific Expectations

C1.1 identify the topic, purpose, and audience for a variety of writing tasks

C1.2 generate, expand, explore, and focus ideas for potential writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate

Learning Goals

  • Students will create a written product where a thesis is supported by integration of quotes
  • Students will present the product in an appropriate manner to a meaningful audience
  • Students will voice their original ideas; demonstrate content knowledge; persuade an audience; listen critically and build upon ideas of others; ask clarifying questions and challenge statements of others; negotiate and compromise
  • Students will generate, expand, explore, and focus ideas for “An Ounce of Cure”, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can create a written product where a thesis is supported by integration of quotes
  • I can present the product in an appropriate manner to a meaningful audience
  • I can voice my original ideas; demonstrate content knowledge; persuade an audience; listen critically and build upon ideas of others; ask clarifying questions and challenge statements of others; negotiate and compromise
  • I can generate, expand, explore, and focus ideas for “An Ounce of Cure”, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.
