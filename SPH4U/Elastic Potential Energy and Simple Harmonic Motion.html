Elastic Potential Energy and Simple Harmonic Motion

Elastic potential energy is energy stored as a result of applying a force to deform an elastic object. 
The energy is stored until the force is removed and the object springs back to its original shape, doing work in the process. 

Many objects are designed specifically to store elastic potential energy, for example:

The coil spring of a wind-up clock
An archer's stretched bow
A bent diving board, just before a diver jump
The twisted rubber band that powers a toy airplane
A bouncy ball, compressed at the moment it bounces off a brick wall.

Spring Forces

Motion of Oscillations

Oscillatory motion is defined as the to and fro motion of an object from its mean position. The ideal condition is that the object can be in oscillatory motion forever in the absence of friction but in the real world, this is not possible and the object has to settle into equilibrium.

Examples of Oscillatory Motion

Oscillation of simple pendulum
Vibrating strings of musical instruments are a mechanical example of oscillatory motion
Movement of spring
Alternating current is an electrical example of oscillatory motion
A series of oscillations are seen in the cosmological model

Simple Harmonic Motion

Simple harmonic motion (SHM) is a type of oscillatory motion that is defined as the particle moving along a straight line with an acceleration that is moving toward a fixed point on the line such that the magnitude is proportional to the distance from the fixed point.
