The Five Key Equations for Uniformly Accelerated Motion

Free Falling Objects

free fall the motion of a falling object where the only force acting on the object is gravity

When you let go of a ball, it will fall down because Earth‚Äôs gravity pulls it down. When you throw a ball upward, it will move upward for a time, stop, change direction, and then move downward. In both cases, the ball is moving under the influence of gravity. An object that is only moving under the influence of gravity is said to be in free fall.
