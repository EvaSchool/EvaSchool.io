Forces of Friction

What Causes Friction?

Friction always opposes the direction of motion or the attempted motion.
Friction is caused due to the irregularities between the surface and the object. .
The more irregularities between the surface and the object    -->  The more roughness -->       The more friction the object is encountered, and vice versa.

Friction Dependence

We learned that friction depends on how smoother or how rough the surface is.
Also friction is affected by the Normal Force acting on the object. 
This Normal force is considered to be a reaction force from the ground on the object. 
This force prevents object from sliding on surfaces.

Kinetic Friction

Kinetic Friction is involved whenever the object is sliding across a surface. 
The magnitude of Kinetic Friction force and Normal force are linked by the given formula. 
Coefficient of Kinetic Friction is just a number that relates the force of kinetic Friction between two surfaces in contact with the normal force when they meet.

Example on Kinetic Friction

By Considering a hockey puck sliding across the ice, we can see that 
The kinetic friction force opposes the direction of motion. 
The amount of kinetic friction force between the ice and hockey puck is quite small. 
This is due to the fact that the coefficient of friction between the hockey puck and the ice is small.
