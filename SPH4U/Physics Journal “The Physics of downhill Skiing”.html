Acceleration and Kinetic Friction

Reducing the coefficient of kinetic friction between the skis and the snow will decrease the force of kinetic friction.
Reducing the force of kinetic friction will increase the acceleration of the skier.
Wax on the bottom of the skis helps reduce the kinetic friction between the skis and the snow.

Acceleration and Air Resistance

The air resistance of an object is proportional to the area of that object.
The skiers go into a crouching position, called a tuck. 
This will decrease the air resistance and will increase the acceleration.

Acceleration and Larger mass

Maintaining control

The skiers use friction to slow down while making turns on the snow. 
The skiers have to angle the skis to dig into the snow, making use of the normal force. 
The tighter the curve, the shorter the overall distance the skier has to go and the faster he reaches the bottom.
Skis that are shorter and side-cut can significantly reduce the radius of curvature.

Maintaining Control with Leg Strength

Leg strength is also important for making sharp, controlled turns that increase the overall speed down the slope.
Skiers train the muscles that allow them to make the purest possible curves (Turns).

Ski Poles

Skiers use poles to give boost for extra force when they start from rest.
As the skier pushes on the slope with the poles, the slope exerts a force on the skier.
The parallel component of this force will increase acceleration down the slope. 
The perpendicular component of this force will reduce the normal force of the slope on the skier, reducing kinetic friction.

Helmet and Safety

During a crash, a skier’s speed changes from a high speed to zero in almost no time. 
That causes a large force to act on the skier. 
A helmet provides a cushion that allows the skier’s head to take more time to slow down from full speed to zero during crash.
