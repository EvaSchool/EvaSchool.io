The Law of Conservation of Energy

“Energy is neither created nor destroyed. It can only change form.”

The total mechanical energy of a moving roller coaster car is the same at every point. That is, the sum of the gravitational energy and the kinetic energy remains constant.

Isolated and Open Systems

An isolated system is a system that cannot interact or exchange energy with external systems; also called a closed system.

For example, you may consider a diver to be an isolated system if you ignore influences such as air friction and the energy of vibrations in the diving board.

An open system is a system that can interact with another external system.

In reality, a diver is an open system that exchanges energy with the air, the diving board, the water, and other real-world systems.

Law of Conservation of Energy

The concepts of isolated and open systems allow us to state the law of conservation of energy more formally:

“Energy is neither created nor destroyed in an isolated system. It can only change form.”

Biological Energy Transformations

Biochemical energy is a type of chemical potential energy stored in the cells and other basic structures of biological organisms.

 Power

Power is the rate of work done by a force over time or the rate at which the energy of an open system changes.

The SI unit of power is the watt (W), named after James Watt (1736–1819), a developer of the steam engine. One watt is equal to one joule per second. Note that the symbol for watt is not italicized (W), but the variable used for work is an italic W.

Notice that power is the rate at which work is done. Work is a way to transfer energy from one system to another over time. As a result, power is also equal to the energy output of a device per unit of time.
