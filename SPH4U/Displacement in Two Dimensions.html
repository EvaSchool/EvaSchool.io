The Perpendicular Components of a Vector Method

A more straightforward way of adding two or more displacement vectors is to resolve, or separate, each vector into perpendicular components. For two dimensions, the perpendicular components of a vector are the parts of the vector that lie along either the x-axis or the y-axis. We obtain the components of the total displacement vector by adding the parallel displacement components. To do this, we need to be familiar with obtaining the components of a vector, which requires trigonometry. The components of

Adding Vectors Algebraically

You can add two or more displacement vectors by separating each vector into its horizontal and vertical components. Once you have these components, add all the horizontal components as one-dimensional vectors. Then do the same with the vertical displacement components.
