Electric Potential and Electric Potential Energy Due to Point Charges

Callout

Electric Potential Energy

The energy stored in an electric field can do work on a positively charged particle.

The electric potential is inversely proportional to the distance from the charge and proportional to the amount of charge producing the field.

Callout

Only changes in potential energy are important, so you must always be clear about choosing the reference point for potential energy. Electric potential is proportional to electric potential energy, so you must also pay attention to the reference point for electric potential.

For example, when dealing with a point charge, follow the standard convention by choosing V 5 0 at an infinite distance from the source charge. Infinite distance represents a distance so far away that any potential produced by a charge is negligible and for all intents and purposes equal to zero. In many other problems, choose Earth as V 5 0 because Earth conducts charge well. Put another way, the electric ground is the point where V 5 0.

For two or more charges, use the superposition principle. Remember that the electric potentials of negative charges are negative, and the electric potentials of positive charges are positive. However, because electric potential is not a vector, the total electric potential of several point charges equals the algebraic sum of the electric potentials resulting from each of the individual charges and their specific distances from the chosen location.

The Van de Graaff Generator

The Van de Graaff generator, produces an electric potential on the conducting sphere, or dome, by separating the charge at one place and moving the charge to the conductor. As you can see in the figure, a moving cloth or rubber conveyor-style belt receives electrons from a lower conducting comb at point A. The negatively charged part of the belt moves upward toward the conducting sphere and gives up the negative charge to the upper comb at point B.

The charge travels to the conducting sphere and gives the dome a net negative charge. As the generator continues to run, the amount of charge on the sphere builds. When the charge reaches high enough levels, the electrons jump from the sphere through the air to another conductor brought close to the sphere, producing artificial lightning.
