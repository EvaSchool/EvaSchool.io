Learning Goals

Understand the basic makeup of waves and how we describe characteristics of a wave.  We also introduce reflection.

Analyze how light interacts with a transparent medium and extend Snell’s law to internal reflection.

Analyze interference patterns produced by apertures when waves pass through them.

Understand how light can be represented as both a particle (follows classical particle mechanics) and a wave.

Study about interference patterns of light waves through double slits and model the pattern that is produced.

  

Success Criteria

  • Explain the basic makeup of waves and how we describe characteristics of a wave as well as reflection.
  • Explain how light interacts with a transparent medium and extend Snell’s law to internal reflection.
  • Explain interference patterns produced by apertures when waves pass through them.
  • Understand how light can be represented as both a particle (follows classical particle mechanics) and a wave.
  • Explain interference patterns of light waves through double slits and model the pattern that is produced.
