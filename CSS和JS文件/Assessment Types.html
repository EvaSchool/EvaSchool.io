Understanding Assessment For, As, and Of Learning in the OSSD

Introduction

The Ontario Ministry of Education emphasizes a balanced approach to assessment that supports and improves student learning. Within the framework of the Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD), three distinct but interrelated types of assessment are used: Assessment for Learning, Assessment as Learning, and Assessment of Learning. These assessment types are central to effective teaching and are outlined in the Ministry document Growing Success (2010).

1. Assessment For Learning (AfL)

Definition

Assessment for Learning is formative assessment. It occurs before and during the learning process, and its primary purpose is to inform instruction by providing feedback that helps students improve.

Purpose

  • To determine students’ prior knowledge and skill levels.
  • To diagnose learning needs and misconceptions.
  • To guide instructional planning and differentiation.
  • To provide ongoing feedback to help students improve.

Characteristics

  • Conducted throughout the learning process.
  • Focuses on learning goals and success criteria.
  • Involves descriptive feedback, not just marks.
  • Includes quizzes, drafts, observations, peer assessments, etc.

Teacher’s Role

  • Clarify learning intentions and success criteria.
  • Adjust instruction based on assessment data.
  • Provide timely, specific feedback.
  • Encourage student reflection and goal setting.

2. Assessment As Learning (AaL)

Definition

Assessment as Learning emphasizes the student’s role in the learning process. Students actively monitor and reflect on their own learning, becoming self-directed learners.

Purpose

  • To develop students’ metacognitive skills.
  • To promote responsibility and ownership of learning.
  • To help students identify their strengths and areas for growth.

Characteristics

  • Student-centered and reflective.
  • Involves goal setting, self-assessment, peer feedback.
  • Encourages self-monitoring and strategy adjustment.

Teacher’s Role

  • Teach students how to self-assess and give peer feedback.
  • Provide tools for reflection (e.g., journals, checklists).
  • Model how to use feedback effectively.
  • Support students in setting and reviewing learning goals.

3. Assessment Of Learning (AoL)

Definition

Assessment of Learning is summative assessment. It takes place at the end of the learning cycle and is used to determine achievement levels and assign grades.

Purpose

  • To evaluate and certify student achievement.
  • To report grades to students, families, and schools.
  • To inform decisions about credit granting and progress.

Characteristics

  • Formal and structured.
  • Results in grades or marks.
  • Includes tests, final projects, performance tasks, etc.

Teacher’s Role

  • Ensure assessments are valid and aligned with expectations.
  • Use professional judgment to assign grades.
  • Gather evidence from observations, conversations, and products.
  • Communicate results clearly and fairly.

Integrating the Three Types of Assessment

Each assessment type serves a unique purpose, and all three should be used in combination for effective teaching and learning:

Aspect Assessment For Learning Assessment As Learning Assessment Of Learning
Timing Ongoing, during learning Ongoing, during learning End of learning cycle
Focus Diagnosing and guiding instruction Developing self-monitoring and reflection Evaluating final achievement
Student Role Receiver of feedback Active participant in learning Demonstrator of achievement
Feedback Descriptive and ongoing Reflective and growth-oriented Summative and evaluative

Conclusion

The integration of Assessment for, as, and of Learning ensures a comprehensive, student-centered approach to assessment under the OSSD framework. When used together, they support academic success, promote self-awareness, and empower students to take ownership of their learning journey.
