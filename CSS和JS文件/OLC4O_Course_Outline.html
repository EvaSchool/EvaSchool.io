Course Overview

This course is designed to help students strengthen their literacy skills to meet the requirements for the Ontario Secondary School Literacy Graduation Requirement. Students will read and respond to a variety of informational, narrative, and graphic texts, and produce a range of writing forms. With regards to Reading and Responding to Texts, Students will:

  • Read and demonstrate understanding of a variety of informational, narrative, and graphic texts;
  • Use knowledge of text structures and features to understand and analyze texts;
  • Apply a variety of reading strategies to enhance comprehension and retention;

With regards to Writing, Students will:

  • Generate and organize ideas to produce clear and coherent writing for different purposes;
  • Use the writing process to draft, revise, and polish writing pieces, including summaries, opinion pieces, news reports, and personal reflections;
  • Apply conventions of grammar, spelling, and punctuation accurately;

With regards to Oral Communication, Students will:

  • Communicate orally for a variety of purposes and audiences;
  • Listen actively and respond appropriately to oral texts and discussions;
  • Reflect on and improve oral communication skills;

With regards to Media Literacy, Students will:

  • Demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts;
  • Analyze how media forms and techniques influence meaning and audience response;
  • Create media texts for different purposes and audiences;

Units of Study:

  • Unit One: Literacy and Grammar
  • Unit Two: Informational and Graphic Texts
  • Unit Three: Grammar, Summaries and Narrative Texts
  • Unit Four: Newswriting, Opinion Essays and Grammar
  • Unit Five: Oral Literacy
