Complexity of Managerial Work

Select to learn more.

 Although the management process may seem straightforward, the reality is more complex. Henry Mintzberg, in his book "The Nature of Managerial Work," describes the daily work of corporate chief executives as incredibly busy and demanding. Their days are filled with various activities like managing emails, phone calls, meetings, and more.


Today, with the addition of emails, instant messages, text messages, and voicemails, managerial responsibilities have become even more intricate.

Informational Roles:

Monitor: Scanning for information.
Disseminator: Sharing information.
Spokesperson: Acting as an official communicator.

Interpersonal Roles:

Figurehead: Modeling key principles and policies.
Leader: Providing direction and instilling enthusiasm.
Liaison: Coordinating with others.

Decisional Roles:

Disturbance-handler: Dealing with problems and conflicts.
Resource Allocator: Handling budgets and resources.
Negotiator: Making deals and forging agreements.
Entrepreneur: Developing new initiatives.

"Busy Workdays" "Financial and Personal Rewards''

Managers work long hours and at an intense pace. Their days are filled with fragmented and varied tasks, involving communication through various media. Most importantly, they achieve their work through building and managing interpersonal relationships.

Despite the demands and stress, managerial work can be intellectually challenging and personally and financially rewarding. Managers often find satisfaction in contributing to their organizations' success.
