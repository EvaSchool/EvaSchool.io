Developing a Quality Workforce

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will learn how an organization develops their quality workforce. 

Overall Expectations

E1. demonstrate an understanding of the various organizational structures used to manage the workforce effectively.
E2. assess the ways in which organizational structures have changed to adapt to the changing nature of work.
E3. evaluate the role of human resources within an organization.

Specific Expectations

E1.1 assess the four traditional organizational structures (functional, divisional, hybrid, matrix)

E1.2 assess current organizational structures (e.g., team, network, boundaries) with respect to the ways in which they increase productivity and competitive advantage;

E1.3 explain the role of the manager in dealing with trends in organizational design structures (e.g., shorter chain of command, decentralization, increased empowerment, move towards more organic structures);

E1.4 explain how organizational design (e.g., communication channels, team structures, alternative work schedules) can support a positive corporate culture

E2.1 assess the relationship between the individual and an organization, considering issues such as the meaning of work, the psychological contract, the quality of work life, and job satisfaction;

E2.2 compare alternative job-design approaches (e.g., rotation, simplification, enrichment, enlargement); 

E2.3 compare the strengths and weaknesses of various work settings and arrangements for individuals and groups (e.g., job sharing, self-managed teams, work schedules, offsite work, contract work).

E3.1 identify and describe the impact and importance of legal considerations in the human-resource process (e.g., with regard to wages, employment equity, health and safety, employee rights, bargaining agreements);

E3.2 analyse the relationship between an organization’s objectives and the human resource process (e.g., planning, recruitment, selection);

E3.3 explain the strategies and concepts involved in developing and retaining a quality workforce (e.g., career development, diversity policies, labour–management relations, orientation, training);

E3.4 describe, drawing on information from a variety of sources, including the Internet, current management opportunities and the education and training they require;

E3.5 explain the nature and importance of performance appraisal within an organization; 

E3.6 compare major performance-appraisal methods and techniques (e.g., comparisons, behaviourally anchored rating scale, graphic-rating scale, 360° feedback).

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn orientation and socialization and describe their importance to organizations.
  • Students will give examples of on-the-job and off-the-job training.
  • Students will identify strengths and weaknesses of trait-based, behavior-based, and results-based performance appraisals.
  • Students will understand how 360-degree appraisals work.
