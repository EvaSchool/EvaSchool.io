Agenda Setting: 

Effective managers develop an agenda, which is a set of action priorities encompassing goals and plans over various time frames. Initially, these agendas are vague and loosely connected, but they become more specific as managers gather information from diverse sources. Managers continuously keep these agendas in mind and act upon them when opportunities arise.

Networking:

Managers implement their agendas by collaborating with individuals both within and outside the organization. Networking is the process of building and maintaining positive relationships with people who can assist in achieving their goals. This practice creates social capital, a valuable resource that enables managers to attract support and assistance from a wide range of individuals.

 

An effective manager's network may include peers, superiors, higher-level executives, subordinates, and team members, as well as external contacts like customers, suppliers, and community representatives.
