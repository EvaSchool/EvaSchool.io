Growth Strategy

Growth strategies aim to expand an organization's size and scope, often through acquisitions, mergers, and global expansion. However, excessive growth can be challenging to manage effectively.

Stability Strategy

Stability strategies focus on maintaining the existing course of action without significant changes. They are chosen when an organization is performing well or when the environment is stable.

Renewal Strategy

Renewal strategies, also known as retrenchment strategies, are designed to address weaknesses and improve performance. They may involve cutting size and reorganizing operations.

Combination Strategy

Combination strategies combine elements of other strategies simultaneously. They are common in complex and diversified organizations.

  • Growth and Diversification Strategies

    Growth is often pursued through diversification, where expansion occurs in new and different business areas. This can be related to diversification, where the new businesses align with the organization's existing capabilities.

Restructuring Strategies

Restructuring strategies are employed when organizations face challenges. They include turnaround, downsizing, and divestiture strategies to correct weaknesses and improve competitiveness.

Restructuring by turnaround focuses on fixing specific performance problems. 

Restructuring by downsizing decreases the size of operations, often by reducing the workforce. 

Restructuring by divestiture also reduces size, this time by selling off parts of the organization to refocus on core competencies, cut costs, and improve operating efficiency. 

Global Strategies

Global strategies involve how organizations operate in the global market. Firms may adopt globalization, multi-domestic, or transnational strategies based on their approach to standardization and customization.

Cooperative Strategies

Cooperative strategies, such as joint ventures and co-opetition, involve collaboration between organizations to pursue mutual interests.

E-Business Strategies

E-business strategies focus on leveraging the internet for competitive advantage. This includes B2B and B2C strategies, which can involve IT and web portals to link organizations with customers and suppliers.

Strategic Portfolio Planning

For highly diversified firms, strategic portfolio planning, like the BCG matrix, helps allocate resources among different businesses based on market growth rate and market share.

Stars - are high-market-share businesses in high-growth markets. They produce large profits through substantial penetration of expanding markets. The preferred strategy for stars is growth and further resource investments in them are recommended.

Question marks - are low-market-share businesses in high-growth markets. They do not produce much profit, but they compete in rapidly growing markets. The preferred strategy is growth, but the risk exists that further investments will not result in improved market share. Only the most promising question marks should be targeted for growth; others are candidates for retrenchment by restructuring or divestiture.

Cash cows - are high-market-share businesses in low-growth markets. They produce large profits and a strong cash flow. Because the markets offer little growth opportunity, the preferred strategy is stability or modest growth. “Cows” should be “milked” to generate cash that can be used to support investments in stars and question marks.

Dogs – are low-market-share businesses in low-growth markets. They do not produce much profit, and they show little potential for future improvement. The preferred strategy for dogs is retrenchment by divestiture.
