Contingency planning is all about preparing for unexpected changes in circumstances. It's essential in today's uncertain world, just as scenario planning helps us envision multiple future scenarios and prepare for them.

Scenario planning is a long-term version of contingency planning. It involves identifying several possible future scenarios or states of affairs and then making plans to deal with each scenario should it actually occur. In this sense, scenario planning forces us to think really far ahead and be open to lots of possibilities.

Working in the cloud

The term ‘cloud computing’ also refers to the technology that makes cloud work. This includes some form of virtualized IT infrastructure—servers, operating system software, networking, and other infrastructure that’s abstracted, using special software, so that it can be pooled and divided irrespective of physical hardware boundaries. For example, a single hardware server can be divided into multiple virtual servers.

Scheduling flexibly

Flexible scheduling is an alternative to the traditional work schedule. Employees working under a flexible schedule can vary their arrival and departure time or choose or trade shifts for themselves.
