Leadership Traits and Behaviours

For centuries, people have recognized that some individuals excel as leaders while others do not. The question that still sparks debate is why. Historically, the issue of leadership success has been studied from the perspectives of trait, behavioral, and contingency approaches.

 Leadership Behaviors

Leadership Styles

Moving beyond early trait studies, researchers turned their attention to leader behaviors when dealing with followers. Leadership styles, recurring patterns of behaviors exhibited by leaders, became the focus of this research.

 

Stylized Quote

"Effective leaders exhibit various styles of leadership to match their followers' needs."

 

 Task-Oriented Leadership
A leader high in concern for the task focuses on planning, defining work, assigning responsibilities, setting clear standards, urging task completion, and monitoring performance.
People-Oriented Leadership

 A leader high in concern for people behaves warmly and supportively toward followers, maintains good social relations, respects their feelings, is sensitive to their needs, and shows trust in them.

Leadership traits and behaviors are critical elements in understanding what makes an effective leader. Whether rooted in personal qualities or observable actions, leadership encompasses a range of styles and approaches to match the needs of various situations and followers.
