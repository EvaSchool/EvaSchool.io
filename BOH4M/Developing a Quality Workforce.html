Orientation and Socialization

Orientation is a critical first step for newcomers in an organization. It sets the stage for their journey into the workplace. A good orientation program helps clarify the organization's mission and goals, explains its culture, and communicates important policies and procedures. It plays a significant role in socialization, which is the process of helping new members adapt to the organization's ways.

Weak or neglected orientation can result in informal socialization, where newcomers learn about the organization through casual interactions with coworkers.

Training and Development

Training is essential for both initial employee onboarding and continuous skill improvement. On-the-job training is a common approach, which can include job rotation, coaching, and mentoring. Mentoring, in particular, involves formal relationships between early-career employees and senior individuals who provide advice and guidance.

Performance Management

Performance management is a crucial aspect of human resource management. It involves setting performance standards and objectives, regularly assessing performance, and taking actions to improve future performance.

Performance appraisal serves both evaluation and development purposes. The evaluation aspect focuses on past performance and is used to measure results against standards. The development aspect looks to the future, identifying performance goals and potential obstacles.

Various methods are used for performance appraisal, including graphic rating scales, behaviorally anchored rating scales (BARS), and the critical incident technique. These methods aim to provide reliable and valid assessments of employee performance.

 

Multi-Person Comparisons

 

Some performance appraisals involve multi-person comparisons. This means formally comparing one person's performance with that of one or more others. Rank ordering is one approach, where all individuals are arranged in order of their performance achievement.

Another comprehensive approach is 360° feedback, which involves input from superiors, subordinates, peers, and even internal and external customers in the appraisal process. This well-rounded feedback can provide a holistic view of an employee's performance.
