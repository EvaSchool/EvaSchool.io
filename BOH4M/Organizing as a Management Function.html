Organizing as a Management Function

 

Organizing is a fundamental management function that involves arranging people and resources to achieve a common goal. Once plans are created, the manager's task is to ensure their successful execution. Organizing plays a central role in this process by clarifying jobs and working relationships.

What Is Organization Structure?

The organization structure refers to the formal arrangement of various parts within an organization. It encompasses tasks, workflows, reporting relationships, and communication channels that connect the work of individuals and groups.

Organization structure is like the blueprint that outlines how an organization's activities are organized.

Formal Structures

Formal structures are often represented by organization charts. These charts depict reporting relationships and the official arrangement of work positions within the organization.

Organization charts provide a visual representation of the formal hierarchy and communication lines.

Reading an organization chart allows you to understand key aspects of the formal structure, such as the division of work, supervisory relationships, communication channels, major subunits, and levels of management.

Informal Structures

 

Behind every formal structure lies an informal structure, which comprises unofficial but crucial working relationships among organizational members.

Informal structures represent the "human" side of an organization, where real interactions occur beyond formal titles and roles.

If the informal structure were to be diagrammed, it would reveal who interacts and collaborates regularly, regardless of their formal positions. Social network analysis is a tool used to identify these informal structures and social relationships within an organization.

Social network analysis helps uncover hidden connections and communication patterns that impact how work gets done.

In this analysis, people identify those they frequently seek help from, communicate with regularly, and engage with in various ways. Social networks are then visualized to illustrate the strength and type of relationships between individuals.
