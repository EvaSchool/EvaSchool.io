<Accordion.Section title="Human Resource Planning">

 
Human resource planning involves analyzing an organization's staffing needs, assessing the existing workforce, and determining future workforce requirements. It aligns with the organization's mission, objectives, and strategies. Key components of this process include job analysis, job descriptions, and job specifications.
</Accordion.Section>
<Accordion.Section title="Recruitment Techniques">
Recruitment aims to attract a qualified pool of job applicants. Effective recruitment involves advertising job vacancies, preliminary contact with potential candidates, and initial screening to create a pool of qualified applicants. Both external and internal recruitment methods have advantages and disadvantages.
</Accordion.Section>

<Accordion.Section title="Realistic Job Previews">
Traditional recruitment often focuses on selling the job and organization to applicants, potentially creating unrealistic expectations. Realistic job previews offer a balanced view of the job and organization, providing candidates with accurate information before accepting the job.
</Accordion.Section>

<Accordion.Section title="Selection Techniques">

 
The selection process aims to choose individuals from a pool of applicants who offer the greatest performance potential and fit with the organization. Steps in the selection process include applications, interviews, employment tests, reference and background checks, physical examinations, and final hiring decisions.
</Accordion.Section>
</Accordion>
