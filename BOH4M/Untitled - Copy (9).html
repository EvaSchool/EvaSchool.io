Issues in Leadership Development

In today's world, there is considerable interest in "super leaders" whose visions and charismatic personalities have an extraordinary impact on others. Charismatic leaders, as they are often called, possess special powers to inspire people in exceptional ways. While charisma was once thought to be a trait limited to a lucky few, it is now considered part of a broader set of personal leadership qualities that can be developed through foresight and practice.

Transformational Leadership 

Leadership scholars James MacGregor Burns and Bernard Bass suggest that the research and models discussed thus far tend to focus on transactional leadership. These models emphasize frameworks that can be systematically applied to achieve performance goals. However, these models often lack enthusiasm and emotion, which are qualities associated with charismatic leaders. Transformational leadership, as described by Burns and Bass, embodies inspiration and emotion. It describes leaders who are personally excited about their work and who inspire others to achieve extraordinary results.

Qualities of Transformational Leaders

Transformational leaders bring a strong sense of vision and contagious enthusiasm to their roles. They elevate followers' confidence, aspirations, and performance commitments through qualities such as:

Vision

Having clear ideas and direction, communicating them effectively, and fostering excitement about shared goals.

Charisma

Using personal charm and emotion to inspire enthusiasm, faith, loyalty, pride, and trust in others.

Symbolism

Identifying heroes and conducting ceremonies to celebrate excellence and achievements.

Empowerment

Assisting others by removing obstacles, sharing responsibilities, and delegating challenging tasks.

Intellectual Stimulation

Engaging others by raising awareness of problems and stimulating their imaginations.

Integrity

Demonstrating honesty, credibility, consistency in values, and commitment to promises.

Emotional Intelligence and Leadership

Emotional intelligence (EI) plays a significant role in leadership development. Daniel Goleman defines EI as "the ability to manage ourselves and our relationships effectively." Goleman's research links emotional intelligence to leadership effectiveness, particularly in senior management positions. He believes that emotional intelligence skills can be cultivated and focuses on a core set of EI competencies.

EI Competencies for Leaders

Self-awareness

Understanding one's moods and emotions and their impact on work and others.

Self-regulation

Effectively managing emotions and behaviors.

Empathy

Recognizing and understanding others' emotions.

Social Skills

Managing relationships, inspiring and influencing others positively.

Gender and Leadership

Are there inherent gender differences in leadership? Research largely supports the gender similarities hypothesis, indicating that males and females share psychological properties. It is well-established that both women and men can be equally effective leaders. However, it's crucial to avoid falling into stereotypes that limit leadership based on gender.

Interactive Leadership

Rather than focusing on gender differences, it may be more productive to embrace the concept of interactive leadership. An interactive leadership style, which emphasizes collaboration and adaptability, aligns well with the needs of modern organizations and workers. Both men and women can excel in this style.

Moral leadership begins with personal integrity, a fundamental concept in transformational leadership. Leaders with integrity are honest, credible, and consistent in aligning their actions with their values. In high-pressure work environments, leadership anchored in personal integrity is indispensable. Making difficult decisions becomes more manageable when guided by what is ethically right.

Drucker's "Old-Fashioned" Leadership

Management consultant Peter Drucker advocated a pragmatic view of leadership. He believed that successful leadership is based on hard work and responsibility, not just rank. Good leaders surround themselves with talented individuals and earn and maintain the trust of others. Personal integrity is key to establishing and retaining the trust of followers.

Issues in leadership development encompass various aspects, from transformational leadership and emotional intelligence to the role of gender and personal integrity. Effective leaders combine these elements to inspire and guide their teams toward success.
