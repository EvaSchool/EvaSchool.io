Managerial Performance

Managers play a critical role in organizations by helping people perform effectively. They are accountable for results in their areas of responsibility. Let's explore what constitutes managerial performance.

Accountability

Continue Learning

Accountability is the requirement for one person to answer to a higher authority for performance results in their area of work responsibility.

Effectiveness

Effective managers help others achieve high performance and satisfaction in their work, introducing the concept of quality of work life (QWL).

Quality of Work Life (QWL)

Continue Learning

QWL is an indicator of the overall quality of human experiences in the workplace. It includes factors like fair pay, safe conditions, career opportunities, and pride in the organization.

Changing Nature of Managerial Work

Managerial work is evolving from "directing" and "order giving" to "helping" and "supporting." The role of a manager is shifting towards coordination, coaching, and team leadership.

Employee Engagement

Engaging employees in decision-making and opportunity development is crucial. Involving employees in program development creates a strong foundation for success.

Traditional Pyramid

In traditional organizations, managers are often seen as top-level authorities directing the workforce.

Upside-Down Pyramid

The upside-down pyramid view places operating workers at the top, supported by managers who mobilize and deliver the necessary support for optimal performance.

Customer-Centric

The entire organization is devoted to serving customers, and managers' roles are to support workers in this mission.
