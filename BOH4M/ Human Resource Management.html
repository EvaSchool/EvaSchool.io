<Accordion.Section title="Human Resource Management Basics">
Human Resource Management (HRM) revolves around attracting, developing, and retaining a skilled and motivated workforce. An organization's long-term success hinges on its ability to excel in these areas.
HRM's goal is to enhance organizational performance by ensuring that capable and enthusiastic individuals are in the right roles, and well-supported for success. Three key responsibilities of HRM include attracting, developing, and maintaining a quality workforce.
</Accordion.Section>

<Accordion.Section title="Global Human Resource Management">

 
Managing a global workforce presents unique challenges. Companies like IBM and Lenovo adopt strategies such as "global integration" and "world sourcing" to find talent worldwide. Keeping track of expertise and hiring the best workers globally are critical endeavors.
</Accordion.Section>

<Accordion.Section title="Legal Environment of HRM">

 
The legal environment in HRM is crucial for ensuring diversity and inclusivity. Laws against employment discrimination, like the Canadian Human Rights Act and the Employment Equity Act, prohibit discrimination based on various characteristics. However, criticisms of employment equity revolve around concerns of reverse discrimination.

HR professionals must stay informed about dynamic legal issues, such as sexual harassment, pregnancy discrimination, the legal status of part-time workers and independent contractors, and workplace privacy.
</Accordion.Section>
