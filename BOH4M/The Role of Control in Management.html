  • Playing sports like golf or tennis.

Through these activities, you set objectives, constantly assess your progress, and make adjustments to achieve your goals—elements of effective control.

The Importance of Controlling

Control is all about measuring performance and taking action to ensure desired outcomes. Its fundamental purpose is to guarantee that plans are executed, and that real performance aligns with or surpasses objectives.

 

 "The foundation of control is information."

Stylized Quote

Control fits into the management process alongside planning, organizing, and leading. It ensures that the right things happen at the right time, in the right way, and helps maintain consistency with organizational objectives.

 

Types of Controls

Stylized Quote

In the realm of control, different types serve various phases of the input-throughput-output cycle, following the open-systems perspective.

 

  

Feedforward Controls (Preliminary Controls):

  • Implemented before work begins.
  • Ensures clarity of objectives and availability of necessary resources.
  • Aims to prevent problems by addressing the question: "What needs to be done before we begin?"

    Concurrent Controls (Steering Controls):

    Continue Learning
    • Focus on monitoring activities during the work process.
    • Solves issues as they arise, aiming to improve things in real time.
    • Asks: "What can we do to improve things right now?"

      Feedback Controls (Post-Action Controls):

      Continue Learning
      • Applied after work is completed.
      • Evaluate the quality of outcomes.
      • Primarily reactive, addressing how to enhance future performance.

         

        Each type of control plays a critical role in ensuring high performance and managing potential challenges.

        Internal and External Controls

        Managers have two primary options concerning control systems: internal and external.

        Internal Control:

        • Leverages individuals' capacity for self-discipline.
        • Encourages self-control, empowerment, and participation.
        • Supports the exercise of self-discipline in performing tasks.

        Bureaucratic Control:

        • An external control method that utilizes authority, policies, procedures, and supervision.
        • Ensures alignment with organizational interests.
        • Often involves hierarchy and authority.

        Clan Control:

        • Influences behavior through organizational culture, norms, and shared values.
        • Collective identity fosters consistent behavior patterns.
        • Reflects the power of culture in guiding behavior.

        Market Control:

        • The influence of market competition on organizational and individual behavior.
        • Encourages organizations to adjust practices based on customer feedback and competitors' actions.
        • Driven by the dynamics of staying competitive in the marketplace.

         

        Effective control often involves a blend of these options, adapting to specific organizational needs and contexts.
