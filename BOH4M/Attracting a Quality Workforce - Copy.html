Human Resource Planning 

Human resource planning is the process of analyzing an organization's staffing needs and determining how to best fill them. It identifies staffing needs, assesses the existing workforce, and determines what additions or replacements are required for the future. This process becomes strategic when done in reference to the organizational mission, objectives, and strategies.

The foundations for human resource planning include job analysis - the orderly study of job facts to determine what is done when, where, how, why, and by whom. The job analysis provides information that can then be used to write or update job descriptions and create job specifications.

External vs. Internal Recruitment

External and Internal Recruitment External recruitment seeks job candidates from outside the hiring organization, often through job advertisements and external networks. This approach brings in outsiders with fresh perspectives and expertise.

Internal recruitment, on the other hand, seeks applicants from inside the organization. It is usually less expensive and deals with individuals whose performance records are well-known within the organization.

Realistic Job Previews 

Realistic Job Previews Traditional recruitment often focuses on selling the positive aspects of a job and the organization, sometimes concealing potential negatives. This can lead to disillusionment and costly turnover when new hires discover the realities of the job.

Realistic job previews aim to provide candidates with a balanced and open view of the job and the organization, including both positive and challenging aspects. It allows candidates to make informed decisions before accepting a job offer.

Selection Techniques

The process of selection involves choosing the best-fit candidates from a pool of applicants. It's crucial to ensure a good fit between the new hire and the organization.

Selection techniques include:

  • Applications and Interviews: These provide an opportunity for both the applicant and the employer to learn about each other.

  • Employment Tests: Designed to assess various attributes like intelligence, aptitudes, personality, interests, and ethics.
  • Reference and Background Checks: Important for verifying qualifications and work history.

  • Physical Examinations: Ensuring physical capability to perform job requirements.

The best selection decisions often involve extensive consultation among various stakeholders, emphasizing the candidate's capacity to perform well.
