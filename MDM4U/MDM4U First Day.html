MDM4U, Data Management

Welcome to MDM4U!

This course broadens students’ understanding of mathematics as it relates to managing information. Students will apply methods for organizing and analyzing large amounts of information; solve problems involving probability and statistics; and carry out a culminating project that integrates statistical concepts and skills.

Throughout the course, students will develop skills in data collection, representation, and interpretation. They will explore the relevance of data management to real-world applications and enhance their ability to think critically, reason logically, and communicate effectively.

Prerequisite: MCR3U (Functions) or MCF3M (Functions and Applications), as specified by the Ontario Ministry of Education

This course was designed with your growth in mind. The goal is to promote thinking over memorization and emphasize real-world application. Data management is a critical skill across many disciplines—from business to science—and will help you become a more informed decision-maker.

Engage actively with the content, explore problems from different perspectives, and apply your reasoning to real-life scenarios. Don't be afraid to make mistakes—learning from them is part of the process. Be curious, be analytical, and stay open to feedback and improvement.
