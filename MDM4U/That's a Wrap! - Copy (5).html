That's a Wrap!

Learning Goals

  • Distinguish between discrete and continuous variables.

  • Make models of probability distribution using frequency tables, histograms, and polygons.

  • Determine the theoretical probability for a continuous random variable over an interval.

  • Calculate z-scores.

  • Use technology to investigate the properties of normal distributions.

  • Analyze these properties to determine probabilities.

  • Determine the upper and lower limits of confidence intervals.

  • Define the margin of error.

  • Make connections between normal, binomial, and hypergeometric distributions.

Success Criteria

I can ...

  • Differentiate between discrete and continuous variables.
  • Make models of probability distribution with the use of frequency tables, histograms, and polygons.
  • Determine the theoretical probability for a continuous random variable over an interval.
  • Solve for z-scores.
  • Investigate the properties of normal distributions by utilizing technology.
  • Analyze these properties to determine probabilities.
  • Determine the upper and lower limits of confidence intervals.
  • Describe the margin of errors.
  • Form connections between normal, binomial, and hypergeometric distributions.
