Confidence Intervals

Confidence Level

  • the probability that a particular statistic is within the range indicated by the margin of error.
  • commonly used confidence levels are 90%, 95%, and 99% error.
  • the range of possible values of the measured statistic at a particular confidence level.

The margin of Error

the range of values that a particular measurement is said to be within.
• the smaller the margin of error, the greater the accuracy of the measurement.

Example

A researcher might summarize the findings as “Lake trout make up 20.4% of the fish population in Lake Lavieille. This estimate is considered correct within ±3.0%, 19 times out of 20.” What does this statement mean?

Suppose the researcher samples the fish in the lake 20 times. The researcher is confident that the percentage of lake trout found in the samples will be within 3% of 20.4% in 19 out of the 20 samples. 

The margin of error is 3% above or below the mean.

The confidence interval range is
20.4% - 3% = 17.4% to 20.4% + 3% = 23.4%

The confidence level is 19 out of 20, or 95%.
