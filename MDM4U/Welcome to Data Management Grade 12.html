Welcome to Data Management Grade 12

This course broadens students’ understanding of mathematics as it relates to managing data. Students will apply methods for organizing and analysing large amounts of information, solve problems involving probability and statistics; and carry out a culminating investigation that integrates statistical concepts and skills. Students will also refine their use of the mathematical processes necessary for success in senior mathematics. Students planning to enter university programs in business, the social sciences, and the humanities will find this course of particular interest.


Unit Title

Description

Counting and Probability 

In this unit, students will learn the vocabulary and notation associated with probability through various contexts. These include basic counting methods, understanding permutations and combinations, employing mathematical notation to represent permutations and combinations, tackling counting problems, and addressing probability issues by applying counting principles.

Probability Distributions Introduction 

In this unit, students will gain an understanding of probability distributions associated with discrete random variables. They will investigate and establish connections between Binomial and Hypergeometric distributions. Additionally, students will learn to identify that variations between a probability histogram and a frequency histogram can arise due to variability.

Introduction to Organization of Data Analysis 

In this unit, students will focus on organizing data for analysis, demonstrating their understanding of the significance of data in statistical studies. They will also describe the characteristics of a quality sample and compare various sampling techniques. Students will design effective surveys, collect data, and understand data organization. Additionally, they will find data sources, refine their topic of interest, and develop a plan in preparation for the Culminating Investigation.
Statistical Analysis 

In this unit, students will investigate and analyze data with one variable, as well as explore, examine, and conclude findings from data with two variables. They will assess the reliability of statistical summaries and engage in a culminating research project, where they will analyze, interpret, conclude, and draft a report on their study. Additionally, students will summarize and present their findings, and evaluate and critique the presentations of their peers.
