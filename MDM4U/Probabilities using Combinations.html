Probabilities using Combinations

Concept

You can sometimes use combinations or Pascal’s triangle to determine probabilities.

The numerator, n(A), represents the number of successful outcomes, usually involving restrictions.

The denominator, n(S), represents the total number of outcomes, with no restrictions.

Example

A scratch-and-win contest at a store allows you to scratch five numbers. If all of your numbers match the winning set of five numbers, chosen from 1 to 25 without regard to order, you win the grand prize.

a) What is the probability of winning the grand prize?

b) To win the second prize, four of the five winning numbers must match. What is the probability of winning the second prize?

Example

In the game of Plinko, a disc is dropped into a slot at the top of a board. When it hits a peg, it falls to the left or right as it travels down the board. State the probability of the disc ending up in each slot at the bottom of the board.
