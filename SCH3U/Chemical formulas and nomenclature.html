Chemical Formulas and Nomenclature

  • Throughout history people have experienced compounds and had to call them something based on their own experiences. That is where these “common names” come from. They do not tell us about what makes up the compound.
  • Take water for instance does this name tell you about the Hydrogen or the Oxygen in the compound or how much of each is there?

 

Naming Compounds Hydrated salts Molecular Compounds

  • And Copper(II) chloride 
  • If the salt has water with it (still a solid!!!) we add that to the end of the name.
  • The word “Hydrate” tells us there is water present and how much is given by a prefix.
