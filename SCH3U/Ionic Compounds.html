Ionic Compounds

  

  • Ionic compounds are made up of Cations and Anions which are held together by ionic bonds.
  • Remember cations and anions are made by electron transfer (one gives and one accepts). This transfer is known as a reaction.

Properties of ionic solids
  • They are hard and brittle.
  • They have relatively high melting and boiling points. For example, sodium chloride melts at 801 °C.
  • They conduct electricity as molten liquids but not as solids.
  • They conduct electricity when dissolved in water.
