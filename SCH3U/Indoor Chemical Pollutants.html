Indoor Chemical Pollutants

Indoor chemical pollutants are mostly gases, but some are in the form of particulates.Particulate matter is produced in home offices by printers and copiers. It also results from combustion in fireplaces and wood-burning stoves, and from lit candles and cigarettes. 

Pollutants can also get into indoor air through an unintentional phenomenon called off -gassing. Off-gassing is the release of gases from a substance at room temperature. You might notice that a new shower curtain has a smell for a few days. This is an example of off -gassing.

Methanal

a VOC, is a colourless, flammable gas with a distinctive sharp odour. Low levels of this compound are quite common in our indoor air. It can enter the air by two methods: from off -gassing and from combustion—including cigarettes. 

The health risks associated with methanal are related to length of exposure and individual sensitivity. Very high concentrations of methanal are linked to an increased risk of cancer in the nasal cavity.
