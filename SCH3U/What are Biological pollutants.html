What are Biological pollutants?

Biological pollutants are living organisms or the products of living organisms. Many of these organisms thrive in the warm environments of our homes. Bacteria and viruses that cause infectious diseases, such as chicken pox and influenza, are biological pollutants. Other biological pollutants include mites, mould, dust, dander, and pollen. Fecal matter from pets, cockroaches, and rodents can also dry and become airborne.

Even humans are a source of biological pollutants. We each shed thousands of skin cells every minute. These cells are a major component of dust. This organic matter is a food source for dust mites. Dust mites and their waste are among the most common household allergens.
