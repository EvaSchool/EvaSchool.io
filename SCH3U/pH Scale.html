pH Scale

  • Living things can tolerate only small changes in pH. Most freshwater fish, for example, thrive in a pH range of 5.5 to 7.5, depending on the species.
  • “Clean” rain can have a pH of about 5.6. Rain is naturally acidic due to the formation of some carbonic acid.
  • acidic environments can be beneficial. For example, the outer covering of human hair is called the cuticle and consists of tiny overlapping plates.
  • Washing hair with a slightly acidic shampoo or a lemon rinse causes these plates of the cuticle to close tightly around the shaft of hair. This makes each hair strand smoother and less likely to tangle with its neighbours. Most shampoos are slightly acidic for this reason.
