Dipole-Dipole Forces

In general, dipole–dipole forces are relatively strong intermolecular forces. They occur between all polar molecules. We can hypothesize that the more polar a substance is, the stronger the dipole–dipole force of attraction that exists.

London Dispersion Force

London forces exist between all molecules, even polar molecules. In a polar compound, however, the London forces are insignificant compared to the much stronger dipole–dipole forces. Chemists now class dipole–dipole forces and London dispersion forces as van der Waals forces in honor of the scientist who first proposed their existence.

an unusually strong dipole-dipole force between a hydrogen atom attached to a highly electronegative atom (N, O, or F) and a highly electronegative atom in another molecule

Hydrogen Bond

There are two main reasons for hydrogen bonding. First is the large difference in electronegativities. Second is the small size of the hydrogen atom, which means that the positive pole is highly concentrated. It strongly attracts the negative pole of a nearby molecule.
