Atomic Structure

Over time our understanding of what makes up atoms has changed! At first, we thought that atoms were the smallest building blocks and were indivisible.

Later electrons (negative charge) were discovered (1897) by Thomson and were 1000 times smaller (in mass) than Hydrogen.

In 1909 Rutherford discovered the Nucleus in which it was assumed that the space between atoms in a material is mostly empty and there are heavy parts distributed in space. In 1920 he said positive particles existed in the material surrounded by mostly empty space!?

Bohr’s Proposal of Energy Levels

This Planetary model allows us to represent atoms in a systematic way.
The allowed orbits are called energy levels and the further away from the nucleus you are the higher your energy is. Electrons in the same orbit have similar energies. (known as degeneracy).
The nucleus is made up of protons (positive) and neutrons (have no charge) but they weigh a similar amount. Electrons are about 1000 times smaller than these and orbit around the nucleus.
Electrons can move in between these energy levels (transitions) and when they return back to their original position the energy difference is released as light (x-ray is high enough energy difference).   

Atomic number, Atomic mass

The atomic number is the number of protons in the Nucleus. Normally “Z” is the symbol. It is the position of the element on the periodic table for example Cl is element 17 and that is “Z”.
Atoms are electronically neutral therefore the plus and minus charges are equal in number so the number of electrons in a neutral atom is equal to “Z”
