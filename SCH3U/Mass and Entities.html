Mass and Entities

Determining the Number of Entities in Compound 

The number of atoms of an element in a sample of molecules is determined by multiplying the number of molecules by the number of atoms of the element per molecule.

Even the smallest visible drop of water contains more molecules than you can count in a lifetime! Because molecules are so small, chemists use mass as a convenient way to estimate the number of molecules in a sample.

 

Chemists know that water has a molar mass of 18.0 g/mol. Therefore, they know that 18.0 g of water contains 1.00 mol or 6.02 × 1023 molecules of water
