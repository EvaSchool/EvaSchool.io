Ions and Octet Rule

The Octet Rule

Octet is a work we have heard in mathematics. Basically, energy levels (shells) outside of the first one want 8 electrons in them. (first wants 2 electrons).

This leads us to say the outermost shell when bonding wants eight electrons (whether electrons are gained or lost). This loss or gain of electrons leads to the formation of ions (charged atoms/molecules).

Ions: Cations

Neutral atom: Z and electron count are the same.  Take Na for example Z = 11.  There are also 11 electrons in this atom. Assume A = 23
