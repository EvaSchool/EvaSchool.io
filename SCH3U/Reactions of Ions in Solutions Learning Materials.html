Total ionic equation

- Chemical equation in which all highly soluble ionic compounds are written as dissociated ions.

Like all chemical equations, a total ionic equation must be balanced. To be balanced, the equation must meet two important criteria:

1. All entities on one side of the equation must be accounted for on the other.

2. The net electrical charge on both sides of the equation must be the same.
