What is an Ideal Gas?

A hypothetical gas composed of particles that have no size, travel in straight lines, and have no attraction to each other (no intermolecular forces).

In order to develop the ideal gas law, we will need to consider Charles’ law, Avogadro’s law, and Boyle’s law as mathematical relationships:

These three relationships show that the volume of a gas depends on the temperature, pressure, and amount of gas present.

 

The constant that turns all of the individual gas laws into a single equation is known as the universal gas constant. It is represented in the equation as R.

The ideal gas law states that the product of the pressure and volume of a gas is equal to the product of the amount of gas, the universal gas constant, and the absolute temperature.
