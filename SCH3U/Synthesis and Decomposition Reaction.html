SYNTHESIS REACTIONS INVOLVING HYDROGEN

Hydrogen is unusual for a Group 1 element. Most Group 1 elements form IONIC COMPOUNDS when they bond with other elements. Hydrogen, however, usually forms MOLECULAR COMPOUNDS. Molecular compounds are made up of atoms, not ions. However, ionic charges are still useful in predicting the products of synthesis reactions involving hydrogen.

Synthesis Reactions Involving Compounds

COMPOUNDS also can participate in synthesis reactions. For example, when carbon dioxide is bubbled into water the two compounds react to form a new substance. The new substance is an acid called carbonic acid.

Bromothymol blue is an example of an acid–base indicator. It is blue in a basic solution and yellow in an acidic solution. When carbon dioxide reacts with water containing bromothymol blue, the mixture turns from blue to yellow, showing that an acid has formed (Figure 6).
