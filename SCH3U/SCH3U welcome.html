Course Outline

Course Overview

This course enables students to deepen their understanding of chemistry through the study of matter, chemical bonding, chemical reactions, solutions and solubility, and gases and atmospheric chemistry. Students will develop scientific investigation skills in the laboratory and in other settings, and will relate their studies to real-world applications. Emphasis will be placed on the importance of chemistry in daily life and on its impact on society and the environment.

Prerequisite Courses Required

Science, Grade 10, Academic (SNC2D)

With regards to Scientific Investigation Skills, Students will:

  • Demonstrate safe and accurate laboratory techniques;
  • Formulate questions and hypotheses based on observations;
  • Plan and conduct investigations to test hypotheses;
  • Analyze and interpret data, and communicate results clearly;

With regards to Matter, Students will:

  • Describe the structure of atoms, ions, and molecules;
  • Explain periodic trends in the periodic table;
  • Relate atomic structure to the properties and behaviour of elements;

With regards to Chemical Bonding, Students will:

  • Describe ionic, covalent, and polar covalent bonds;
  • Predict molecular shapes and polarity using bonding theories;
  • Relate bonding to physical and chemical properties of substances;

With regards to Chemical Reactions, Students will:

  • Classify and balance different types of chemical reactions;
  • Use stoichiometry to calculate quantities in chemical reactions;
  • Analyze energy changes in chemical reactions;

With regards to Solutions and Solubility, Students will:

  • Describe properties of solutions and factors affecting solubility;
  • Perform calculations involving concentration and dilution;
  • Analyze environmental impacts of solutions and solubility;

With regards to Gases and Atmospheric Chemistry, Students will:

  • Describe the gas laws and apply them to problem-solving;
  • Analyze the behaviour of gases in various conditions;
  • Discuss the role of gases in environmental and atmospheric processes;

Units of Study:

  • Unit One: Scientific Investigation Skills and Career Exploration
  • Unit Two: Matter, Chemical Bonding, and Chemical Trends
  • Unit Three: Chemical Reactions
  • Unit Four: Solutions and Solubility
  • Unit Five: Gases and Atmospheric Chemistry
