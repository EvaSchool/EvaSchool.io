Human Population Dynamics Human Population Past and Present Part 1

Overall Expectations

F1. analyze the relationships between population growth, personal consumption, technological development, and our ecological footprint, and assess the effectiveness of some Canadian initiatives intended to assist expanding populations.

F2. investigate the characteristics of population growth and use models to calculate the growth of populations within an ecosystem. 

F3. demonstrate an understanding of concepts related to population growth and explain the factors that affect the growth of various populations of species.

Specific Expectations

F1.1 analyzes the effects of human population growth, personal consumption, and technological development on our ecological footprint.

F2.1 uses appropriate terminology related to population dynamics, including, but not limited to carrying capacity, population growth, population cycle, fecundity, and mortality.

F2.3 determines, through laboratory inquiry or using computer simulations, the characteristics of population growth of two different populations (e.g., the different population cycles of a predator and its prey; the population cycles of two populations that compete for food; the increase of Aboriginal compared to non-Aboriginal populations and the significant difference in average age between the two groups) [PR, AI, C].

F3.4 explains the concept of energy transfer in a human population in terms of the flow of food energy in the production, distribution, and use of food resources. 

Learning Goals

  • Students will analyze the effects of human population growth, personal consumption, and technological development on our ecological footprint.

  • Students will use appropriate terminology related to population dynamics, including, but not limited to: carrying capacity, population growth, population cycle, fecundity, and mortality.

  • To have an overview of a brief history of human population growth.

  • To analyze how a growing population leads to the spread of disease.

  • To understand the age structure of the Population.
