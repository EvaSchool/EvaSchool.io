Wrap Up

Mathematical models and graphs help ecologists predict and monitor trends in populations.

  • Exponential growth and geometric growth models describe population growth when resources are assumed to be unlimited.
  • There is an upper limit to the number of individuals that most environments can support. This upper limit is known as the carrying capacity of the population.
  • The logistic growth model assumes that the population growth rate cannot be positive when the population size exceeds its carrying capacity.

Populations are dynamic. The growth of a population is limited by the presence or absence of factors that are necessary for survival.

  • Density-dependent factors limit the growth of a population and are dependent on population density.
  • Density-independent factors limit the growth of a population regardless of population density.
