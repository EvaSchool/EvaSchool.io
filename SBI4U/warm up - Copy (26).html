Warm up

Do you know?

In multicellular organisms, individual cells cannot effectively secrete wastes to the external environment. One of the main functions of the excretory system (aided by osmoregulation) is to concentrate wastes and expel them from the body. The second main function of the excretory system is to regulate fluids and water within the body. 

Most metabolic wastes and toxins are dissolved in the body’s internal environment, so maintenance of the body fluids is essential for keeping the body free of waste products and enabling it to function properly. In this section, you will learn about the functions of the excretory system and examine how the excretory system works in simpler organisms and in more complex organisms, such as humans and other mammals.
