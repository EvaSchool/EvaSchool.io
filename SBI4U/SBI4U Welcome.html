Course Outline

Course Overview

This course provides students with the opportunity for in-depth study of the concepts and processes that occur in biological systems. Students will study biochemistry, metabolic processes, molecular genetics, homeostasis, and population dynamics. Through laboratory work, research, and analysis, students will develop scientific investigation skills and apply biological knowledge to contemporary issues in science, technology, and society. This course is intended for students planning to study the life sciences at the postsecondary level.

Prerequisite Courses Required

Biology, Grade 11, University Preparation (SBI3U)

With regards to Biochemistry, Students will:

  • Analyze the molecular structure and function of carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids;
  • Explain the role of enzymes in biochemical reactions and factors affecting enzyme activity;
  • Relate biochemical principles to human health and biotechnology applications;

With regards to Metabolic Processes, Students will:

  • Explain energy transformations in cellular processes, including photosynthesis and cellular respiration;
  • Analyze the roles of enzymes, coenzymes, and other molecules in metabolic pathways;
  • Evaluate the impact of metabolic processes on organism health and environmental systems;

With regards to Molecular Genetics, Students will:

  • Describe DNA structure, replication, and repair mechanisms;
  • Explain transcription, translation, and gene regulation;
  • Analyze the role of biotechnology in genetic research and its ethical implications;

With regards to Homeostasis, Students will:

  • Explain mechanisms of homeostasis in animals, including hormonal and nervous system regulation;
  • Analyze the effects of environmental factors and diseases on homeostatic balance;
  • Relate homeostasis to human health and medical treatments;

With regards to Population Dynamics, Students will:

  • Analyze factors affecting population growth and decline in ecosystems;
  • Interpret population data and use models to predict changes;
  • Evaluate the effects of human activity on population sustainability and biodiversity;

Units of Study:

  • Unit 1: Biochemistry
  • Unit 2: Metabolic Processes
  • Unit 3: Molecular Genetics
  • Unit 4: Homeostasis
  • Unit 5: Population Dynamics
