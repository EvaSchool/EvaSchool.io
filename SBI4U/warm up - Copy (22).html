Warm-up

The emergence of DNA evidence in the courtroom, several decades ago, has transformed the criminal justice system. 

DNA evidence collected at crime scenes and from possible suspects has proven to be a powerful tool for police. 

Investigators have used DNA evidence to solve decades-old cold cases and exonerate innocent people who had been convicted.

In June 2000, the National DNA Data Bank was founded in Canada. 

It is maintained by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police in Ottawa.

It is used to preserve the DNA of all known convicted criminals (about 200,000 individuals) as well as 65,000 DNA evidence samples found at crime scenes. 

Investigators can compare DNA from a new crime scene with DNA from the data bank to see if they can find a match. 

As well, Canadian DNA data are shared with other police forces around the world.
