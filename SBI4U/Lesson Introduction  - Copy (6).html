Introduction to Metabolism and Energy 

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B1. analyze the effects of various human activities on the diversity of living things.

 

Specific Expectations

B3.2 compare and contrast the structure and function of different types of prokaryotes, eukaryotes, and viruses (e.g., compare and contrast genetic material, metabolism, organelles, and other cell parts)

Learning Goals

  • Describe the importance of energy in biological systems to do work.
  • Describe how the first law of thermodynamics relates to energy conversions form potential energy in chemical bonds.
  • Describe how the second law of thermodynamics determines if reactions can occur spontaneously, and how free energy can drive a reaction forward or be released for use in a system.

Success Criteria

  • Understand the importance of energy in biological systems to do work.
  • Explain how the first law of thermodynamics relates to energy conversions from potential energy in chemical bonds.
  • Explain how the second law of thermodynamics determines if reactions can occur spontaneously, and how free energy can drive a reaction forward or be released for use in a system.
