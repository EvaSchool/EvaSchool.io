What is cell division?

All cells are produced by the divisions of pre-existing cells. 

Continuity of life depends on one cell's division 

Cell division is the process by which the formation of two or more daughter cells from a single mother cell takes place is called cell division.

Why do cells divide?

1 . For growth:  All plants and animals begin their life as a single cell. This cells Divides fast repeatedly to form clusters of cells of different shapes To form tissues and organs that perform different functions.  Thus, Cell division is essential for growth.

2 . For repair:   When there are accidental injuries, one may get cuts in the skin or fractures in the bone. The cells divide to cover up the Gaps and joint broken ends bring about the repair of such injuries.

3. For replacement of worn-out cells: 20 million red blood cells in our body are destroyed every minute. These are replaced by new cells through the division of their parent cell in the bone marrow. Old and dried leaves in plants fall off and new ones grow as a result of cell division.

Skin cells forms form a vital protective layer that is constantly replaced as the dead cells on the surface wear away.

Red blood cells have a lifespan of between 100 and 120 days Although they do not divide by themselves, they are produced in specialized tissue of our bone marrow at an amazing rate of About 200 billion new cells every day.

Other cells rarely divide. For example, neurons in the brain and throughout the body rarely divide or replace themselves.

The human body can replace 20 million cells in just in few Seconds, which means over a lifetime, your body produces A lot of hair, blood, and skin.

                              

2 types of Nucleic Acids 

DNA >>>> Deoxyribonucleic acid >>>> stores hereditary information >>>> responsible for inherited traits in all eukaryotes and prokaryotes and many viruses

Nucleic Acid – A blueprint for proteins that are synthesized in cells; stores hereditary information

RNA >>>> Ribonucleic acid >>>> hereditary molecule of some viruses >>>> some involved in protein synthesis in all cells

All cells whether it is animal cells, plant cells, fungal cells, protists, archaea, or bacteria use some molecules of deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) to carry information from the parent cell to the daughter cell. For instance, some viruses may have RNA as genetic material, while others have DNA as genetic material. Apart from being responsible for the inheritance of genetic information in all living beings, DNA also plays a crucial role in the production of proteins.

Discovery of DNA

The shape of DNA molecule was studied by Rosalind Franklin in 1953 and the structure was finally worked out by Watson and Crick in the same year.

Structure of DNA

DNA contains a special, coded genetic program with detailed and specific instructions for each organism’s heredity.

Both DNA and RNA are polymers of repeating units called Nucleotides 

Nucleotide – The building block of nucleic acids >>>> consists of a 5-carbon sugar, a nitrogenous base, and 1-3 phosphate groups

2 general types of nitrogenous bases:

Pyrimidines and Purines

Both have high nitrogen content

Pyrimidines >>>> single organic rings >>>> 3 bases are uracil (U), thymine (T), and cytosine (C)

Purines >>>> 2 ringed organic structures >>>> 2 bases are adenine (A) and guanine (G)

DNA and RNA: Nucleotide Polymers

Phosphodiester Bond >>>> Link that is formed between nucleotides by a phosphate bridge

Arrangement of alternating sugar and phosphate groups >>>> forms the backbone of a nucleic acid chain

DNA Chain >>>> nucleotide >>>> deoxyribose, a phosphate group, one of the four bases A, T, G, or C

RNA Chain >>>> nucleotide >>>> ribose, a phosphate group, one of the four bases A, U, G, or C
