SBI4U, Biology, Grade 12 University Preparation

Welcome to SBI4U!

Welcome to SBI4U! This course will help you develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and processes that occur in living systems. You will explore biological structures and functions at the molecular, cellular, and systemic levels, and examine the interrelationships between organisms and their environments. This course is designed to prepare you for postsecondary studies in the life sciences, health sciences, or related fields.

In this course, you will study biochemistry, metabolic processes, molecular genetics, homeostasis, and population dynamics. You will also conduct laboratory investigations, research current biological issues, and analyze scientific data to develop and communicate evidence-based conclusions.

Prerequisite: Biology, Grade 11, University Preparation (SBI3U).

SBI4U will challenge you to think critically about complex biological systems and the applications of biological knowledge in modern society. Through practical experiments, projects, and independent research, you will strengthen your skills in scientific investigation, data analysis, and communication.

To succeed in this course, stay organized, complete assignments on time, and actively participate in discussions and lab activities. Biology is a cumulative subject—maintaining consistent study habits will help you master the material.

What You’ll Learn

  • Biochemistry: Structure and function of biological molecules, enzyme activity, and metabolic pathways.
  • Metabolic Processes: Cellular respiration, photosynthesis, and energy transfer in living systems.
  • Molecular Genetics: DNA structure and replication, protein synthesis, gene regulation, and biotechnology.
  • Homeostasis: Mechanisms of maintaining balance in biological systems, including hormonal and nervous system control.
  • Population Dynamics: Ecological interactions, species distribution, and the impact of human activity on ecosystems.
