DNA Structure and Function: A History and The Elucidation of the Structure of DNA part 1

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will: 

D1. analyze some of the social, ethical, and legal issues associated with genetic research and biotechnology.

D3. demonstrate an understanding of concepts, processes, and technologies related to the transmission of hereditary characteristics.

 

Specific Expectations

D3.2 explains the concepts of DNA, genes, chromosomes, alleles, mitosis, and meiosis, and how they account for the transmission of hereditary characteristics according to Mendelian laws of inheritance.

D3.5 describe some reproductive technologies (e.g., cloning, artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, recombinant DNA), and explain how their use can increase the genetic diversity of a species (e.g., farm animals, crops).

D3.7 describes, on the basis of research, some of the historical scientific contributions that have advanced our understanding of molecular genetics (e.g., discoveries made by Frederick Griffith, Watson and Crick, Hershey and Chase).

Learning Goals

  • Students will be able to explain the concepts of DNA, genes, chromosomes, alleles, mitosis, and meiosis, and how they account for the transmission of hereditary characteristics according to Mendelian laws of inheritance.

  • Students will be able to describe some reproductive technologies (e.g., cloning, artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, recombinant DNA), and explain how their use can increase the genetic diversity of a species (e.g., farm animals, crops).

  • Students will be able to describe, on the basis of research, some of the historical scientific contributions that have advanced our understanding of molecular genetics (e.g., discoveries made by Frederick Griffith, Watson and Crick, Hershey and Chase).
