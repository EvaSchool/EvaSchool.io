Introduction to DNA Replication, Repair and Aging Part 1

Overall Expectations

D3. demonstrate an understanding of concepts, processes, and technologies related to the transmission of hereditary characteristics.

 

Specific Expectations

D3.2 explains the concepts of DNA, genes, chromosomes, alleles, mitosis, and meiosis, and how they account for the transmission of hereditary characteristics according to Mendelian laws of inheritance.

D3.5 describe some reproductive technologies (e.g., cloning, artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, recombinant DNA), and explain how their use can increase the genetic diversity of a species (e.g., farm animals, crops).

Learning Goals

Students will be able to describe some reproductive technologies (e.g., cloning, artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, recombinant DNA), and explain how their use can increase the genetic diversity of a species (e.g., farm animals, crops).
