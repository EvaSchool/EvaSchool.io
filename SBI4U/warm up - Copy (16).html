Warm-up

Transcription uses the message coded in a DNA strand as a template for the synthesis of a complementary single-stranded mRNA molecule. The next step in the process of manufacturing a protein is translating the information coded in mRNA to a protein.
In translation, the encoded message is read, codon by codon, by a ribosome and, with the presence of transfer RNA molecules, the ribosome assembles one amino acid at a time into a polypeptide chain.
