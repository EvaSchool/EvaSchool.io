Wrap-up

Carbon atoms form the backbone of biological molecules. They can link
together to form chains, branched structures, and rings.


Functional groups have polar or ionic qualities that influence how they
interact with water and other molecules.


During a dehydration reaction, water is removed from subunits as they
combine to form a larger molecule.


During a hydrolysis reaction, larger molecules react with water and break
down into smaller subunits.
