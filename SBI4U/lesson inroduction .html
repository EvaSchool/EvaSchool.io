Natural Population Dynamics and Population Characteristics

Overall Expectations

F1. analyze the relationships between population growth, personal consumption, technological development, and our ecological footprint, and assess the effectiveness of some Canadian initiatives intended to assist expanding populations.

F2. investigate the characteristics of population growth and use models to calculate the growth of populations within an ecosystem. 

F3. demonstrate an understanding of concepts related to population growth and explain the factors that affect the growth of various populations of species.

Specific Expectations

F2.1 uses appropriate terminology related to population dynamics, including, but not limited to carrying capacity, population growth, population cycle, fecundity, and mortality.

F2.2 uses conceptual and mathematical population growth models to calculate the growth of populations of various species in an ecosystem.

F2.3 determines, through laboratory inquiry or using computer simulations, the characteristics of population growth of two different populations. 

F3.2 describes the characteristics of a given population, such as its growth, and density.

Learning Goals

  • Students will use appropriate terminology related to population dynamics, including, but not limited to: carrying capacity, population growth, population cycle, fecundity, and mortality.
  • Students will use conceptual and mathematical population growth models to calculate the growth of populations of various species in an ecosystem.
  • Students will determine, through laboratory inquiry or using computer simulations, the characteristics of population growth of two different populations. 
  • Students will describe the characteristics of a given population, such as its growth, density.
