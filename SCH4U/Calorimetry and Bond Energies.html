Calorimetry

  •  the experimental process of measuring the thermal energy change in a chemical or physical change

In calorimetry, the total amount of thermal energy absorbed or released by a chemical system is given the symbol q. The magnitude of q depends on three factors:

Callout

q=mc∆T

where:

q = thermal energy

m = mass of the substance 

c = specific heat capacity of the substance

∆T = change in temperature = Tfinal - Tinitial

Enthalpy Change

  • enthalpy (H) – the total amount of thermal energy in a substance; up until this time, measurement of enthalpy is still not possible
  • enthalpy change (ΔH) – the energy released to or absorbed from the surroundings during a chemical or physical change; can be measured using the calorimetry data. 

If the pressure remains constant, the enthalpy change of the chemical system is equal to the flow of thermal energy in and out of the system.

Enthalpy and Bond Energies

During a chemical reaction, the bonds in the reactants must break first. For bonds to be broken, energy must be added – an endothermic process. Energy associated with bond breaking has positive signs. Making new bonds in the products releases energy – an exothermic process.
