Introduction

Thermochemistry and Energy changes in Chemical and Nuclear Reactions   Calorimetry and Enthalpy and Bond Energies   Hess Law  Standard Enthalpies of Formation  Present and Future Energy Sources and Explore an Issue in Wind Technology 

Lesson Description

Learn about thermochemistry.

Learn about energy and chemical systems.

Learn about nuclear reactions.

Overall Expectations

D.1 analyze technologies and chemical processes that are based on energy changes and evaluate them in terms of their efficiency and their effects on the environment. 

Specific Expectations

D.1.1 analyze some conventional and alternative energy technologies and evaluate them in terms of their efficiency and impact on the environment.

D1.2 analyse the conditions required to maximize the efficiency of some common natural or industrial chemical reactions, and explains how the improved efficiency of the reaction contributes to environmental sustainability.

D.2 investigate and analyze energy changes and rates of reaction in physical and chemical processes and solve related problems. 

D.2.1 use appropriate terminology related to energy changes and rates of reaction.

D2.2 write thermochemical equations, expressing the energy change as an ∆H value or as a heat term in the equation.

D2.3 solve problems involving analysis of heat transfer in a chemical equation, using the equation Q=mc∆T.

D.2.4 plan and conduct an inquiry to calculate, using a calorimeter, the heat of the reaction of a substance, compare the actual heat of the reaction to the theoretical value, and suggest sources of experimental error.

D.2.5 solve problems related to energy changes in a chemical reaction, using Hess’s Law.

D.2.6 conduct an inquiry to test Hess’s Law.

D.2.7 calculate the heat of reaction for a formation reaction, using a table of standard enthalpies of formation and applying Hess’s Law.

D.2.8 plan and conduct an inquiry to determine how various factors affect the rate of chemical reaction.

Lesson Description

Learn about calorimetry and enthalpy changes.

Solve problems with thermal energy changes.

Solve problems with enthalpy changes.

Draw potential energy diagrams.

Overall Expectations

D.3 demonstrate an understanding of energy changes and rates of reaction.

Specific Expectations

D.3.1 compare the energy changes resulting from physical change, chemical reactions, and nuclear reactions, in terms of whether energy is released or absorbed. 

D.3.2 compare the energy change from a reaction in which bonds are formed to one in which bonds are broken and explains these changes in terms of endothermic or exothermic reactions.

D3.3 explain how mass, heat capacity, and change in temperature of a substance determine the amount of heat gained or lost by the substance.

D.3.4 state Hess’s law and explain using examples, how it is applied to find the enthalpy changes of a reaction.

D.3.5 explain, using collision theory and potential energy diagrams, how factors such as temperature, the surface area of the reactants, the nature of reactants, the addition of catalysts, and the concentration of the solution control the rate of a chemical reaction.

D.3.6 describe simple potential energy diagrams of chemical reactions.

D.3.7 explain with reference to a simple chemical reaction, how the rate of a reaction is determined by the series of elementary steps that make up the overall reaction mechanism.
