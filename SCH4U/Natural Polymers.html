Natural Polymers

Cellulose is a natural polymer that provides structure to plant tissues. Wood is primarily cellulose, and humans cannot digest cellulose. The cellulose parts of plants grown to produce human food are frequently used as animal food. Some companies are also using this waste as raw material to produce biofuels.

Polysaccharides

Polysaccharides are large polymers of monosaccharides.

 

Cellulose, starch, and glycogen are all polysaccharides (polymers of glucose). 


Amino Acids


Amino Acid is an organic molecule that contains a carboxyl (-COOH) group, an amino group (-NH2), and a hydrogen atom all attached to the same carbon atoms. 

Peptides

 

A peptide is two or more amino acids linked together through a condensation reaction between the amine and acid groups.

Additional condensation reactions lengthen the chain and produce a protein.


Nucleic Acids

Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA) is the substance that stores and transmits the genetic information; found in the nucleus of cells

 

Ribonucleic Acid (RNA) is a polymer in the cell cytoplasm that stores and transmits genetic information. Nucleic acids are polymer molecules that makeup DNA and RNA.
