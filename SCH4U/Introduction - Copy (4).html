Lesson Description

Throughout this course, students will:

Overall Expectations

F3. Demonstrate an understanding of the principles of oxidation-reduction reactions and practical applications of electrochemistry. 

Specific Expectations

F3.1 Explain Redox reactions in terms of the loss and gain of electrons and the associated change in oxidation number.

F3.2 Identify components of galvanic cells and explain how each component functions in a redox reaction.

F3.3 Describe galvanic cells in terms of oxidation and reduction half-cells whose voltages can be used to determine overall cell potential.

F3.4 Explain how a hydrogen half-cell is used as a standard reference to determine the voltages of another half-cell.

F3.5 Explain some applications of Electrochemistry in common industrial processes.

F3.6 Explain the corrosion of metals in terms of an electrochemical process and describe some common corrosion-inhibiting techniques.
