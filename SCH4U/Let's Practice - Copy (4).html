Solid phosphorus pentachloride, PCl5(s), can undergo a reversible reaction to form liquid phosphorus trichloride, PCl3(l), and chlorine gas, Cl2(g). Write the equilibrium law expression for this heterogeneous equilibrium.

Callout

Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction. Then, write the equilibrium law equation. Omit the concentrations of the pure solid, PCl5(s), and the pure liquid, PCl3(l).

PCl5(s) ⇌ PCl3 (l) + Cl2(g)

K = [Cl2(g)]

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction is already provided:

PCl5​(s)⇌PCl3​(l)+Cl2​(g)

For the equilibrium law equation, or the expression for the equilibrium constant (K), we only include the concentrations of gases and aqueous solutions. Since PCl5​ is a solid and PCl3​ is a liquid, their concentrations do not appear in the expression. Thus, the equilibrium constant for this reaction is:

K=[Cl2​(g)]

This means the equilibrium constant K is equal to the concentration of chlorine gas at equilibrium.
