Thermochemistry Energy Changes in Chemical and Nuclear Reactions

 

Callout

What is the relationship between chemical reactions and energy?

Going for a run: the cells in your body transform some of the chemical energy associated with glucose molecules into the kinetic energy of moving muscles; some of this chemical energy is also transformed into thermal energy, so your body temperature increases, and you start to sweat. 

Terminology

  • thermochemistry – the study of the energy changes that accompany physical or chemical changes in matter
  • energy – the ability to do work; SI unit is joules (J)
  • work – the amount of energy transferred by a force over a distance; SI unit is joules (J)
  • Thermal energy – total quantity or kinetic and potential energy in a substance
  • Heat – transfer of thermal energy from a warm object to a cooler object 
  • Temperature – a measure of the average kinetic energy in a substance. 

Law of Conservation of Energy

 Energy cannot be created or destroyed. 

Exothermic Reactions

         A chemical reaction that releases energy to the surroundings. This happens when more energy is released from the formation of new bonds in the products than is required to break bonds in the reactants. In the combustion of methane, energy is released as light or thermal energy. As you can see in the balanced equation, the energy term is included on the product side to indicate the release of energy. In an exothermic reaction, the temperature of the surroundings increases.

Specific Heat Capacity

  • quantity of thermal energy required to raise the temperature of 1 g of a substance by 1 oC; SI unit is J/(g oC)

This is the reason why sand is hotter than water. Sand has a lower specific heat capacity than water, which is why its temperature is raised easily.
