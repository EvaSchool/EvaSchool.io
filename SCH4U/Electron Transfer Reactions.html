Electron Transfer Reactions

 

Callout

What is an Electron Transfer Reaction?

Electron transfer (ET) occurs when an electron relocates from an atom or molecule to another such chemical entity. ET is a mechanistic description of a redox reaction, wherein the oxidation state of the reactant and product changes. Numerous biological processes involve ET reactions.

Half-Reaction Equations

A half-reaction equation is a chemical equation that represents one of the two parts of a redox reaction.

Oxidation Numbers


An oxidation number is a number used to keep track of electrons in oxidation–reduction reactions according to certain rules; also known as the oxidation state. 

Oxidizing Agents and Reducing Agents

Oxidizing agent: the reactant that is reduced (gains electrons from another substance) during an oxidation–reduction reaction.

Reducing agent: the reactant that is oxidized (loses electrons to another substance) during an oxidation–reduction reaction.
