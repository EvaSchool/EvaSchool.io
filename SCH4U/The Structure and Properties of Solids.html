Ionic Crystals

  • form from the interaction of metal and nonmetal ions.
  • the ions are arranged in a crystal lattice structure with alternating packing of the positive and negative ions.
  • notable characteristics include being hard and brittle, 
  • conducting
  • electricity when dissolved in water, and having high 
  • melting points which is a result of strong ionic bonds. 

Metallic crystal

  • formed by metals in the solid state, which is a structure composed of closely packed atoms held together by electrostatic interactions and free-moving electrons.
  • unlike ionic crystals, metallic crystals have varying properties explained by the electron sea theory

Molecular crystal 

  • a solid composed of individual molecules held together by intermolecular forces which determines its structure and properties 

Covalent Network Crystal 

Diamond

  • a covalent network crystal
  • hardest naturally occurring substance on earth.

The carbon atoms in a diamond molecule form a tetrahedral structure. Each carbon atom forms 4 single covalent bonds to other carbon atoms by way of sp3 hybrid orbitals.

Buckyball

  • a spherical arrangement of carbon atoms that forms a hollow cage-like structure
  • its actual name is Buckminsterfullerene, in honor of Richard Buckminster Fuller, who built the architectural domes of this shape
  • extremely stable structures that have very high melting points
  • used in chemical sensors, photovoltaic cells, cosmetics, and vehicles for delivering drugs to cells. 

Doping – modification of silicon or germanium by adding a small amount of elements such as arsenic or boron; allows engineers to create semiconductors with specific conductive properties.
