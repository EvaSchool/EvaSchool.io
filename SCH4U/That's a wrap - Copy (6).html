That's a Wrap!

Please review the following Learning Goals of this section and check the Success Criteria you already achieved.

Learning Goals

Students can: 

Identify the different equilibrium condition and reactions.

Determine concentrations for chemical equilibria.

Learn about Fritz Haber’s basic research on chemical equilibrium system.

Determine whether a system is at equilibrium or not using the reaction quotient (Q).

Determine the shift required to bring a system to equilibrium based on the reaction quotient (Q).

Solve problems related to equilibrium by performing calculations involving concentrations of reactants and products.

Success Criteria

Students can:

  1. Explain the different equilibrium condition and reactions.
  2. Calculate concentrations for chemical equilibria.
  3. Explain Le Châtelier’s Principle.
  4. Describe what happens in Changing an Equilibrium System without Affecting Equilibrium Position.
  5. Identify solubility product constant, Ksp, and write the expression for it.
  6. Solve problems related to solubility equilibrium by performing calculations involving concentrations of reactants and products.
  7. Predict whether a precipitate will form when two solutions are mixed.
  8. Predict the equilibrium shift when a common ion is added to an equilibrium system.
