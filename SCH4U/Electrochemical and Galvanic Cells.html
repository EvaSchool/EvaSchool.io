WHAT IS GALVANIC CELL?

 

Galvanic cell is an arrangement of two half-cells that produce electricity spontaneously.

  

 

TRANSFORMING CHEMICAL TO ELECTRICAL ENERGY


It is composed of two half-cells connected by a wire. 

Each half-cell consists of an electrode that is in contact with a solution of ions.

An electrode is a solid electrical conductor.

In this galvanic cell, electrons flow from the reducing agent, Zn(s), to the oxidizing agent, CuSO4(aq), through a conducting wire. 

The flow of ions through the sodium sulfate salt bridge keeps the solution in each half-cell electrically neutral.

 

CHARACTERISTICS OF  GALVANIC CELL

 

In honour of Luigi Galvani, this cell is called a galvanic cell: a device consisting of two connected half-cells that produce current electrical energy spontaneously. 

The reactions in a galvanic cell occur at the interface between the electrodes and the solutions where the electron transfer occurs.

The electrode where oxidation occurs is called the anode. 

The electrode where reduction occurs is called the cathode.
