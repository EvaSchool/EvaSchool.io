Summary Review

  • Most chemical reactions occur in a series of elementary steps. The sequence of the elementary steps making up a reaction is known as its reaction mechanism. 
  • A rate law equation can be written for each elementary step of a reaction, and the overall rate law equation for a reaction may be deduced from these. 
  • The slowest elementary step in a reaction mechanism is the rate-determining step.
  • There are two requirements for a plausible reaction mechanism:

  1. The elementary steps must combine to give the correct overall balanced equation.
  2. The mechanism must agree with the experimentally determined rate law equation.
