That's a Wrap!

Please review the following Learning Goals of this section and check the Success Criteria you already achieved.   

Learning Goals

  • Students will understand how to name parent alkenes and alkynes.
  • Students will understand what cis and trans isomers are.
  • Students will understand how to name side branches.
  • Students will understand how to name cyclic compounds.
  • Students will understand how to handle substitution reactions in alkenes and alkynes.
  • Students will understand how to name aromatic compounds.
  • Students will understand reactions with aromatic compounds.
  • Students will understand how to name alcohols, ethers, and thiols.
  • Students will understand reactions with alcohols.
  • Students will understand reactions with ethers.

Success Criteria

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:

  • Describe some impacts of organic compounds on human health, society, and the environment.
  • Use appropriate terminology, including IUPAC nomenclature, to describe organic compounds and their reactions.
  • Safely perform laboratory investigations involving organic compounds.
  • Create molecular models to represent simple organic compounds and their reactions.
  • Compare the structural formula and properties of different classes of organic compounds.
  • Explain the changes that occur during various organic chemical reactions and predict the products of those reactions.
  • Explain the concept of isomerism and relate variations in the properties of isomers to their structural formulas.
  • Name parent alkenes and alkynes.
  •  Know cis-trans isomers.
  • Name side branches.
  • Name aldehydes and ketones.
  • Understand reactions with aldehydes and ketones.
  • Name cyclic compounds.
  • Handle substitution reactions in alkenes and alkynes.
  • Name amines and amides.
  • Understand reactions of amines and amides.
