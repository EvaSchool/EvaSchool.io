Synthetic Addition Polymers

Nomex is a polymer made from amide monomers. This polymer is used in fabrics that can withstand the extreme temperatures of an industrial fire. Nomex is an example of a condensation polymer.

Condensation Polymers

The monomers involved in a condensation reaction have two reactive functional groups and not necessarily carbon double bonds.

The two most common types of condensation polymers have either ester linkages or amide linkages. 


                       

Polyesters

Polyester is a polymer formed by a series of esterification reactions between monomers. Monomers with two functional groups, carboxyl, and hydroxyl, form chains by undergoing condensation reactions at both ends.

As you can see, the monomers attach to each end of the chain, either with its carboxyl group reacting with the hydroxyl group on the chain, or with its hydroxyl group reacting with the carboxyl group in the chain.


Dacron

Dacron is a brand name for polyethene terephthalate (PET). It is a condensation copolymer formed by the reaction of ethane-1,2-diol (ethene glycol) and benzene 1,4-dicarboxylic acid (terephthalic acid).

Polyamides

A polyamide is a polymer formed by a condensation reaction resulting in amide linkages between monomers. Amide linkages are formed by a condensation reaction between an amine and carboxylic acid.

One distinct characteristic of a polyamide is its strength. Polyamides are also resistant to damage from insects and heat. Kevlar is used as body armor while Nomex is valued for its heat resistance. They are both polyamides.

Nylon 6,6

Nylon is a copolymer. One common form of nylon is produced when hexane-1,6-diamine (hexamethylenediamine) and hexanedioyl dichloride (adipoyl chloride) react.
