Lesson Description

To learn about rates of chemical reactions.

To learn how to monitor changes in the progress of chemical reactions.

To know about average rate of reaction.

To learn about instantaneous reaction rate.

To learn about stoichiometric rate relationship.

Overall Expectations

D.3 demonstrate an understanding of energy changes and rates of reaction.

Specific Expectations

D.3.4 state Hess’s law and explain using examples, how it is applied to find the enthalpy changes of a reaction.

D.3.5 explain, using collision theory and potential energy diagrams, how factors such as temperature, the surface area of the reactants, the nature of reactants, the addition of catalysts, and the concentration of the solution control the rate of a chemical reaction.

D.3.6 describe simple potential energy diagrams of chemical reactions.

D.3.7 explain with reference to a simple chemical reaction, how the rate of a reaction is determined by the series of elementary steps that make up the overall reaction mechanism.
