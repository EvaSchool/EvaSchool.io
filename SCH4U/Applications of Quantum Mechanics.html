Laser Technology

In 1917, Einstein established the theoretical foundations for the laser. He proposed the theory that electrons can be stimulated to higher energy states and then release radiation when they return to the ground state.

Laser Technology

Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation

  • A device that produces light of a single color with all waves travelling parallel to each other. 

Lasers are classified based on the laser beam’s power. Class 1 lasers are safe for most uses and include laser lights, levels, some pointers, and the lasers in printers and disc players. Class 4 lasers range from the medical lasers used to remove hair, blemishes, and scars to powerful industrial lasers used to cut through metals, or stacks of fabric in the manufacture of garments.

Quantum Analysis and Diagnostic Technologies

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

  • A medical tool in which magnetic fields interact with atoms in the human body, producing images that doctors can use to diagnose injuries and diseases
