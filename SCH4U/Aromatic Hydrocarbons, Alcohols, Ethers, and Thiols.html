Aromatic Hydrocarbons, Alcohols, Ethers, and Thiols

Aromatic Hydrocarbons:

These are unsaturated hydrocarbons that has a ring structure and a bonding arrangement that causes it to be chemically stable.

Properties of Aromatic Hydrocarbons:

Many aromatic hydrocarbons are liquid at room temperature but there are also crystalline solids. 

 

Aromatic compounds are insoluble in water because it is nonpolar. 

 

Aromatic compounds are volatile which means that they easily evaporate and disperse in air. 

  

Alcohols:

These are organic compounds that contain the hydroxyl group (-OH). 

 

Alcohols are polar molecules because of the presence of a C-O bond.
