Aufbau Principle

The theory is that an atom is “built up” by the addition of electrons, which fill orbitals starting at the lowest available energy orbital before filling higher energy orbitals. 

Electron configuration - the location and number of electrons in the energy levels of an atom or ion.

Hund’s Rule

States that in a particular set of orbitals of the same energy, the lowest energy configuration for an atom is the one with the maximum number of unpaired electrons allowed by Pauli’s exclusion principle; unpaired electrons are represented as having parallel spins. 

The electron configuration for a carbon atom could be written as 1s22s22p2

Explaining the Periodic Table 

valence electrons - electrons in the outermost shell (outermost principal quantum level)

Elements with the same valence electron configuration show similar chemical behavior. 

transition metals - elements whose highest-energy electrons are in d orbitals; elements scandium through zinc; the electron configuration of an atom of a transition metal is obtained by adding electrons to the five 3d orbitals.
