Schrodinger’s Standing Wave

The hydrogen atom visualized as a standing wave around the nucleus. In (a) and (b), the circumference of a particular circular orbit corresponds to a whole number of wavelengths. (c) Otherwise, destructive interference occurs. This model is consistent with the idea that only certain electron energies are allowed, because the atom is quantized.

Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle

It is impossible to know the exact position and speed of an electron at a given time.

wave function - the mathematical probability of finding an electron in a certain region of space.
