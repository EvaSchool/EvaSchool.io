Welcome to Chemistry Grade 12 (SCH4U)

This course enables students to deepen their understanding of chemistry through the study of organic chemistry, atomic and molecular structure, energy changes and rates of reaction, chemical systems and equilibrium, and electrochemistry. Students will further develop problem-solving and laboratory skills as they investigate chemical processes, and will enhance their ability to communicate scientific information.

This course prepares students for university programs in science, health, and engineering. It emphasizes inquiry-based learning and critical thinking in both theoretical and practical contexts.

Unit Title Description
Unit One: Organic Chemistry Students will explore the structures, properties, and reactions of various organic compounds, including hydrocarbons, alcohols, and acids. Real-world applications such as polymers and pharmaceuticals are also discussed.
Unit Two: Structure and Properties of Matter This unit examines atomic theory, chemical bonding, and molecular geometry. Students will develop a deeper understanding of how structure determines physical and chemical properties.
Unit Three: Energy Changes and Rates of Reaction Students will study thermochemical principles, calorimetry, and reaction kinetics. Concepts such as activation energy and catalysts are explored through both theory and investigation.
Unit Four: Chemical Systems and Equilibrium This unit focuses on dynamic equilibrium in chemical systems, including Le Chatelier’s Principle and solubility equilibria. Applications include acid-base and buffer systems.
Unit Five: Electrochemistry Students will examine redox reactions, galvanic and electrolytic cells, and the role of electrochemistry in technological applications such as batteries and corrosion prevention.
