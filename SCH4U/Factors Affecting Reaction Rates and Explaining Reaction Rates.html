Factors Affecting Reaction Rates and Explaining Reaction Rates

 

Callout

Factors Affecting Rates of Reaction

The rate of reaction is affected by many factors:

  • chemical nature of the reactants
  • concentration of reactants
  • surface area 
  • the temperature of the reaction system
  • presence of catalyst

Chemical Nature of the Reactants

Sodium is very reactive with oxygen while copper oxidizes when exposed to air at a slower rate. Gold, on the other hand, does not oxidize at room temperature which makes it ideal for use in jewelry. 

Callout

Concentration of Reactants

As the concentration of reactant increases, so would the reaction rate.

You may have seen signs in hospitals warning about smoking or using flames where oxygen gas is in use. This is because cylinders of compressed gas used to supply oxygen to patients can be 50% oxygen gas which may cause reactions to be rapid, violent, and even explosive. 

Surface Area

In dissolving sugar in water, sugar will dissolve faster if we use powdered sugar than sugar cubes. 

In cases where you have reactants on different states of matter (solid and liquid), the greater the surface area of the solid, the faster the reaction. 

Temperature of the Reaction System

 

The image shows a snake and a mouse. In contrast to warm-blooded animals, which maintain and regulate their own body temperature, cold-blooded animals cool down when the temperature of the surroundings drops. This lowers the rates of the chemical reactions that control muscle activity, causing the animal to become less active. 

Callout

Presence of a Catalyst

Polyethene (also called polyethylene) is a polymer made by passing ethene gas over a metal catalyst. The metal catalyst speeds up the addition reaction that opens the double bond of ethene, allowing ethene molecules to join to make polyethylene.

Callout

Activation Energy (Ea)

  • the minimum energy that reactant molecules must possess for a reaction to be successful.
  • used to overcome the electrostatic repulsive forces between colliding entities.
  • used to weaken the bonds of the reactants.

Summary Review

  • Increasing the surface area and the concentration of a reactant increases the total number of collisions and so the number of effective collisions.
  • Increasing the temperature will increase the average kinetic energy of the entities of reactants. This will result in more entities having a quantity of kinetic energy the same or greater than the activation energy and will increase the number of collisions. 
  • A catalyst provides a reaction pathway with a lower activation energy.
