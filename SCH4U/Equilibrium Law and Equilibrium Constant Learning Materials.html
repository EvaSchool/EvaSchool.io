What is the equilibrium law and why is it useful?

Equilibrium law the mathematical description of a chemical system at equilibrium. The equilibrium law is sometimes called the law of mass action. Suppose that A, B, C, and D are chemical entities in gas or aqueous states, and that a, b, c, and d are the coefficients in a balanced chemical equation. We can now represent a chemical equilibrium system by this general chemical equation:

aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

The square brackets in the equation indicate the concentrations of the chemical entities at equilibrium. K is the symbol for the equilibrium constant, a constant numerical value that defines the equilibrium law for a given system. Since the units of the equilibrium constant vary according to the values of a, b, c, and d, equilibrium constant values are usually reported without units.
