Biocatalysis

  • Any method that uses biocatalysts to increase the reaction rate.
  • Pharmaceuticals: researchers explore ways to use biocatalysts to reduce cost instead of traditional chemical methods
  • Environment Protection: biocatalysts have the potential to reduce or eliminate by-products that are toxic to the environment.

The Application

Industrial Preparation of Paper

  • The kraft process, the removal of lignin which reduces the quality of paper, uses sodium hydroxide and sodium sulfide and results in large volumes of pollutants. This process also uses a lot of energy. 
  • The kraft process generates hydrogen sulfide gas and other sulfur gas compounds as by-products. 
  • The use of chlorine bleach to produce white paper produces dioxins, substances that cause cancer in humans and other organisms. 

Biocatalysis in Paper Industry

  • Laccases: This class of enzymes can remove lignin from cellulose in wood without the need for harsh chemicals. These enzymes break down the lignin, leaving the cellulose intact.
  • Xylanase: Treatment with this enzyme enables manufacturers to reduce chlorine consumption by 15 to 25%. This enzyme is used in bleaching procedures in which chlorine is completely replaced with chlorine dioxide or non-chlorine chemicals.
