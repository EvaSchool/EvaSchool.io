The change in enthalpy that accompanies the formation of 1 mol of a compound from its elements in their standard states. 

standard state – the most stable form of a substance under standard conditions, 25oC and 100kPa

  • gas – noble gases; diatomic elements (hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, fluorine, and chlorine)
  • liquid – bromine and mercury
  • solid – most elements

Fossil Fuels

most widely used class of fuel
energy stored in fossil fuels originated in the Sun

Coal

  • a fossil fuel formed from decaying plant matter that has been buried for millions of years
  • plant material, which is composed of cellulose, is subjected to high pressure and temperatures beneath Earth’s surface, and over time, the process removes a large proportion of the oxygen and hydrogen atoms and leaving mostly atoms of carbon

Types of Coal 

  • Lignite – has the lowest carbon content
  • Sub-bituminous
  • Bituminous
  • Anthracite – most valuable because of its high carbon content

Nuclear Energy

  • a conventional energy source that is not subject to most of the disadvantages of fossil fuels
  • not a limited resource and does not release greenhouse gases
  • produce less air pollution than electrical generating plants fueled by the combustion of coal. 

 

Hydrogen Gas Fuel

  • can be utilized as a fuel source because of its high enthalpy of combustion

Callout

  • Limitations:
  • Production cost: hydrogen gas can be produced by passing an electric current through water; the quantity of energy required for the process is 285 kJ, the same amount released by the combustion of hydrogen gas.
  • Storage and transport: tankers and pipelines used to transport fuels are generally made with metal; molecules of hydrogen gas are able to infiltrate the metal lattice structure.
