The Economy

Lesson Description

This lesson explores the multifaceted dynamics of the economy, examining its intricate relationship with various socio-economic factors, cultural influences, population mobility, and changes in the nature of work in Canada. Students will delve into how economic trends are shaped by factors such as government policies, technological advancements, and global market forces.

Additionally, they will explore how culture, tradition, and language contribute to shaping social trends within the economic sphere, influencing consumer behavior, business practices, and societal values. The lesson will also address the impact of population mobility on social patterns and trends, considering factors such as migration, urbanization, and demographic shifts. 

Furthermore, students will analyze changes in the nature of work in Canada, including shifts in employment patterns, the rise of the gig economy, and the integration of technology into various industries. Through this comprehensive exploration, students will gain a deeper understanding of the complexities of the economy and its profound implications for Canadian society.

Overall Expectations

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors.

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

C2.3 Explain how increases in population mobility shape social patterns and trends.

C2.4 Explain changes in the nature of work in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
