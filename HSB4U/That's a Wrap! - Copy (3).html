What have I learned?

In conclusion, socialization serves as a cornerstone process in understanding how individuals adapt and engage within society, which is intertwined with various theories of social change. Major theories such as functionalism, conflict theory, and symbolic interactionism offer different perspectives on how social change occurs, highlighting psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions as contributing factors. Social science methodologies and research designs, including surveys, experiments, and ethnographic studies, provide insights into the complexities of socialization and its impacts on society. Furthermore, economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors interact to shape socialization processes, influencing individuals' behaviors and identities. Finally, various means of creating social change, such as advocacy, policy reform, and grassroots movements, demonstrate the dynamic nature of societal evolution and the importance of understanding socialization in driving positive transformations within communities.

Learning Goals

  • Learn the forces that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence;
  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can learn the forces that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Can appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
