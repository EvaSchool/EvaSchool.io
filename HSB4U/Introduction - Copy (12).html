Deviants and Robert Merton’s Social Strain Typology

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will explore the concept of deviance through Robert Merton's Social Strain Typology. We will examine population trends in Canada and globally, including patterns of rural-suburban-urban migration, the impact of the baby boom effect, and differences in demographic profiles between developed and developing nations. Additionally, we will delve into demography, discussing concepts such as replacement level, fertility rate, and demographic transition, and analyzing their social implications. Furthermore, we will investigate how socio-economic factors, such as extracurricular activity costs, transportation access, technological resources, and healthcare and education availability, shape societal trends. Finally, we will explore the influence of culture, tradition, and language on social trends, highlighting their significance in understanding and addressing deviant behavior within communities. Through this comprehensive exploration, students will gain a deeper understanding of deviance and its interconnectedness with broader social, economic, and cultural factors.

Overall Expectations

C1 Demographics: demonstrate an understanding of the importance of demographics as a tool for studying social patterns and trends, both nationally and globally.

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

C1.1 Describe population trends in Canada and around the world (e.g., rural-suburban-urban migration, the baby boom effect, differences between the demographic profiles of developed and developing nations).

C1.2 Explain concepts related to demography (e.g., replacement level, fertility rate, demographic transition), and assess the social impact of demographic change.

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors (e.g., extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, access to computers, access to health care and education).

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
