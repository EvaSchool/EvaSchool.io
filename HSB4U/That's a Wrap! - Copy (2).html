What have I learned?

In conclusion, this lesson has covered a broad spectrum of topics related to understanding social change. We explored major theories, including evolutionary, cyclical, challenge and response, functionalist, and conflict theories, and discussed various psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions contributing to social change. Additionally, we examined major social science methodologies and research designs, such as primary and secondary sources, open-ended and close-ended survey questions, and case studies. We also considered the influence of economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors on social change, including global warming, environmental activism, leadership changes, and pluralism. Finally, we discussed different means of creating social change, such as research-based interventions, advocacy, policy reforms, and grassroots movements, providing a comprehensive understanding of the multifaceted nature of societal transformation.

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information.
  • Learn the forces that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
