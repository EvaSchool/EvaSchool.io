Socialization Trends and Challenges

Lesson Description

In this lesson on Socialization Trends and Challenges, we will explore the theories underpinning social change, including functionalism, conflict theory, and symbolic interactionism, alongside psychological, social, material, and cultural factors driving societal shifts. We'll examine social science methodologies, economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural influences on socialization, means of creating social change, and population trends globally and in Canada. Additionally, we'll cover demography concepts like replacement level and demographic transition, assessing their social impact, and discuss how socio-economic factors and cultural elements shape trends, offering a comprehensive understanding of contemporary social dynamics.

Overall Expectations

B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change

B2 Causes and Effects of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change

B3 Technological Change: demonstrate an understanding of patterns and effects of technological change from a social science perspective

C1 Demographics: demonstrate an understanding of the importance of demographics as a tool for studying social patterns and trends, both nationally and globally.

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change.

B1.2 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change.

B1.3 Explain major social science methodologies and research designs.

B2.2 Explain how various economic, environmental, political, or sociocultural factors.

B2.3 Explain various means of creating social change.

C1.1 Describe population trends in Canada and around the world (e.g., rural-suburban-urban migration, the baby boom effect, differences between the demographic profiles of developed and developing nations).

C1.2 Explain concepts related to demography (e.g., replacement level, fertility rate, demographic transition), and assess the social impact of demographic change.

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors (e.g., extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, access to computers, access to health care and education).

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
