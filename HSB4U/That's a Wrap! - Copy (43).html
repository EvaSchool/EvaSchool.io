What have I learned?

 

The lesson on Labour Law and Globalization explores key provisions in provincial, national, and international agreements addressing human rights, discrimination, and the impact of low literacy rates on living standards. It examines socio-economic factors like international competition and prejudice that contribute to poverty and discusses arrangements between governments and transnational corporations, along with the roles of organizations like the IMF and WHO in global economic stability.

The lesson evaluates globalization's impact on Canadian society, including economic, social, and environmental aspects, and highlights the effects of unfair exploitation on individuals and groups, including Canadian government policies that perpetuate exploitation. Finally, it addresses environmental changes from fossil-fuel exploitation and their implications for Canadians' well-being.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
