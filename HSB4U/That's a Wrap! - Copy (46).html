What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, understanding global interactions is crucial in navigating the complexities of a globalized world. This lesson has provided insight into various aspects, including the world-system theory and exploitation, Canada's role as a global citizen, and the necessity of possessing the right tools for change. Through discussions on theories like Wallerstein's world-system theory, which highlights the disparities between core and periphery nations, and examples such as the exploitation within the coffee trade, students have gained a deeper understanding of global economic systems.

Furthermore, Canada's involvement in global relief efforts, as demonstrated through instances like the donations after the Indian Ocean tsunami and the earthquake in Haiti, underscores the importance of international cooperation and solidarity. By focusing on national and foreign policies and embracing principles of equality and wealth sharing, students are better equipped to contribute positively to global interactions and address challenges on a global scale.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
