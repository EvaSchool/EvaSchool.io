Generational Theories

Lesson Description

This lesson on Generational Theories delves into major theories explaining social change, including Evolutionary, Cyclical, Challenge and Response, Functionalist, and Conflict Theories, and examines their key ideas. It explores psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions influencing social change, covers social science methodologies, and discusses economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors shaping societal shifts. Moreover, it analyzes various means of creating social change, providing a comprehensive understanding of how generational theories inform our understanding of societal transformations.

Overall Expectations

B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change

B2 Causes and Effects of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change

B3 Technological Change: demonstrate an understanding of patterns and effects of technological change from a social science perspective

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change.

B1.2 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change.

B1.3 Explain major social science methodologies and research designs.

B2.2 Explain how various economic, environmental, political, or sociocultural factors.

B2.3 Explain various means of creating social change.

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information.
  • Learn the theories that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate and identify the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
