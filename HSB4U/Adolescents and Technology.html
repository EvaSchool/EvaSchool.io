Adolescents and Technology

  • The foregoing discussion tried to explain the basis of adolescent behaviour.  It presented Maslow’s human motivation and his hierarchy of needs, Sherif’s group formation and its influence on human behaviour, and Merton’s divergent behaviors.
  • This section will show how trends in adolescent behaviour have changed over time, the reasons why they are drawn to such particular trending behaviours and the unprecedented role technology plays in adolescence which no previous generation has ever experienced.
  • Adolescents and technology intersect in various ways, influencing their daily lives, social interactions, and overall development. In today's digital age, adolescents are immersed in a wide array of technological devices and platforms, including smartphones, tablets, laptops, and social media platforms. These technologies offer avenues for communication, entertainment, learning, and self-expression. Adolescents use technology to connect with their peers, engage in online communities, access information, and express their creativity through various digital mediums such as photography, video editing, and content creation. However, along with the benefits, there are also challenges and concerns associated with adolescents' use of technology, including issues related to screen time, cyberbullying, privacy, and digital literacy. Understanding the complex relationship between adolescents and technology is crucial for educators, parents, and policymakers to support healthy and responsible technology use among adolescents.

Social Networking

  • With the advent of modern technology, adolescents of today have become so technologically inclined and in most cases have become their parents’/grandparents’ “saviour” when it comes to social networking and technology.
  • There has been no greater influence on adolescents today than technology. Texting and social networking have become the norms of communication among them. 
  • Aside from providing communication with their peers, it also provides entertainment, educational tools, and virtual means to express their identity.
  • It facilitates greater understanding and connection to the global community. No one can argue that technology has become the most significant agent of socialization among adolescents.

Cyberbullying

  • Like any other phenomenon, the inextricable link between social networking and adolescents also has some negative effects.
  • Majority of adolescents (even adults for that matter) conduct their personal relationships/business under the very eye of virtual audiences which leaves them vulnerable to cruel responses, insults or harassment from their own peer groups.
  • Cyberbullying happens when an individual is being bullied or harassed by means of inappropriate pictures or messages on social networking sites, by email or by other electronic means.
  • It may begin quite innocently but may escalate to become serious criminal activity. It has generated considerable response from the government through legislations in order to overcome and eliminate it.
  • It can be said that the very same social networking sites that offer opportunities for friendship and connection with people globally, can also lead to serious criminal activity and personal harm to some.

Sexting

Sex during adolescence has been a great concern due to its possible negative consequences. In recent years, however, another phenomenon has emerged through the use of technology, which is sexting.
Sexting is the act of sending sexually charged text messages or posting sexual contents online. It has gained government’s attention and has recently prompted the legislation of laws protecting children on issues of sexuality, pornography, and privacy.
Though it may not be exclusively relegated to the youth, it has provided another level of self-disclosure and intimacy that has not existed in the past adolescent relationships.
Its negative impact on the adolescent psychological/emotional side, their future relationships and future career, deserves a closer look.
