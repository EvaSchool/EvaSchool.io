What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, this lesson on the economy explores how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors, including employment patterns, youth workforce participation, and the impact of migration on social patterns.

It also discusses the influence of culture, tradition, and language on social trends, particularly in identity formation and interactions related to work. Moreover, the lesson highlights the role of increases in population mobility in shaping social patterns, emphasizing migration's impact on workforce dynamics and local economies. 

Additionally, it explains changes in the nature of work in Canada, touching on shifts in employment trends, youth unemployment rates, and the evolving landscape of debt and financial stability, providing insights into the complex interplay of socio-economic factors and cultural influences in shaping social trends within the Canadian context.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behaviour and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
