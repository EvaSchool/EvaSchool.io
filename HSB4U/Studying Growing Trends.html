Studying Growing Trends

  • Demography (the statistical study of a population) is a means of analyzing and predicting social, cultural, economic, and political trends.
  • Demographic data provide practical applications including its usefulness for public policy making and for marketing purposes.
  • Demographics provide a good source of information about how society has changed over time. 
  • In Canada, the most important source of demographic information such as age, ethnicity, gender, income, language, religion, marital status, among others is the Canadian census. 
  • A census is an official count of a population and an important tool for examining said demographic information. It provides a snapshot of a society within a defined period of time.

The Canadian Census

  • Statistics Canada is in charge of the census program which they do every five years. The last census was conducted in 2016 and they are preparing to conduct the next one in 2021. 
  • They gather information from all Canadian households through a survey. The census is either returned by mail or completed online.
  • Lifestyles and different family structures of Canadian households are revealed in the census. 
  • A census family is defined as a married couple or a common-law couple, with or without children, or a lone parent with at least one child, living in the same dwelling.

Some changes or trends that have been noted of late in the Canadian census include:

  • The once predominant married couple family is giving way to other forms such as common-law couple and same-sex couple families.
  • Same-sex family structure was recognized in the 2006 census (gay marriage was legalized in June 2005).
  • A large number of young adults remain in the parental home during their post-secondary education or return to it after completing their studies.
