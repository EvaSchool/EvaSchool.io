External Factors Influencing Change

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we'll explore the external factors influencing social change, examining major theories like functionalism, conflict theory, feminism, and cognitive theory, and describing psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions such as cognitive dissonance, paradigm shifts, class struggles, and globalization.

Through discussions and activities, participants will gain a comprehensive understanding of how these factors interact to drive societal transformations, empowering them to navigate complex social dynamics effectively.

Overall Expectations

B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change (e.g., functionalism, conflict theory, interpretive theories, feminism, cultural materialism, structuralism, humanism, queer theory, cognitive theory).

B1.2 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change (e.g., cognitive dissonance, paradigm shift, class struggle, tension and adaptation, material and/or cultural change, globalization).

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the major social science forces that influence social change.
  • Describe the schools of thought relating to social change.
