What have I learned?

In conclusion, Robert Merton's Social Strain Typology offers insight into deviant behavior amidst shifting demographic trends and socio-economic factors. As populations transition between rural, suburban, and urban areas and the effects of the baby boom shape developed nations, concepts like replacement level, fertility rate, and demographic transition illuminate the societal impacts of demographic changes. Socio-economic factors such as extracurricular costs, transportation access, and healthcare availability significantly shape societal trends, while culture, tradition, and language influence social norms and values. By considering these factors alongside Merton's typology, we gain a deeper understanding of deviant behavior's implications for social dynamics and change.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Can recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
