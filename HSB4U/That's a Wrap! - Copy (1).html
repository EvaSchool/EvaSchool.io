What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the study of social change involves exploring various theories such as functionalism, conflict theory, interpretive theories, feminism, cultural materialism, structuralism, humanism, queer theory, and cognitive theory.

These theories offer distinct perspectives on societal transformation, which is influenced by economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors like global warming/climate change, environmental activism, the threat of separatism, leadership changes, and pluralism. Resistance to change can stem from factors such as cost, traditional values, and fear of negative consequences. 

Furthermore, recent technological advancements, including computer technology and the Internet for social networking, and workplace technologies necessitating ergonomic solutions, highlight the significant impact of technology on societal dynamics and individual experiences. 

Understanding these theories and factors provides valuable insights into the complexities of social change and its implications for individuals and society.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the major social science forces that influence social change.
  • Describe the schools of thought relating to social change.
  • Develop a detailed research plan to investigate a topic related to social change.
