What have I learned?

 

As we conclude our exploration of Theoretical Responses to Economic Globalization, it's evident that various theoretical frameworks offer valuable insights into understanding the complexities of economic globalization and its impact on societies. From the Modernization Theory's focus on societal transformation to the Dependency Theory's critique of unequal trade relationships, and the World-Systems Theory's examination of global inequalities, each perspective sheds light on different aspects of this multifaceted phenomenon.

Additionally, the New International Division of Labor Theory highlights the evolving nature of global production processes and the challenges faced by workers in an interconnected world. By critically analyzing these theories, we gain a deeper understanding of the socio-economic dynamics at play in the era of globalization, empowering us to address its opportunities and challenges more effectively on both national and international levels.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
