Socialization Trends and Challenges

  • The family has been regarded by many sociologists as the most important institution in society, and therefore is the primary agent of socialization but recent changes have shown that there are many factors that shape an individual’s beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors.
  • Tweens are girls and boys typically between the ages of 8 to 13, no longer a child, and not yet an adolescent.

Emergence of Canadian Tweens

  • The influence of advertising and marketing has been shaping the minds and behaviour of consumers from a very young age more than ever.  
  • Advertising and marketing have started to target very specific consumers that led to the creation of a new set of individuals which is the budding adolescents or tweens as they have been aptly referred to.
  • This phenomenon has become so prevalent in North America. Sociologists have recently regarded them as a distinct new demographic, thus creating a shift in parenting techniques and specific trends on how children now come of age.
  • Children in recent age have been afforded more rights and privileges. 
  • They are encouraged to express their individuality starting at an early age. They have become stakeholders in family decisions and their consumer choices matter as well.
  • Their privileged position in the family is attributed to factors such as families having fewer children, parents being older, families being single parents, and parents working outside the home.
  • A sad reality however, is that they are also susceptible to teen/adolescent problems, such as depression, suicidal tendencies, and the hyper-sexualization of youth especially among tween girls.

Sexualization of children

  • The fashion industry through their aggressive advertising efforts have seriously targeted the younger demographic. 
  • They, however, have particularly picked out young girls with an emphasis on a narrow standard of physical beauty.
  • The kind of media advertising that objectifies young girls in terms of their physical characteristics and sexiness has created social values and attitudes that have negative consequences for young girls and society as a whole.
  • Seeing that their reality is different from what they see in the media, a growing number of young girls have been observed to be experiencing problems such as depression, eating disorders, or low self-esteem.

Delayed transitions

  • In contrast to the tween trend which makes adolescents become adults before their time, there is this growing trend of fledgling adults (boomerang generation) who remain in the parental home after their teenage years and well into adulthood. This transition now seems to be taking longer than before.

Sociologists point to five developmental tasks that individuals normally progress through from adolescence to adulthood:

  • Completing some form of education.
  • Leaving their parents’ home.
  • Gaining full time employment.
  • Entering romantic relationships with an eye to long-term commitments such as marriage and cohabitation.
  • Preparing and planning to have children.

However, many youths of today have a different reality from the developmental tasks listed above.  Some of the reasons they delay the transition to adulthood are:

  • they attend school longer than earlier generations;
  • the rising costs of education leave many young people deep in student debt;
  • They are having a hard time finding full-time employment.
  • The rising cost of living especially in the cities (housing, food, etc,).
  • They are getting married later and also having children later.
  • They are taking the chance to stay with parents to save for their future.
  • This may also delay the parents’ transition into retired life. This proves that trends in one life stage have consequences on another.

Socialization Trends and Challenges

  • How the parents spend and value money plays a big role in how children spend and value their money as adults including the value they place upon consumer products and consumption.
  • Consumerism has pushed for the acquisition of goods and services in increasing amounts more than ever.
  • Every consumption the family makes has implications on many factors such as the economy, environment, social justice on a global scale.
  • Hence, this new awareness has led to an increased interest in ethical consumption.
  • While social or modern media has propelled consumerism to great heights, it has also been useful in making the public aware if the products they consume are ethically produced or in an environmentally responsible way and in accordance with the principles of social justice and equity.
