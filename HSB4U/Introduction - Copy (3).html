Origins of Adolescence

Lesson Description

In this lesson on the Origins of Adolescence, students will explore major theories of social change, including functionalism, conflict theory, interpretive theories, feminism, cultural materialism, structuralism, humanism, queer theory, and cognitive theory, comparing their key ideas. They will also examine various psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions contributing to social change, along with major social science methodologies and research designs. Additionally, students will learn about economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors influencing the emergence of adolescence and explore different means of creating social change, from advocacy to technological innovation, providing a comprehensive understanding of the origins of adolescence and its societal implications.

Overall Expectations

  • B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change
  • B2 Causes and Effects of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change
  • B3 Technological Change: demonstrate an understanding of patterns and effects of technological change from a social science perspective

Specific Expectations

  • B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change.
  • B1.2 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change.
  • B1.3 Explain major social science methodologies and research designs.
  • B2.2 Explain how various economic, environmental, political, or sociocultural factors.
  • B2.3 Explain various means of creating social change.

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information.
  • Learn the forces that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
