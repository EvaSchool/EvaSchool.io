What have I learned?

 

This lesson explores the complex dynamics of global market trends, examining their impact on human rights, socio-economic conditions, transnational arrangements, transnational organizations, and environmental sustainability. Students will analyze provincial, national, and international agreements addressing human rights issues, while gaining insights into discrimination in global markets. Statistical data on literacy rates will be evaluated alongside an examination of how socio-economic conditions and structural frameworks contribute to poverty.

The lesson will delve into the relationships between governments and transnational corporations, assess the roles of transnational organizations, explore globalization's impacts on Canadian society, and scrutinize historical and contemporary government policies perpetuating exploitation. Environmental changes resulting from fossil fuel exploitation will be discussed, emphasizing critical thinking to understand the intricate connections between global market trends and various socio-economic, political, and environmental factors.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
