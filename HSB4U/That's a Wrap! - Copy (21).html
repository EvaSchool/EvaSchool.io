What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, Canada's political system, a constitutional monarchy with a democratically elected government, grants citizens aged 18 and older the right to vote in various elections. Despite historically low political participation among young Canadians aged 18 to 24, recent years have seen some improvements, attributed to efforts by politicians to engage youth through social media and initiatives to enhance civic education.

This conclusion encapsulates key theories of social change, including psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions, and acknowledges the role of influential Canadian leaders in driving social change. 

It also discusses various means of creating social change, examines relationships between conformity, alienation, poverty, affluence, and social change, and highlights the impact of social change on individuals and Canadian society as a whole.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
