Adolescents and Technology

  • Though it may not be exclusively relegated to the youth, it has provided another level of self-disclosure and intimacy that has not existed in the past adolescent relationships.
  • Its negative impact on the adolescent psychological/emotional side, their future relationships and future career, deserves a closer look.

Social Challenges for Adolescents

  • The technological advances available to adolescents today have afforded them a world of advantages, but has also amplified the struggles young people face. 
  • The prevalence of digital communication (e.g., Facebook, Instagram, Twitter) has changed the way they communicate with their peers and romantic interests, by becoming boundless as to space and time.
  • The consequence, however, is withdrawal from actual relationships or physical activities making them experience what is called social isolation and in extreme cases, may even lead to suicide.

Social Isolation

Melvin Seeman (1918-), an American Sociologist who led one of the most well-known studies on social alienation offers the following distinct elements:

Powerlessness

belief by the individual that s/he does not have the necessary skill or means to accomplish the desired goals

Meaninglessness

when an individual is unable or have a diminished sense of ability to predict the outcome of his/her behaviour

Normlessness

occurs when social norms no longer serve as an effective guide for personal behaviour

Social isolation

dissociation from social norms leads to social withdrawal where the individual may feel segregated from the community.

Self-estrangement 

the state of emotional and psychological being in which the individual negates personal interests and prefers to engage in impersonal, external activities to satisfy his/her needs.

Suicide

  • Among the many struggles young adults face, perhaps the issue of suicide is the most tragic and extreme. Many experts preface suicide by using Seeman’s types of social alienation. 
  • It is definitely a very sensitive issue and while there is no definitive explanation as to the increase in suicide rates today, experts point to social relationships as a guiding force that contributes to suicidal tendencies among the teens.

  • Gabor Maté (1944-) a Canadian-Hungarian physician offers a convincing argument about teen suicide. 
  • He connects its prevalence to reliance on peer orientation as a socializing force among adolescents today.
  • He notes the dangers of peers replacing parents as the primary agent of socialization among adolescents. 
  • When peer relationship is strained or breaks down, an adolescent may experience rejection or even bullying, and without the socializing presence of parents, the person may feel isolated and rejected and may contemplate suicide if the situation is prolonged.

  • Émile Durkheim (1858-1917) in his book Suicide published in 1897 offered the most persuasive theory about suicide.
  • As a social phenomenon, he explained that the deterioration or collapse of social relationships, or the overbearing ones, could lead an individual to choose suicide as a remedy to their personal and social difficulties.
