What have I learned?

In conclusion, the study of adolescent development encompasses diverse theories like Piaget's cognitive stages, Erikson's psychosocial framework, and Kohlberg's moral development, which outline social change. Psychological, social, and cultural conditions, alongside economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors, shape adolescent development and contribute to broader societal shifts. Various social science methodologies aid in exploring these complexities, while understanding these factors enables the creation of interventions and policies aimed at fostering positive social change for adolescents and society at large. 

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information
  • Learn the theories that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate and identify the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
