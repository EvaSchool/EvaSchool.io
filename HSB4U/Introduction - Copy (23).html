The Media

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will delve into the profound influence of media on shaping social trends within the Canadian context. We will start by examining how socio-economic factors play a pivotal role in shaping media trends, including the influence of advertising revenue, corporate ownership, and consumer spending habits.

Next, we will explore how culture, tradition, and language influence the portrayal of social issues and trends in media content, highlighting the impact of cultural values and linguistic nuances on audience perceptions. Additionally, we will discuss how increases in population mobility, such as urbanization and immigration, contribute to shifting social patterns and trends portrayed in media narratives. 

Finally, we will analyze changes in the nature of work in Canada, focusing on how media representations of labor markets, job opportunities, and workplace dynamics reflect broader socio-economic shifts and cultural attitudes towards work. Through this comprehensive exploration, students will gain a deeper understanding of the multifaceted relationship between media and social trends, equipping them with critical insights into contemporary societal dynamics.

Overall Expectations

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors.

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

C2.3 Explain how increases in population mobility shape social patterns and trends.

C2.4 Explain changes in the nature of work in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
