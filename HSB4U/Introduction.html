Social Science and Change

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will explore the dynamic field of social science and its role in understanding and explaining social change.

Through a comparative analysis of major theories and methodologies used in social science research, students will develop a deeper understanding of how societies evolve, and the methods used to study these transformations.

Overall Expectations

B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change (e.g., functionalism, conflict theory, interpretive theories, feminism, cultural materialism, structuralism, humanism, queer theory, cognitive theory).

B1.3 Explain major social science methodologies and research designs (e.g., qualitative and quanti­tative methods, critical ethnography, participant observation, survey, field observation, interview, experiment, focus group).

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the major social science forces that influence social change.
  • Describe the schools of thought relating to social change.
