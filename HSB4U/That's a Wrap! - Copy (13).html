What have I learned?

In conclusion, the discussion on adolescents and technology unveils a multifaceted landscape shaped by the intersection of technological advancements and social challenges. This examination sheds light on how population trends, such as rural-suburban-urban migration and the baby boom effect, alongside concepts in demography like replacement level and demographic transition, impact adolescents' access to technology and their social interactions. Furthermore, socio-economic factors such as extracurricular activity costs, transportation accessibility, and healthcare and education availability significantly influence the trends observed in adolescent technology usage and its social ramifications. Moreover, the influence of culture, tradition, and language on social trends among adolescents underscores the need for a nuanced understanding of these dynamics to address the evolving challenges and opportunities presented by technology in shaping adolescent experiences and well-being on a global scale.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Can recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
