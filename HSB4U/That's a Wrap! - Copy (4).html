What have I learned?

In conclusion, the exploration of theories of adolescent development not only illuminates the complex transition from childhood to adulthood but also offers insights into social change. By comparing key ideas from dominant values to gradual change theories, and examining their relevance within societal transformation, we uncover psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions driving both individual development and social change. Understanding major social science methodologies and research designs enables critical analysis of factors contributing to social change, including economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural influences. Delving into various means of creating social change, such as structural functionalism, conflict theory, feminist theory, symbolic interaction, and social exchange, provides a comprehensive understanding of the dynamic interplay between theory and practice in shaping societies and individuals.

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information
  • Learn the theories that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate and identify the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
