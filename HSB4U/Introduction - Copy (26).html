Unit 3 Assessment of Learning

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will complete an assessment of your learning for this unit that is provided by the teacher.

Overall Expectations

B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change.

B2 Causes and Effects of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change.

C1 Demographics: demonstrate an understanding of major theories, perspectives, and research methods in psychology.

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Outline and compare the key ideas of major theories used to explain social change.

B1.2 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change.

B1.3 Explain major social science methodologies and research designs.

B2.1 Describe various psychological, social, material, or cultural conditions that are used to explain social change.

B2.2 Explain how various economic, environmental, political, or sociocultural factors.

B2.3 Explain various means of creating social change.

B2.4 Explain the relationships between conformity, alienation, and social change.

B2.5 Explain the relationships between poverty, affluence, and social change.

B2.6 Explain the impact of social change on indi­viduals in Canada and on Canadian society.

C1.1 Describe population trends in Canada and around the world.

C1.2 Explain concepts related to demography.

C1.3 Explain the significance of immigration to Canadian society.

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors.

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

C2.3 Explain how increases in population mobility shape social patterns and trends.

C2.4 Explain changes in the nature of work in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Complete an overall assessment of your learning for this unit.
  • Communicate your learning in a variety of ways.
  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
