What have I learned?

 

In summary, social justice embodies principles of fairness and equality, addressing disparities through deterrence methods employed by various institutions. Media, politicians, and social groups often amplify social panic surrounding crime and deviance, shaping public discourse. However, tensions arise when deterring social deviance conflicts with preserving civil liberties, challenging the balance between maintaining order and upholding individual rights. Despite these complexities, social justice remains crucial in designing societies that prioritize fairness, equality, and respect for human rights across all levels of governance and societal interactions.

Learning Goals

  • Understand the systems of social stratification and the social science theories on social inequality in Canada.
  • Appreciate and describe the Canadian criminal justice system and social justice movements throughout the world.
  • Acquire the research and inquiry skills of evaluating and citing sources of information in any social science study.
  • Learn about volunteer opportunities and ways to work toward social justice.
