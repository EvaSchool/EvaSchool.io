What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, globalization, encompassing economic integration and interconnectedness across societies, cultures, and economies worldwide, presents both opportunities and challenges. While it facilitates global distribution of goods and services, enhances economic opportunities, and fosters socio-economic development, it also raises critical concerns regarding human rights, discrimination, poverty, and environmental sustainability.

Various agreements and organizations address human rights issues, but discrimination persists, and low literacy rates globally impact individuals' standard of living. Socio-economic conditions, including unfair exploitation and unfavorable economic structures, exacerbate poverty. Moreover, arrangements between governments and transnational corporations influence development outcomes, often with mixed results for developing nations. 

In Canada, globalization affects economic, social, and environmental dimensions, but it also leads to unfair exploitation and historical injustices perpetuated by government policies. Addressing these challenges is crucial for fostering a more equitable and sustainable global society in the face of globalization's complexities.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
