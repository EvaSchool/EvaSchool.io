What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the examination of the class system in Canada reveals a complex social landscape where various social groups, including the upper class, middle class, working class, and lower class, coexist with varying degrees of influence, wealth, and opportunities. Contrary to the notion of a "classless" society, Canada demonstrates clear social stratification, with each class facing unique challenges and opportunities.

While meritocracy is often touted as a means for individuals to achieve higher status based on personal merit, barriers such as financial constraints, discrimination, and limited access to education and opportunities persist, particularly for marginalized groups. This analysis underscores the importance of understanding the dynamics of social inequality and the impact of various social science theories on shaping our perceptions of class and opportunity in Canadian society.

Learning Goals

  • Understand the systems of social stratification and the social science theories on social inequality in Canada.
  • Appreciate and describe the Canadian criminal justice system and social justice movements throughout the world.
  • Acquire the research and inquiry skills of evaluating and citing sources of information in any social science study.
