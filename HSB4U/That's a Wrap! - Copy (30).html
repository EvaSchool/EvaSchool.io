What have I learned?

 

In summary, the study of social change in social science integrates various theories like functionalism, conflict theory, feminism, cultural materialism, and cognitive theory, each offering distinct perspectives on the drivers of societal transformations.

These theories are complemented by a range of research methodologies including qualitative methods like critical ethnography, participant observation, interviews, and focus groups, as well as quantitative approaches like surveys, field observations, and experiments.

By combining these theories and methodologies, social scientists gain a comprehensive understanding of the complex dynamics of social change, enabling them to address contemporary societal challenges more effectively.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the major social science forces that influence social change.
  • Describe the schools of thought relating to social change.
