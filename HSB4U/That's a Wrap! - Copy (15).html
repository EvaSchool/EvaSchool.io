What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, this lesson on money and power illuminates the intricate interplay between economic forces, societal structures, and demographic trends in Canada and globally.

Through Karl Marx's theories and Antonio Gramsci's concept of hegemony, we explore how population trends, influenced by immigration and socio-economic factors, shape power dynamics within society. Demographic concepts also highlight the significance of population mobility and the role of culture, tradition, and language in shaping social norms.

Furthermore, we examine how changes in the nature of work, such as automation, impact wealth distribution and exacerbate inequalities. This comprehensive understanding underscores the need for nuanced approaches to address social disparities and promote equitable change.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behaviour and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
