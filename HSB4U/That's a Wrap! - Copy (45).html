What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the non-economic aspects of globalization encompass a broad spectrum of influences on world politics, culture, and human rights. Political globalization has led to the formation of international organizations like the United Nations, World Health Organization, and World Bank, which work towards maintaining peace, promoting health, and eliminating poverty globally. However, the impact of globalization on human rights remains contentious, with studies indicating that individuals in developing nations often experience exploitation and violations.

Additionally, cultural globalization and Americanization have raised concerns about the erosion of local traditions and values as Western culture becomes dominant worldwide through mass communication and multinational corporations. Despite the potential benefits of interconnectedness, the complexities of globalization underscore the importance of addressing its non-economic aspects to ensure a fair and equitable global society.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
