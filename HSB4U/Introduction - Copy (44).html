The Changing Canadian Landscape

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will examine the evolving Canadian landscape by analyzing population trends and socio-economic factors. They will explore shifts in population distribution, including rural-suburban-urban migration and the influence of the baby boom generation, while also considering differences between developed and developing nations. Additionally, students will investigate how socio-economic factors such as extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, computers, healthcare, and education shape population trends in Canada, leading to demographic changes and socio-economic disparities. Through this exploration, students will gain insight into the complex interplay between population dynamics and socio-economic factors, enabling them to critically evaluate the impact of these trends on individuals and communities and contribute to discussions on equitable and sustainable development.

Overall Expectations

C1 Demographics: demonstrate an understanding of the importance of demographics as a tool for studying social patterns and trends, both nationally and globally.

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

C1.1 Describe population trends in Canada and around the world (e.g., rural-suburban-urban migration, the baby boom effect, differences between the demographic profiles of developed and developing nations).

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors (e.g., extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, access to computers, access to health care and education). 

Learning Goals

  • Acquire knowledge on the foundations of social change.
  • Demonstrate significant technological forces that influence these changes and learn how they  influence Canadian teenagers.
  • Describe the impact of technological changes to society and how Canadians are affected by these changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to interpret, synthesize and analyze research and apply these skills to your findings.
  • Learn how to use social media to advocate for an issue or cause you care about.
