What have I learned?

 

In summary, the examination of external factors influencing societal change provides insight into the intricate dynamics of human civilization. Drawing upon major theories such as functionalism, conflict theory, feminism, cultural materialism, and cognitive theory, we compare their key ideas to understand the mechanisms driving social change.

Additionally, we explore various psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions such as cognitive dissonance, paradigm shifts, class struggles, tension and adaptation, material and cultural changes, and globalization.

These conditions illuminate the complexities of societal transformations, highlighting the interplay between internal and external forces shaping human societies. As technology continues to play a pivotal role in societal progress, as emphasized by sociologist William Felding Ogburn's seminal concept, its pervasive influence reshapes social structures, cultural norms, and economic systems. 

Thus, a comprehensive understanding of external factors and their interactions offers valuable insights for navigating contemporary challenges and guiding efforts towards positive societal change.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the major social science forces that influence social change.
  • Describe the schools of thought relating to social change.
