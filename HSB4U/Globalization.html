Globalization

 

  • The term became a media buzzword, which denotes both good and bad things.
  • Since globalization is an ongoing process that continues to effect change in the world, it is difficult to define and has come to mean many things.
  • it is the process by which societies, cultures, politics, and economies around the world are becoming increasingly interconnected and integrated.
  • As a result, worldwide patterns of political, economic, and socio-cultural relationships form between nations.

  

 

Economic Globalization

       

  • Globalization, oftentimes referred to as economic globalization is the process of increasing economic integration between countries.
  • This results in the increased free trade of products and services, the growing scale of cross-border trade of commodities and services, and the flow of international capital and technologies.
  • Countries can share capital, production, labor, markets, technology, and resources, which leads to lower prices for distributors and consumers.
  • All of these happen in a free market capitalism, a system where producers are free to enter a line of business at whatever price they can charge; while consumers are free to buy whatever products they want at whatever price they are willing to pay.
  • There are winners, as well as losers.
  • A fully functioning free market economy is considered to be a positive effect of globalization.
  • It leads to the global distribution of the production of goods and services through the reduction of barriers to international trade (e.g., tariffs, export fees, and import quotas).

Positive Effect of Globalization

As there are many positive effects of globalization, there are many factors that negate such results.

Those who are on the other side of the spectrum believe that globalization is a source of economic domination and oppression of the poor, developing nations, by wealthy, developed countries.

  • It does not always lead to economic growth but only highlights the differences between classes in society.

Negative Effect of Globalization

  • Other negative effects of globalization include poor working conditions, income inequality, and possible dangers of financial interdependence among countries.
