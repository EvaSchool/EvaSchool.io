What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the lesson on Wealth and Poverty has provided a comprehensive understanding of various socio-economic factors contributing to income inequality and poverty. Through discussions on income disparity, literacy, employment and education, and the impact of poverty on both individuals and society, we have gained insights into the complex nature of this issue.

Moreover, the lesson highlighted the role of governmental policies and international organizations in addressing poverty, emphasizing the importance of targeted interventions and long-term strategies. Overall, the lesson underscores the significance of collaborative efforts at both individual and systemic levels to combat poverty effectively and foster inclusive economic growth.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
