Challenges to Social Relationships in Adolescence

Lesson Description

This lesson addresses contemporary challenges faced by adolescents in maintaining social relationships amidst technological advancements and socio-economic factors. Students will examine recent technological changes, including the impact of computer technology and the Internet on social networking, and explore how new technologies in various fields affect social structures and interactions. They will also analyze the influence of socio-economic factors such as extracurricular activity costs, transportation access, computer availability, and healthcare and education accessibility on social trends among adolescents. Additionally, students will explore the role of culture, tradition, and language in shaping social behaviors, and gain an understanding of major social science theories related to deviance. Through interactive discussions, students will develop a nuanced understanding of the complexities surrounding social relationships in adolescence and potential strategies for addressing these challenges.

Overall Expectations

B3 Technological Change: demonstrate an understanding of patterns and effects of technological change from a social science perspective

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

C3 Social Deviance: demonstrate an understanding of social science theories about social deviance, and of how various responses to deviance affect individuals and society.

Specific Expectations

B3.1 Identify some recent technological changes and describe how they affect individuals
(e.g., computer technology and the Internet provide extensive opportunities for social networking; many workplace technologies place a physical strain on workers and require ergonomic remedies).

B3.2 Explain how various new technologies (e.g., in medicine, education, entertainment, health and wellness) can affect social structures and interactions.

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors (e.g., extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, access to computers, access to health care and education).

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

C3.1 Describe the key ideas of major social science theories related to deviance (e.g., labelling theory, social control theory, differential association theory, cultural theories).

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
  • Choose an issue you are interested in and develop a campaign to raise awareness about it.
