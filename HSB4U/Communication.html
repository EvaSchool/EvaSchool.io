Communication

Communication has evolved with technology. Though no matter how quick and advanced technology has become, there are still limitations as to what it can do.

Because of these limitations, the ways to communicate have been modified to better understand what is being communicated.

By using fewer characters, other ways of expressing meaning have to be developed.

The Evolution of Language

People all over the world communicate through a universal language. Otherwise, miscommunication and disconnectedness may ensue.

With the so-called online communication, a new language has evolved to suit this type of communication. A new digital dialogue now uses acronyms to save time and to make it easier to type on smartphone and tablet keyboards.

  • An SMS text-base service like Twitter for example has a limit of 280 characters, which makes the use of short forms almost a necessity to get the message across. 
  • Some popular examples are “NVM” for never mind, “LMK” for let me know, even the short form for okay which is OK has even more shortened to just “K.” 

Spell Check: Blessing or Curse?

  • A built-in feature into word processing programs and smartphone applications is the spell checker, that not only detects wrong spelling but also checks the grammar. 
  • In addition to this is the auto-detect software that is integrated into most text-based communication programs that provide possible words or phrases even before you finish typing them. 

Unfortunately, people have become so used in applying short forms of words and abbreviations when communicating through a smartphone, thereby negating the use of proper spelling and grammar.

Spelling accuracy is also affected by the speed by which a recipient expects a response, otherwise, an interest in conversation may be lost.

If proper spelling is not practiced, the skill is reduced. 

Emoticons

American philosopher George Herbert Mead’s understanding of the self shows the importance of socialization and the priority it holds in people’s lives. He believed that the mind plays an important role in the interpretation and processing of social information. 

  • Since a lot of communication nowadays happens through the use of technology, a barrier to communication and interpretation of the self may have been created. 
  • Emoticons were created to bridge this gap.
  • Emoticons (emote icons) are visual representations of a facial expression using punctuation marks and letters.
  • Intend to clarify a tone in an Internet-based communication. 
  • Totally created a new communication language, with the majority of teenagers being fluent and to a degree, disfluent in their own oral and literate language.

  • A challenge for older generations is to catch up with the evolving language of the younger generation to bridge the gap in digital communication.
  • While it’s a challenge for them, it’s a way of having a universal language that everyone understands.
