Welcome to Grade 12 Challenge Change in Society

Welcome to Math Grade 11! This course introduces basic features of the function by extending students' experiences with quadratic relations. It focuses on quadratic, trigonometric, and exponential functions and their use in real-world simulation. Students will represent functions numerically, graphically, and algebraically, simplify expressions, solve equations, and solve application problems. Students will reason mathematically and communicate their thinking as they solve multi-step problems.

Unit One: Challenge of Change

Students will learn to describe the processes of social science research as they relate to the study of change. They will learn to demonstrate and appreciate the study of change as one of the more significant concepts in social sciences today. Students will also know how to develop a detailed research plan to investigate a topic related to social change.

Unit Two: The Challenge of Change at Home: Factors that Shape an Individual

Students will practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information. Students will also learn the forces that shape and influence an individual’s behavior from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence. They will know how to appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.

Unit Three: Social Justice and Inequality: Changes 

Students will learn about the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research. Students will also learn to acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community. Appreciating the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.

Unit Four: Social Justice and Inequality: Challenges

Students will know to describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context. They will learn about the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research. Students will also learn to acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.

Unit Five: Science and Technology: Challenge and Change 

Students will acquire knowledge on the foundations of social change and demonstrate significant technological forces that influence these changes and learn how they influence Canadian teenagers. Students will know how to describe the impact of technological changes on society and how Canadians are affected by these changes. They will develop research and inquiry skills to interpret, synthesize and analyze research and apply these skills to your findings. Students will also learn how to use social media to advocate for an issue or because they care about.

Unit Six - Global Challenge and Change

Students will study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and the ways in which changes related to globalization are felt in Canada. They will learn to describe social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world. Appreciating the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, global and cultural trends and describing how social theorists view global social changes. They will develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research and communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.

Exam

Rich Task Summative

This is a summative task assigned at the end of the course that brings together many of the expectations covered throughout the course and is created to assess and evaluate a student’s ability to demonstrate their understanding of the expectations through not only the product but through conversation and observation.
