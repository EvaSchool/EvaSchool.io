Politics

 

The government of Canada is a constitutional monarchy headed by Queen Elizabeth II. The queen appoints a Governor General to represent her in exercising the Crown’s powers.

As a democratically run government, Canada is headed by a prime minister. Being a full democracy, all Canadian citizens aged 18 or older have the right to vote in the municipal, provincial, and federal elections.

   

Participation Among Young People

It is important to understand the factors that influence political participation to help generate new ways to engage young people in the political process.

While the 18-24-year-olds registered the lowest numbers in all election years and among age groups, the years 2015 and 2019 showed improved numbers from the 2011 federal elections.

One possible explanation for why young Canadians show low turnout rates in elections is they do not feel they have a voice in the political process. To address this issue, politicians use various strategies to encourage young people to vote, like posting their thoughts and ideas on social media to raise the awareness of young and tech-savvy voters. 

The direct correlation between education and political participation is another factor that is being looked at. They say the more education a Canadian has, the more likely he or she will participate in the political process. The idea of social responsibility is encouraged through popular media as well as in the classroom. Classes dealing with the voting process and civic responsibility have been included as compulsory subjects to get a high school diploma.
