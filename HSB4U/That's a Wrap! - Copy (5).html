What have I learned?

In summary, the exploration of generational theories provides a rich understanding of social change dynamics. Karl Mannheim's "fresh contact" concept highlights the unique perspectives of young individuals, while the Strauss-Howe Generational Theory identifies cyclical patterns in generational values. The theory of Generational Replacement emphasizes the crucial role of youth in driving long-term social change. Together, these theories offer insights into the multifaceted nature of social change, encompassing psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions. They also underscore the importance of economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors. Additionally, these theories shed light on diverse methodologies and research designs in social science, providing a nuanced perspective on the means of instigating social change.

Learning Goals

  • Practice research and inquiry skills on the challenges faced by an individual at home by learning the key differences between primary and secondary sources of information
  • Learn the theories that shape and influence an individual’s behaviour from the early stages of socialization and into adolescence.
  • Appreciate and identify the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
