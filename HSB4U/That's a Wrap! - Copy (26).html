What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the study of social inequality delves into the multifaceted nature of disparities in access to wealth, income, and resources among different social groups. Through examining key economic indicators like income and wealth distribution, as well as measures of poverty such as the Low-Income Cut-Off (LICO), we gain insights into the economic challenges faced by individuals and families.

Additionally, understanding the concepts of absolute and relative poverty helps contextualize the experiences of those lacking essential resources compared to others within society. By analyzing these factors, we can develop a more comprehensive understanding of social inequality and its impact on various communities.

Learning Goals

  • Understand the systems of social stratification and the social science theories on social inequality in Canada.
  • Appreciate and describe the Canadian criminal justice system and social justice movements throughout the world.
  • Acquire the research and inquiry skills of evaluating and citing sources of information in any social science study.
  • Learn about volunteer opportunities and ways to work toward social justice.
