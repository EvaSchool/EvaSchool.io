What have I learned?

 

In conclusion, the social construction of ideas encompasses various theories and conditions that drive social change. Major theories like those by Max Weber provide insights into societal transformation, while diverse psychological, social, material, and cultural factors shape these changes.

Social science methodologies facilitate the study of social change, considering economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural influences. Conformity, alienation, and factors like poverty and affluence interact in complex ways, affecting societal dynamics. 

In Canada, social change impacts individuals and society through evolving gender roles, political representation, and equity measures such as affirmative action, highlighting the importance of understanding and addressing its implications.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
