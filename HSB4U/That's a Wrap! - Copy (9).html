What have I learned?

In summary, socialization trends and challenges are shaped by diverse factors and theories of social change, encompassing psychological, social, material, and cultural conditions. Various social science methodologies and research designs elucidate the influence of economic, environmental, political, and sociocultural factors on these trends, while means of creating social change range from grassroots activism to policy reforms. Population trends, demographic concepts, and socio-economic factors contribute to the dynamic nature of societal structures and dynamics. Additionally, culture, tradition, and language play significant roles in shaping social norms and behaviors. An interdisciplinary approach is crucial for understanding and addressing the complexities of socialization trends and challenges in today's globalized society.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Can recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
