Lesson Description

Complete the overall assessment for this course.

Overall Expectations

A3 Assess, record, analyze, and synthesize information gathered through research and inquiry

B2. Demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change;

C3 Demonstrate an understanding of social science theories about social deviance, and of how various responses to deviance affect individuals and society

Specific Expectations

A3.1 Assess various aspects of information gathered from primary and secondary sources (e.g., accuracy, relevance, reliability, inherent values and bias, voice)

B2.1 Describe ways in which influential Canadian leaders have contributed to social change (e.g., Nellie McClung, Agnes Macphail, Tommy Douglas, Lester B. Pearson, Pierre Trudeau, David Suzuki, Phil Fontaine, Roberta Jamieson, Adrienne Clarkson)

B2.2 Explain how various economic, environmental, political, or sociocultural factors (e.g., global warming/climate change, environmental activism, the threat of separatism, leadership changes, pluralism) can lead to social change, and how other factors (e.g., cost, traditional values, fear of negative consequences) can create resistance to change.

B2.3 Explain various means of creating social change (e.g., direct action, protest, advocacy, community organization, revolution, political activism).

C3.1 Describe the key ideas of major social science theories related to deviance (e.g., labeling theory, social control theory, differential association theory, cultural theories).

C3.2 Summarizes and interprets statistics related to social deviance, discrimination, and hate crimes.

C3.3 Describes various methods of deterrence used within local, national, and global institutions and societies.

C3.4 Explain the relationship between social panic about crime and deviance and the attention given to these issues by media, politicians, and other social groups.

Learning Goals

  • Complete an overall assessment of your learning for the course.
  • Communicate your learning in a variety of ways.
