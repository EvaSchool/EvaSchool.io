Non-economic Aspects of Globalization

Lesson Description

This lesson on the non-economic aspects of globalization delves into key provisions of agreements addressing human rights, various types of discrimination, literacy rates' impact, socio-economic conditions' role in poverty, government-corporate arrangements' effects on developing nations, transnational organizations' roles, globalization's impact on Canadian society, unfair exploitation effects, unjust government policies, and environmental changes from fossil-fuel exploitation. Students will analyze these topics to understand globalization's broader implications beyond economic factors.

Overall Expectations

D1 Global Inequalities: demonstrate an understanding of how various social structures and conditions support or limit global inequalities.

D2 Globalization: assess the impact of globalization on individuals and groups.

D3 Exploitation: analyse the impact of unfair or unjust exploitation of people or resources, locally and globally.

Specific Expectations

D1.1 Describe the key provisions of various provincial, national, and international agree­ments for addressing human rights issues.

D1.2 Demonstrate an understanding of various types of discrimination.

D1.3 Summarize the statistics on literacy rates nationally and internationally and assess the impact of low literacy levels on the standard of living of individuals and groups.

D1.4 Explain how various socio-economic condi­tions (e.g., international competition, prejudice, unfavorable economic conditions, military occupa­tion/rule) and structures (e.g., the welfare system, public health and education, non-profit social serv­ice organizations) operate to increase, entrench, or alleviate poverty.

D2.1 Explain various types of arrangements between governments and transnational corporations, including the reasons for such arrangements, and describe their impact on developing nations.

D2.2 Describe the roles of various transnational organizations (e.g., the International Monetary Fund, the World Trade Organization, the World Health Organization) and assess their effective­ness in carrying out their respective mandates.

D2.3 Summarize the impact (e.g., economic, social, environmental) of globalization on Canadian society.

D3.1 Describe the effects of unfair exploitation (e.g., black market sales, human trafficking, the drug trade, human rights violations, use of child labor, expropriation of land) on individuals and groups.

D3.2 Explain ways in which some Canadian gov­ernment policies have resulted in unfair or unjust exploitation of individuals and groups (e.g., policies establishing Aboriginal residential schools and Japanese internment camps; nineteenth-century policies on indentured labor; modern policies related to foreign domestic workers).

D3.3 Identify environmental changes that have resulted from the unchecked exploitation of fossil-fuel resources (e.g., environmental degrada­tion, climate change), and assess the impact of these changes on the well-being of Canadians.

Learning Goals

  • Study the cultural, economic, and political globalization and how changes related to globalization are felt in Canada.
  • Describe how social change and its associated challenges and what it means for Canadians and citizens of the world.
  • Appreciate the topics of multinational corporations, human rights, and global and cultural trends.
  • Describe how social theorists view global social changes.
  • Develop research and inquiry skills to communicate the results of your social science research.
  • Communicate in written or visual form about a global issue that is important to you.
