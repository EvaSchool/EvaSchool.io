Social Challenges for Adolescents

Lesson Description

This lesson delves into the social challenges adolescents encounter, examining population trends in Canada and worldwide, demography concepts like replacement level and demographic transition, socio-economic factors such as extracurricular costs and access to resources, and how culture, tradition, and language influence their experiences. Through this exploration, students gain insights into the complex dynamics shaping adolescent social trends and develop strategies to navigate these challenges effectively.

Overall Expectations

  • B1 Foundations for the Study of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the major theories, perspectives, and methodologies related to social change
  • B2 Causes and Effects of Social Change: demonstrate an understanding of the causes and effects of social change
  • B3 Technological Change: demonstrate an understanding of patterns and effects of technological change from a social science perspective

Specific Expectations

  • C1.1 Describe population trends in Canada and around the world (e.g., rural-suburban-urban migration, the baby boom effect, differences between the demographic profiles of developed and developing nations).
  • C1.2 Explain concepts related to demography (e.g., replacement level, fertility rate, demographic transition), and assess the social impact of demographic change.
  • C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors (e.g., extracurricular activity costs, access to transportation, access to computers, access to health care and education).
  • C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

Learning Goals

  • Appreciate the changes that happen to an individual and how those changes affect the values and attitudes of society at large.
  • Recognize new trends and challenges adolescents and their families face today.
