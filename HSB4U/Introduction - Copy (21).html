Using Technology Wisely

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will explore the prudent use of technology in modern society, considering its impact on various socio-economic factors, cultural influences, population mobility, and the evolving nature of work in Canada.

We will delve into how technological trends are shaped by socio-economic factors such as income inequality, access to resources, and market demands. Additionally, we will analyze the influence of culture, tradition, and language on technological adoption and usage patterns, recognizing their role in shaping societal norms and behaviors. 

Moreover, we will examine how increases in population mobility, facilitated by technological advancements, contribute to the formation of social patterns and trends, particularly in areas such as migration, urbanization, and globalization. 

Lastly, we will discuss changes in the nature of work in Canada driven by technological innovations, including automation, digitalization, and remote work trends, and their implications for the labor market and societal structures. Through this exploration, students will gain a comprehensive understanding of the responsible and effective utilization of technology in contemporary Canadian society.

Overall Expectations

C2 Forces That Shape Social Trends: demonstrate an understanding of how forces influence and shape social patterns and trends.

Specific Expectations

C2.1 Describe how trends are shaped by various socio-economic factors.

C2.2 Describe ways in which culture, tradition, and language influence social trends.

C2.3 Explain how increases in population mobility shape social patterns and trends.

C2.4 Explain changes in the nature of work in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the impact of behavior and how ideas are constructed within the Canadian context.
  • Appreciate the current trends in Canadian society by looking at different theoretical perspectives.
  • Learn the research and inquiry skills of recording and organizing information and data as part of conducting social science research.
  • Acquire an understanding of how to engage and become involved in your school and community.
