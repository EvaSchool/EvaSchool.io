What have I learned?

 

Theoretical explanations of deviance provide valuable insights into understanding why individuals engage in behaviors that deviate from societal norms. From the functionalist perspective, deviance is seen as a natural part of society with both positive and negative consequences.

Strain theory by Robert Merton highlights the role of societal goals and means in shaping deviant behavior, while control theory emphasizes the importance of social bonds in regulating behavior. Symbolic interactionists focus on the role of socialization and labeling theory in shaping deviant identities. 

Finally, the conflict perspective highlights how power dynamics and social inequalities influence the definition and enforcement of deviance. Overall, these theories offer diverse perspectives on deviance and contribute to our understanding of its complex nature within society.
