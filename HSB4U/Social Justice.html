Social Justice

It is traditionally perceived that when there is justice, there is also fair dealing and a sense of equality especially in status, rights, and opportunities.  Hence, social justice means a society that is based on the principles of fairness and equality and that understands and values human rights. 

Origins of Social Justice

The term social justice dates back to the mid-1800s and it was coined by Jesuit scholar Luigi Taparelli (1793-1862).  He prefaced “justice” with “social” to emphasize the social nature of human beings and the importance of various social spheres outside civic government. For him, human beings naturally join together in groups.

Social justice is an important part of many religious faiths.

  • In the Catholic faith, two of its seven key areas of social teachings pertain to social justice: life and dignity of the human person and preferential option for the poor and vulnerable.
  • In the Protestant religion, the propellant of social justice thought and action was the social gospel movement, which placed its emphasis on the application of Christian principles to society’s problems.

Social justice is an important part of many religious faiths.

  • In Islam, one of its five pillars is Zakat, which means alms-giving.
  • In Judaism, the phrase tikkun olam connotes social action, community service, and social justice. 

John Rawls and the Principles of Social Justice

The works of American philosopher John Rawls (1921-2002) provided the secular foundation of social justice by proposing new ways of looking at the relationship between the individual and society. He combined the principles of personal liberty, freedom, and social equality to arrive at a concept of social justice based on fairness.

Rawls drew his theories from the earlier political philosophy of a social contract by which individuals implicitly agree to the terms by which they are governed by society. 

Rawls proposed a theoretical person who is cloaked in a veil of ignorance - ignorant of his/her position in society - and from this original position, must design a just society.

His theoretical person will choose a justice system that adequately provides for those located in the lowest levels of society, guaranteeing justice based on fairness.
