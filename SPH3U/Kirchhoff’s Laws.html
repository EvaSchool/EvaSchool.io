Kirchhoff’s Laws

Callout

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law

In any complete path in an electric circuit, the total electric potential increase at the source(s) is equal to the total electric potential decrease throughout the rest of the circuit.

Series Circuit

The total electric potential across the loads must add up to the electric potential at the source (or sources).

Callout

Parallel Circuit

The electric potential difference across the loads and source must be the same.

Callout

Kirchhoff's Current Law

In a closed circuit, the amount of current entering a junction is equal to the amount of current exiting a junction.

Series Circuit

There is only one possible current.

Callout

Parallel Circuit

The more complete paths there are, the more ways the current can be divided among the paths.
