Uniform and Non- Uniform Motion

Uniform Motion

Uniform or constant velocity involves an object moving at a constant speed in a straight line. This represents the most straightforward form of motion that an object can undergo, aside from when it is at rest.


It's important to note that an object's velocity is considered uniform only when two conditions are satisfied: it maintains a constant speed and moves in a straight line.

A car moving at a straight highway at 100 km/h 

A train traveling along the railroad tracks at a steady or constant speed.

Non-uniform Motion

Motion with non-uniform velocity is motion that is not at a constant speed or not in a straight line. Motion with non-uniform velocity may also be called accelerated motion.

A roller coaster cart descending a curved slope.

The motion of the clock's hands.
