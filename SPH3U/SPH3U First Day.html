SPH3U, Physics, Grade 11 University Preparation

Welcome to SPH3U!

Welcome to SPH3U! This course will help you explore and understand the fundamental principles of physics that govern our universe. You will develop your scientific investigation skills and problem-solving abilities through both theoretical and practical applications. Physics is not only about learning concepts—it’s about developing the ability to think critically, analyze situations, and apply knowledge to solve real-world problems.

In this course, you will study motion, forces, energy, waves, electricity, and magnetism. You will perform experiments, analyze data, and use mathematical models to describe and predict physical phenomena.

Prerequisite: Science, Grade 10, Academic (SNC2D).

SPH3U will challenge you to connect physics concepts to technology, engineering, and societal issues. Through hands-on labs, projects, and collaborative learning, you will strengthen your ability to apply scientific reasoning and communicate your findings effectively.

To succeed in this course, keep up with your readings, complete all assignments on time, and ask questions whenever you need clarification. Physics builds on itself—consistent practice and curiosity are essential.

What You’ll Learn

  • Kinematics: Motion in one and two dimensions, velocity, acceleration, and graphical analysis.
  • Forces: Newton’s laws, friction, dynamics, and applications.
  • Energy in Society: Work, energy transformations, power, and energy efficiency.
  • Waves and Sound: Wave properties, sound production, and wave behaviour.
  • Electricity and Magnetism: Electric circuits, electromagnetism, and technological applications.
