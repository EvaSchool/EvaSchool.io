Thermal Energy VS Heat

Thermal Energy

sum of the potential and kinetic energies possessed by the particles of an object responsible for an object’s warmth or coldness.

  

Heat

         the transfer of thermal energy from a substance with a higher temperature to a substance with a lower       temperature  

  

Always use the word “heat” to mean the TRANSFER of thermal energy, not the thermal energy itself.

Convection current 
a current that occurs when a fluid is continuously heated
caused by warmer, less dense fluid being constantly pushed upward as colder, denser fluid falls downward.

the movement of thermal energy as electromagnetic waves

Thermal Conductor VS Thermal Insulator

Thermal Conductor

a material that is a good conductor of thermal energy allow thermal energy to pass through them relatively easily and quickly.

Thermal Insulator

a material that is a poor conductor of thermal energy do not conduct thermal energy very well includes many types of plastic.
