Now, where is the man’s final position relative to his starting position?          

The starting position is at the origin (0,0). Identify the (x,y) coordinate of the final position relative to the origin. To get the final POSITION, we need to move two units to the right (x=2) and 3 units upward (y=3). The coordinates is at (2,3) or 2 km [E], 3 km [N].

Callout

Lastly, we need to know how far from the original position was the man after his walk.

To find this measurement, we need to draw a straight line directly from the starting position to the man’s final position. Is there a way to calculate the length of this line?

 

Callout

A right triangle was formed, which means we can use the Pythagorean Theorem to determine the length of the hypotenuse. In this case, the hypotenuse represents the man’s DISPLACEMENT, the change in his position.

What is the magnitude of the man’s displacement?

 

   

 Concepts

Tabs

DistanceDisplacement

Imagine that you have a piece of string and you have tied it to your starting position and as you move, the string uncurles and is left behind you so it traces out how far you have walked. This is a measure of the total length of the string, with the base unit expressed in meters.

The string is fixed at the start and uncoils as you walk (same as before) only now when you stop you pull on the rope until it is taught (so there is no slack between the start and end points) and this is the displacement. It is the straight-line distance between the start and end points, with the base unit expressed in meters.

Callout

Example

You ran around an oval track 400 meters in length. If your starting point is the same as where you stopped, what is the distance you ran and your displacement?

   

Distance

You ran the whole track which is 400 meters in length, so the distance is 400 meters.

Displacement

Since the initial and final positions are on the same point, the displacement is
0 meters.
