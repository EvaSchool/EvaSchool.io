A light car with a powerful engine that can exert a large force will experience a large acceleration.

            

Massive truck experiences small accelerations even if the force is large.

              

A small economy car experiences smaller accelerations because the force its engine exerts is smaller.

Newton's Second Law of Motion

If the net external force on an object is not zero, the object will accelerate in the direction of this net force. The magnitude of the acceleration is directly proportional to the magnitude of the net force and inversely proportional to the mass of the object. We can represent Newton’s Second Law of Motion mathematically as:

The graph of net force versus acceleration is linear, and the slope represents the mass. The line must pass through the origin since when the acceleration is zero, the net force must also be zero.

 

The graph of acceleration versus mass is a reciprocal function.
