That's a Wrap!

Learning Goals

  • Learn what magnetic fields are, how to represent them, and some applications of them.
  • Learn how a current-carrying wire creates a magnetic field when a current flows through the conductor.
  • Understanding how solenoids work.
  • Describing the magnetic field of solenoids.
  • Understanding how a magnetic field applies a force to a current-carrying wire when a current flows through it.
  • Describing the design of motors.
  • Understanding how DC motors work.
  • Find ways on how to improve the design.

Success Criteria

I can…

  • Learn what magnetic fields are, how to represent them and some applications of them.
  • Learn how a current carrying wire creates a magnetic field when a current flows through the conductor.
  • Explain how solenoids work.
  • Draw the direction of the magnetic field lines around the solenoid.
  • Explain how a magnetic field applies a force to a current carrying wire when a current flows through it.
  • Demonstrate the direction of the force applied in a motor using the right hand rule.
  • Explain how direct current motors work.
  • Devise ways on how to improve direct current motors.
