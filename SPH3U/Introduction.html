Chapter 1:  

Motion in a Straight Line, introduces fundamental concepts of one-dimensional kinematics. Students learn about displacement, velocity, acceleration, and time in linear motion. They gain skills in graph interpretation and problem-solving, with real-world applications. This chapter forms a strong basis for advanced physics understanding and practical applications of one-dimensional motion. 

Distance Position and Displacement and Speed and Velocity Learn how to distinguish between distance and displacement, key concepts in describing an object's motion. Understand how direction plays a crucial role in displacement measurement. Explore the difference between speed and velocity, and how they relate to motion. Discover how direction affects velocity, making it a vector quantity.
Acceleration Uncover the concept of acceleration, which describes changes in an object's velocity over time. Understand how positive and negative accelerations impact motion.
Comparing Graphs of Linear Motion and Five Key Equations for Motion with Uniform Acceleration Learn to interpret position-time and velocity-time graphs to analyze and compare different types of linear motion scenarios. Master the five fundamental equations that describe the motion of objects under uniform acceleration. Use these equations to solve various motion problems efficiently. 
Acceleration Near Earth’s Surface Explore how gravity affects objects near Earth's surface and discover how acceleration due to gravity plays a vital role in free-fall and projectile motion.
