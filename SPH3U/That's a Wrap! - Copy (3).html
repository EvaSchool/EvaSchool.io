Learning Goals

Learn how to describe electrical energy use and how efficient various methods of generation are.

  • Understanding how energy is transferred.
  • Measuring the ability of objects to transfer energy.
  • Understanding what is current.
  • Measuring the current in a circuit.
  • Understanding the laws of current and voltage split.
  • Applying Kirchhoff's Laws to simple and complex circuits.
  • Understanding what is electrical resistance. 
  • Using Ohm’s Law to solve problems regarding resistance. 
  • Applying Ohm’s Law in real life.
  • Understanding the relationship of resistance and current.
  • Applying Kirchhoff's Laws and Ohm’s Law to solve the equivalent resistance of the circuit.
  • Integrating the Ohm’s Law and Kirchhoff’s Laws in solving complex circuits.

Success Criteria

I can…

  • Learn how to describe electrical energy use and how efficient various methods of generation are.
  • Explain how energy is transferred.
  • Compute for the electric potential difference.
  • Explain what is current.
  • Measure current in a circuit.
  • Explain the laws of voltage and current split.
  • Solve problems using Kirchhoff’s Laws.
  • Explain what is electrical resistance.
  • Solve problems using Ohm’s Law.
  • Give real life applications of Ohm’s Law.
  • Describing the relationship of resistance and current.
  • Solving for the equivalent resistance of the circuit.
  • Solve complex circuits.
