Distance Position and Displacement and Speed and Velocity

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we'll delve into the fundamental principles of motion. You'll gain a clear understanding of the distinctions between distance and displacement, essential concepts in describing how objects move. We'll also emphasize the critical role that direction plays in accurately measuring displacement. Furthermore, we'll explore the contrast between speed and velocity, unraveling their relationship to the dynamics of motion. 

Overall Expectations

B2. Investigate, in qualitative and quantitative terms, uniform and non-uniform linear motion, and solve related problems; 

B3. demonstrate an understanding of uniform and non-uniform linear motion, in one and two dimensions.

C1. Reading Comprehension: Determine meaning in a variety of texts in Chinese, using a range of reading comprehension strategies.

Specific Expectations

B2.1 Use appropriate terminology related to kinematics, including, but not limited to: time, distance, position, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration

 B2.2 Analyse and interpret position–time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time-time-time–time graphs of motion in one dimension (e.g., use tangent slopes to create velocity–time graphs from position-time graphs and acceleration-time-time-time–time graphs from velocity–time graphs; use the area under the curve to create position–time graphs from velocity-time graphs and velocity-time graphs from acceleration–time graphs)

 B3.1 Distinguish between the terms constant, instantaneous, and average with reference to speed, velocity, and acceleration, and provide examples to illustrate each term

B3.2 Distinguishes between, and provides examples of, scalar and vector quantities as they relate to the description of uniform and non-uniform linear motion (e.g., time, distance, position, velocity, acceleration)

Learning Goals

Understand how distance and displacement are related to each other.

Understand the difference between a vector and a scalar quantity.

Understand how to work with average speed and velocity. Work with uniform and non-uniform motion.

Understand the difference between a vector and a scalar quantity.
