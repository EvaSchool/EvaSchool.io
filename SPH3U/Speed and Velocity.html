Average Velocity

The average velocity of an object in motion is its total displacement or change in position, divided by the total time taken for the motion.

Uniform MotionNon-uniform Motion

Motion with uniform or constant velocity is moving at a constant speed in a straight line. It is the simplest type of motion that an object can undergo, except for being at rest.

Note that both requirements (constant speed and straight line) must be met for an object’s velocity to be uniform.

Motion with non-uniform velocity is a motion that is not at a constant speed or not in a straight line. Motion with non-uniform velocity may also be called accelerated motion.
