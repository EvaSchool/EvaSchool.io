That's a Wrap!

In Summary:

Distance 

Distance is the total length of the path traveled by an object.

Distance is a scalar quantity (magnitude only) and can never be negative.

Displacement

Displacement is the change in an object's position from its initial to its final location.

Displacement is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction.

Average Speed

Average Speed is the total distance travelled divided by the total time elapsed.

Average Velocity

Average Velocity of an object in motion is its total displacement, or change in position, divided by the total time taken for the motion. 

Uniform Motion

The object has uniform velocity if it maintains a constant speed and moves in a straight line. 

Non-uniform Motion:

An object has non-uniform velocity when its speed is not constant or when it does not follow a straight-line path.
