Gravitational Force Near Earth

Gravity is an action-at-a-distance force that pulls on objects without making any contact with them. This occurs because Earth is surrounded by a gravitational force field.

Callout

Force field

- a region of space surrounding an object that can exert a force on other objects that are placed within that region and are able to interact with that force

 

To determine the magnitude of Earth’s gravitational force field at a particular location near its surface, physicists use a quantity called gravitational field strength.

Gravitational field strength

- the force per unit mass acting on an object when placed in a gravitational field

- a vector quantity because it has a DIRECTION

- the gravitational field strength due to Earth always points toward Earth’s center

- has units of newtons per kilogram

At Earth’s surface, the gravitational field strength is 9.8 N/kg [down]. Notice that this has the same magnitude as the acceleration due to gravity at Earth’s surface. What does it mean?

The gravitational field strength at a location has the same magnitude as the acceleration due to gravity at that location.

Weight is a vector, and its magnitude is measured in newtons with a spring scale or force sensor.

Callout

On the Moon or a planet other than Earth, your weight is different but your mass is the same. For example, the magnitude of the gravitational field strength on the surface of the Moon is approximately one-sixth of that on Earth’s surface. This means that the force of gravity is weaker on the Moon and the magnitude of your weight is less. However, your body contains the same amount of matter at either location, so your mass is unchanged.
