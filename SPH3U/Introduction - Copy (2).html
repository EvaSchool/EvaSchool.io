Comparing Graphs of Linear Motion

Lesson Description

Welcome to today's lesson, where we'll explore two essential aspects of motion in physics. First, we'll learn how to interpret motion graphs, providing valuable insights into an object's movement. Then, we'll master the five key equations that govern motion with uniform acceleration. These skills will enhance your understanding of the physical world and empower you to analyze various scenarios involving motion. Let's get started!

Overall Expectations

B2. Investigate, in qualitative and quantitative terms, uniform and non-uniform linear motion, and solve related problems.

B3. demonstrate an understanding of uniform and non-uniform linear motion, in one and two dimensions.

Specific Expectations

B2.1 use appropriate terminology related to kinematics, including, but not limited to time, distance, position, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration. 

B2.2  analyze and interpret position–time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time–time graphs of motion in one dimension (e.g., use tangent slopes to create velocity–time graphs from position–time graphs and acceleration-time–time graphs from velocity–time graphs; use the area under the curve to create position–time graphs from velocity–time graphs and velocity–time graphs from acceleration–time graphs);

B3.1 distinguish between the term's constant, instantaneous, and average with reference to speed, velocity, and acceleration, and provide examples to illustrate each term.

B3.2 distinguishes between, and provides examples of, scalar and vector quantities as they relate to the description of uniform and non-uniform linear motion (e.g., time, distance, position, velocity, acceleration).
