Projectile Motion    

Lesson Description

This section delves into the intricate dynamics of projectile motion, elucidating the graphical representation of such motion. Students will gain a comprehensive understanding of handling the two independent components of projectile motion and integrating them to derive comprehensive solutions. Emphasis will be placed on calculating the displacement and velocity of a projectile at any given point using equations of motion, thus equipping students with the necessary tools to address real-world problems involving projectile motion.

Overall Expectations

B2. investigate, in qualitative and quantitative terms, uniform and non-uniform linear motion, and solve related problems;

B3. demonstrate an understanding of uniform and non-uniform linear motion, in one and two dimensions.

Specific Expectations

B2.8 use kinematic equations to solve problems related to the horizontal and vertical components of the motion of a projectile (e.g., a cannon ball shot horizontally off a cliff, a ball rolling off a table, a golf ball launched at a 45-degree angle to the horizontal) [AI, C]

B3.3 describe the characteristics and give examples of a projectile’s motion in vertical and horizontal planes.

Learning Goals

  1. Understanding how to represent projectile motion graphically.
  2. Understand how to handle the two independent components of the motion and interlink them to find a solution.
  3. Calculate displacement and velocity of a projectile at any point in the motion from a word problem using equations of motion.

Success Criteria

  1. Represent projectile motion graphically.
  2. Use two independent components of the motion and interlink them to find a solution.
  3. Calculate displacement and velocity of a projectile at any point in the motion from a word problem using equations of motion.
