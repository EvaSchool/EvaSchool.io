Electrostatic Force and the Strong Nuclear Force

Tabs

Electrostatic ForceStrong Nuclear Force

The force of attraction or repulsion due to electric charges. Like charges repel, and opposite charges attract. This explains why the positively charged nucleus of an atom attracts negatively charged electrons. It cannot, however, explain how the nucleus itself is held together.

Responsible for holding the nucleus together. Like gravity, and unlike the electrostatic force, the strong nuclear force is always attractive and helps hold together the neutrons and protons in the nucleus of an atom.

The strong nuclear force is much stronger than the electrostatic force. The strong nuclear force is responsible only for holding the nucleus of an atom together.

Stable and Unstable Isotopes

When these forces are balanced, an atom is said to be stable. Atoms with higher atomic numbers (more protons) experience a greater electrostatic force of repulsion among the protons, and the protons become more separated. This separation results in a weakening of the strong nuclear force. Additional neutrons add to the strong nuclear force to balance the increasing electrostatic repulsion.

An unstable atom with a nucleus that can spontaneously disintegrate is said to be radioactive. The process by which a radioactive atom spontaneously breaks apart to form smaller atoms is called radioactive decay.
