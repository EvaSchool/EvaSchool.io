Understanding Relative Pronoun: Who and Whom

In the previous lesson, we discussed about the relative clauses who, that, and which. Now, we are going to discuss about the relative clauses who and whom. 

WHO VS. WHOM

Whom should be used to refer to the object of a verb or preposition. 

When in doubt, try this simple trick: If you can replace the word with “he”’ or “’she,” use who. If you can replace it with “him” or “her,” use whom. 

Example: 
Lea brought Sheila. 
Lea: Subject, can be replaced with the pronoun she.
Sheila: Object, can be replaced with the pronoun her.

WHO

Who should be used to refer to the subject of a sentence.
Example: Who brought Sheila?
Lea brought Sheila.

WHOM

Whom should be used to refer to the object of a verb or preposition. 
Example: Whom brought Sheila? 
I brought Sheila.
