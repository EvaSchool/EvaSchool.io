The line from page 15: “The camera crews perched like buzzards on rooftops, only add to the effect,” used a type of figurative language called a simile. 

A simile is a figurative language that compares two separate concepts using connecting words such as “like” or “as.” The use of simile in this part of the story helped create a visual image of the scene. 

Identify the figurative language being used on page 27: “I stood mesmerized by the heat and the luscious scent until the rain interfered, running its icy fingers down my back, forcing me back to life.”
