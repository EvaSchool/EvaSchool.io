That's a Wrap!

  • In shortening relative clauses, sometimes, it is okay to not include the relative pronoun in a relative clause.
  • You cannot take out the relative pronoun if it is the subject of the relative clause.
  • If it is a defining clause, the relative pronoun (e.g. that) could be removed.
  • If it is a non-defining clause, the relative pronoun (e.g. which) cannot be removed.
  • The word whom is not being used commonly anymore. But, you could still use it depending on the circumstances. Whom is used in formal circumstances.
  • Which could refer to the noun immediately before it or could also refer to the entire clause before it. 
  • Which and That can be used when referring to the noun immediately before it.
  • Which can refer to an entire clause. Oftenly informal use to give an opinion or the sum of the idea.
  • When is used to indicate time.  
  • Why is used to indicate reason.
  • Where determines the place of an action.
