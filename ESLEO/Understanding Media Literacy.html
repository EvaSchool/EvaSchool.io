Understanding Media Literacy

Let's watch this quick video about Media Literacy.

What is media literacy?

  • the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and communicate various media messages  

What is a media message?

  • Are the pieces of information different media share, promote, or project
  • Media messages can range from your day-to-day conversations to subtle cultural stereotypes.
  • Anything you consume from the Media (the Internet, TV, your phone, etc.)  is a Media message. 

The Importance of Media Literacy

Why is it important to study media literacy?

  • It empowers students to engage with media healthily.
  • It helps people in making informed decisions and choices in their daily lives.
  • It makes people responsible for media messengers and content creators. 

Like any other analysis, there is a process in becoming Media literate. This starts with the 5 essential Media Literacy Questions! 
Media Literacy Questions: How to Think Critically About Media

Who created this message?

  • What is the agenda of this message?
  • Someone makes all media with a particular vision and background. They have a goal to reach through their message!

What techniques are used to attract my attention?

  • How will this piece of media hold your attention?
  • The media uses taglines, visuals, colors, celebrity endorsers, and even music to grab hold of your attention. 

How might different people interpret this message?

  • How will different audiences react to the same message? 

Why will audiences react differently to the same message?

Explain your answer. 

  • There are many ways to interpret a message, depending on who is reading it.
  • One’s background can affect your interpretation of the media. 

Which lifestyles, values, and points of views are represented--or missing?

  • Media messages are embedded with values and points of view.
  • Consider the impact of unheard voices.
