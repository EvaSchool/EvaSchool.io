The step that takes the most time will differ from movie to movie. Animated films take the most time to produce, while high CGI films will probably take the most time to post-produce.

PRE-PRODUCTION

  • “is the planning process and execution of every task that must take place before production begins” (Studio Binder  2020)
  • Think of it as an entire production crew’s insurance policy! 

Why could it be like a Media Production’s insurance policy? 

  • It makes sure everything about the project falls into place.
  • Good pre-production saves time, energy, and resources for the entire production. 

Pre-Production Tasks

  • Finishing the Script
  • Finalizing the budget
  • Hiring actors, production crew, and more people that can help production
  • Storyboarding scenes
  • Planning where to shoot or record
  • Scheduling shoot days and getting all shooting permits necessary
  • Renting equipment and more! 

All script must have:

  • Script Title
  • Director/Writer’s Names
  • Treatment: the script’s outline in paragraph form
  • (Optional) Scene Numbers
  • (Optional) Duration
  • An Audio column: Contains the lines, sound effects, and music of the script.
  • A Visual column: Contains the shots and (sometimes) storyboard of the script

PRODUCTION

  • Filming and recording of media begins.
  • If you are making a film, this is where hair and makeup come in.
  • Camera operators, directors, light operators, sound operators, and more work together to make shots beautiful!
  • Rehearsals start and are executed.

Production Team

DIRECTOR: Oversees the shooting and assembly of a media product
CINEMATOGRAPHER (for visual media): Pays attention to lighting and the camera's technical capabilities 
ACTORS/TALENT: Responsible for portraying the characters in a film or is the “Star” of the show 
PRODUCTION DESIGNER: Translates the script into visual form;  director's visual guide 
EDITOR: Decides what shots and scenes to use in the final video
SCREENWRITER: Writes the dialogue, shots, and scenes of a video

POST PRODUCTION

  • when the audio and visual materials are cut together to create the final media product
  • happens after shooting and/or recording is complete
  • Editors can add effects, music, and other sound effects during post-production.
