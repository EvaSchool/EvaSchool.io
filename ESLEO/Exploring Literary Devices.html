Exploring Literary Devices

In the previous lesson, we learned about figurative language, which is also a literary device.

In this lesson, we will focus on literary devices and how an author can use them to convey meaning or create an overall mood for their story.

Let’s read the story “The Masque of Red Death” by Edgar Allan Poe, and let’s analyze how the author uses literary devices in developing the story.

There are two types of literary devices

Literary elements are elements that exist within the story. (plot, setting, theme, characters, etc.)
Literary techniques are structures that writers use to give their readers a better understanding of what they are trying to say. (e.g. allusion, foreshadowing, irony, etc.)

FORESHADOWING

This is the use of hints early on in the story to indicate something that will happen.
For example:

  • Worrying foreshadows something that is yet to come.
  • Showing a gun early in a story and explaining it foreshadows something that will happen with the gun.

What is being foreshadowed in this statement: 

“The external world could take care of itself. In the meantime it was folly to grieve or to think. The prince had provided all the appliances of pleasure. ... there were musicians, there was Beauty, there was wine. All these and security were within. Without was the ‘Red Death.’”

ALLUSION

This is used to make an indirect reference to another person or another artistic work.
It is used to create an idea about an object by relating it to another.
For example:

  • Someone might say “No sweat, Sherlock” after finishing something - alluding to Sherlock Holmes’s ability to solve problems quickly.
  • The novel The Sound and the Fury alludes to a line in Macbeth by Shakespeare, which sets his story’s mood.

Specific allusions in the story may not be relatable to the reader today.

IRONY

An irony is a difference between what is meant and what is said. The intended meaning is opposite to the literal meaning.
It’s a contrast between how things might seem and how things really are.

For example:
You’re nicer than the devil.

In the story, why can it be said that Prince Prospero’s name is an irony? 

Aside from this, what other statement in the story shows irony?

FOIL

A foil is a character that contrasts another character (usually the protagonist) to emphasize certain aspects of the more prominent character.

  • For example, in the anime Naruto:
    Naruto’s foil is Sasuke. With Sasuke being a direct contrast to Naruto’s character.

ALLEGORY

These are literary techniques that use symbolism to hide what the author is trying to say.

For example,

In the movie Avatar, the homeworld of the aliens “Pandora Woods” represents the Amazon rainforest - wherein industries are trying to capitalize on the natural resources.

IMAGERY

The use of figurative language to create visual imagery. It uses simile, metaphor, personification, etc., to create a better image for the reader.

For example:
The thunder was roaring so loud.

Participate in the discussion forum: Give examples of imageries used by the author. Based on your understanding, explain why that imagery was used.

SYMBOLISMS

This is the use of an action to imply something more than what is shown.
For example:
In the book A Song of Ice and Fire, the direwolves are a symbolism of the fate of members of House Stark.
