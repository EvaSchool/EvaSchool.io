Exploring Everyday English: Who are you?

English has a lot of applications. One of the very first uses we have for it is in introducing ourselves.

 

Who are you?

When faced with this question, we would probably start with our name, where we are from and our occupation.

Have you ever stopped to think about other ways to answer this question aside from the above? What if someone asks for more than our name? Indeed, who are we?

General Tips in Introducing Yourself

  

Open All Panels

State who you are clearly.

Do not mumble, or else the person you’re talking to might have to ask again.

Be friendly

Introduce yourself in a way that is approachable and smiles. You can start with “Hi there, I’m…” or “Hello, I am…”

Know your audience and context

Depending on who you are talking to, you may have to change your approach. For example, in informal situations like an interview don’t want to start with a joke about yourself.

Use body language

It’s always best to stand if you’re meeting somebody for the first time and you were initially sitting down.

Give only relevant information

You don’t always have to give your life story when someone asks who you are. Learn to listen and read the room, so you don’t give too little or too much information.
