That's a Wrap!

  • Freedom is the power to act, speak, or think without restraint. 
  • A right is something that every person deserves that cannot be taken away from them. 
  • A responsibility is something that is expected of you and that benefits everyone. 
  • The Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms sets out those rights and freedoms that Canadians believe are necessary for a free and democratic society.
  • Democratic Rights gives you the right to vote in federal, provincial or territorial elections, along with the right to stand for public office themselves. 
  • Mobility Rights: All Canadians have the right to enter, stay in and leave Canada.
  • Legal Rights provides a broad set of legal rights that protect us in our dealings with the justice system. 
  • Equality Rights: Everyone has the right no to be discriminated against by the government based on race, religion, sex, abilities, origin or sex orientation. 
  • Official Language Rights: English and French both have equal status, rights, and privileges regarding their use in all Parliament and Government of Canada institutions. 
  • Minority language & education rights: The Charter requires that provincial governments provide education to their citizens in the minority official language where numbers warrant.
