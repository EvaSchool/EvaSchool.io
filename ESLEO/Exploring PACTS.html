Exploring PACTS

Let’s analyze the story using PACTS! 

  • Plot
  • Atmosphere
  • Characterization
  • Theme
  • Setting

PLOT

The plot is the main event of the story. It is made up of 6 parts.

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Exposition

The story’s introduction; includes the primary characters, theme, setting, and time.

Conflict

This is the primary problem that is faced by the characters. It drives the plot of the story.

Rising action

These are the events that lead to the climax of the story.

Climax

The climax is the peak of the story. It is the moment where the main characters reach or fail their goal.

Falling action

The falling action shows everything that happens as a consequence of the climax, where unanswered questions are wrapped-up.

Resolution

The resolution completes the story.

The Plot of “Belling the Cat” 

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Exposition

The Mice once called a meeting to decide on a plan to free themselves of their enemy, the Cat. At least they wished to find some way of knowing when she was coming, so they might have time to run away.

Conflict

Indeed, something had to be done, for they lived in such constant fear of her claws that they hardly dared stir from their dens by night or day.

Rising action

Many plans were discussed, but none of them was thought good enough.

Climax

At last, a very young Mouse got up and said: "I have a plan that seems very simple, but I know it will be successful. All we have to do is to hang a bell about the Cat's neck. When we hear the bell ringing, we will know immediately that our enemy is coming."

Falling action

All the Mice were much surprised that they had not thought of such a plan before. But in the midst of the rejoicing over their good fortune, an old Mouse arose and said:

Resolution

"I will say that the plan of the young Mouse is very good. But let me ask one question: Who will bell the Cat?"

 

ATMOSPHERE

The atmosphere is the mood of the story.

  • Was the tone of the story sad, happy, or funny?

The atmosphere describes the tone of the story from the beginning until the end.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: What was the atmosphere of the short story “Belling the Cat”?  What was the mood set by the descriptions of the writer?

CHARACTERIZATION

  • Identify the protagonist and/or antagonist of the story.
  • Identify the main characters in the story and describe how they were portrayed.

There are four types of characterizations:

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Round

These characters tend to be more fully developed and described than flat or static characters. They will feel like a person you know very well.

Flat

These are minor characters who do not undergo substantial change or growth in the course of the story.

Dynamic

These are characters that change in the course of the story.

Static

These characters are inversely related to dynamic characters. The term static refers to the fact that these characters don’t change in the story’s course.

 

Participate in the Discussion Forum: Identify the protagonists and/or antagonists in the story. Determine their characterization.

THEME

The theme is the central message of the story.

It is the message of the author to the readers. It is also the underlying message of a story.

For example:

  • Love, war, betrayal.
  • A film can contain many different themes.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: Identify the theme of “Belling the Cat.” Describe what the author is trying to tell you in your own words.

SETTING AND TIME

The setting is the time and place that the story is set in. It initiates the mood of the story.

For example,
The setting of The Lord of the Rings is in a medieval middle-earth, a fictional world filled with magical creatures.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: Identify the setting for “Belling the Cat.”
