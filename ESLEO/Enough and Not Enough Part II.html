Enough and Not Enough Part II

You have already learned how to use the word enough. Let’s continue learning how to use it effectively.

Let’s learn through this set of examples:- The game costs $60
- Matt has $50. He doesn’t have enough money to buy the game.
- Sean has $60. He has enough money to buy the game.
- James has $120. He has more than enough to buy the game.

See the difference?

“More than enough”

More than enough is used in the last sentence if the amount greater than that was said (the price)
Example:

 Your friend texted you and said to you that she will be with 2 of your friends and they love pizza. You buy 2 boxes of pizza, each has 8 slices of pizza. 

What will you reply?

It is more than enough!

Using “too”

  • Having more than the asked amount is great. But sometimes, it is not.

Why?

Let’s have some samples:
- I love to go to the mall, but I am too sleepy to go.
- We should save up some money, having too expenses is bad for our business.
- Eating too fast is not good for you.

  • The word too is relative to the speaker.
  • It means that if a food is good for you, it may not be for others.

“So” and “Too”

  • Sometimes the word too is relative 
    but it also shows that the sense is negative.
  • Using the word so makes the adjective stronger but both words makes the sentence negative.

Examples:
The newly opened restaurant has so many people.
The newly opened restaurant has too many people.

“Too much” + Verb

  • When using too much, don’t use another adverb

Examples:
She works too much hard - The underlined word should not be used
She works too hard or She works too much - Correct sentence
