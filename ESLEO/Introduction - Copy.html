Canadian Symbols

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will learn about Canadian Symbols.

Overall Expectations

B1 Read and demonstrate understanding of a variety of texts for different purposes.
B4 Locate and extract relevant information from written and graphic texts for a variety of purposes.
C3 Use correctly the conventions of written English appropriate for this level, including grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation.
D2 Demonstrate an understanding of the rights and responsibilities of Canadian citizenship, and of the contributions of diverse groups to Canadian society.
D4 Demonstrate an understanding of, interpret, and create a variety of media texts.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Read a wide variety of authentic texts of increased complexity on a range of topics.
B4.1 Locate information from a wide variety of print and electronic sources.
C3.1 Use correctly the grammatical structures and conventions of written English appropriate for this level. 
D2.1 Discuss some aspects of Canadian-American relations since World War II.
D4.1 View, read, and listen to a variety of media texts, and explain some ways in which they influence society.
D4.3 Create a variety of media texts for specific purposes and audiences and explain how the purpose and audience influenced their design decisions and language choice

Learning Goals

  • Read and demonstrate their understanding of the different national symbols of Canada.
  • Create or improve an existing Canadian symbol and explain the reasons that influenced their design decision.
  • Use correct written English conventions, including grammar, usage, spelling and punctuation, in their explanation.
