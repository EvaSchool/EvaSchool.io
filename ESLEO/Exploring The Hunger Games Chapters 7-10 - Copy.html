Exploring The Hunger Games Chapters 7-10


In Chapters 7-10 of The Hunger Games, we are immersed in Suzanne Collins’s dystopian world. Katniss and Peeta train start to train and devise their strategies to survive. 

Read The Hunger Games Chapters 7-10.

 

Chapters 7-9

Why would meeting the other tributes make Katniss sick to her stomach?

What is Peeta’s assessment of Katniss’s skill with a bow? How does Katniss react? 

 

What do you think Peeta means by this comment: “she has no idea the effect she can have.”

What does Haymitch demand of the District 12 tributes in public? In your opinion, why does he do this? Was it a smart decision on his part? Why or why not?

 

Why did the tributes from weather districts have an advantage over other tributes? Why did Katniss think she was at least equal to tributes from other poor districts? 

Explain the skills that were taught at the Training Center. What gift was Peeta got at? Why? 

Do you think it was a smart idea for Katniss to show her archery skills to the Gamemakers? Why did she show them in the way she did? Do you think this was a good idea? Why or why not? 

 

What does Katniss believe will be the consequences of her actions in her private audition with the Gamemakers? 

What is the significance of the score given to each tribute? What is Peeta and Katniss’s score? 

Context Clues

Context clues are words or phrases surrounding a word that help the reader to figure out the meaning of a word. 

 In Chapter 8, choose two words that you are unsure about. Using context clues, what do you think is the meaning of each word? Then, use a dictionary to locate and write the actual definition of each word.

What role does Gale serve for Katniss? How does that compare/contrast with Peeta? 

Why do you think Peeta requests to be trained separately from Katniss? 

Explain Katniss’s conflicting thoughts about Peeta at the beginning of Chapter 9. 

 

What problem does Katniss have with her training with Effie? What problem does she have with her training with Haymitch? 

Why is it so important for Katniss and Peeta to present a likeable image on the televised interview? What advice does Cinna offer about presenting herself in public?

Verbs

A verb is an action word. If it is something that you “do,” it is a verb. Verbs often end in -ing or -ed. 

Examples: ran, walking, smiling, poked, saw, opened, whispered, took, etc. 

Verbs also include: is, was, were, has, have, said, etc. 

Participate in the Discussion Forum: List 10 verbs or verb phrases from the first full paragraph of chapter 9.

Remember, each sentence should have at least one verb.
