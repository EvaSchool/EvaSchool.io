Lesson 4 - Canadian-American Relations Since World War II

The relationship between Canada and the United States, two neighbouring North American nations, is one of the most significant bilateral relationships in the world. Since World War II, this relationship has evolved and been influenced by political, economic, social, and cultural factors.

Canadian-American relations since World War II have been characterized by a complex interplay of cooperation and challenges. While the two countries share extensive economic ties, security commitments, and cultural connections, they have also faced disagreements on various fronts.
