Canadian Symbols

A symbol is a kind of picture that tells us something without using words. 

In this lesson, we will focus on the official symbols of Canada. Which Canadian symbols are you are already familiar with? 

BEAVER

On March 24, 1975, the beaver was given the official status symbol of Canada’s sovereignty. This industrious rodent can be seen on coins and countless logos across the country.

COAT OF ARMS

Here is the present design of the Arms of Canada.

Coats of arms are used to distinguish allies from enemies on a battlefield. 

After the First World War, Canada adopted an official coat of arms and national motto as an expression of national pride. His Majesty King George V proclaimed this in 1921.

Design of the Canada Coat of Arms

Mrs. Cathy Bursey-Sabouri drew the present design of the Arms of Canada. The design includes symbols of England, France, Scotland, and Ireland and maple leaves. 

Today, you can find the coat of arms on dollar bills, government documents and public buildings. 

MAPLE LEAF TARTAN

The Maple Leaf Tartan was declared an official symbol on March 9, 2011. David Weiser created it in 1964 for the 100th anniversary of Canada’s Confederation. 

Participate in the chat: What do the colours green, gold, red and brown remind you of?

The tartan’s colour pattern represents the changing colour of the maple leaf throughout seasons. 

The Maple Leaf Tartan is a symbol of national pride and has been worn by Canadians from all backgrounds. 

 

Callout

MAPLE TREE

 

The Maple Tree was officially recognized as one of Canada’s symbols in 1996. Maple trees contribute valuable wood products and sustain the maple sugar industry. 

 

NATIONAL ANTHEM

On July 1, 1980, “O Canada” was proclaimed as Canada’s national anthem. The music was composed by Calixa Lavallée, a well-known composer born in East Canada.

NATIONAL FLAG

Canada’s flag is red and white. In the center of the flag is a red maple leaf. The background is white. There is a red bar on each side of the flag. The bar on the right stands for the east coast of Canada. The Atlantic Ocean is on the east coast. The bar on the right stands for the east coast of Canada. The Pacific Ocean is on the west coast. 

COLOURS OF RED AND WHITE

The colours of red and white represent France and England. These two countries were the first European countries to explore and settle in Canada. 

On December 31, 1964, the red and white flag became the official flag of Canada. The red maple design was chosen out of thousands that were sent to Ottawa from across the country. 

There are no laws about how our flag should be treated. We can find the flag on clothing, pins, bags, postcards and other items. Most Canadians are proud of their flag and treat it with respect.
