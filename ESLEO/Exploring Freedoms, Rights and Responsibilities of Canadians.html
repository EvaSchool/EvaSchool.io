Exploring Freedoms, Rights and Responsibilities of Canadians

Freedom, Rights, and Responsibilities

Freedom is the power to act, speak, or think without restraint. A right is something that every person deserves that cannot be taken away from them. A responsibility is something that is expected of you and that benefits everyone.
These three may be different, but they work hand in hand. 

What is the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedom?

On April 17, 1982, the Constitution Act was adopted. It guarantees fundamental rights and freedoms of all Canadians through The Charter of Rights and Freedoms. 

The Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms sets out those rights and freedoms that Canadians believe are necessary for a free and democratic society.

Fundamental Freedom

The Charter protects the freedom of: 

  • Conscience and religion
  • Thought, belief, opinion and expression
  • Peaceful assembly
  • Association

Democratic Rights

  • The Charter protects rights that enable you to participate in the democratic process and have your voice heard in Parliament. This gives you the right to vote in federal, provincial or territorial elections, along with the right to stand for public office themselves. 

Mobility Rights

  • All Canadians have the right to enter, stay in and leave Canada. The Charter also guarantees that you can look for work or set up a business anywhere in Canada. 

Legal Rights

  • The Charter provides a broad set of legal rights that protect us in our dealings with the justice system. It ensures that everyone is treated fairly during legal proceedings. 

Equality Rights

  • Everyone has the right no to be discriminated against by the government based on race, religion, sex, abilities, origin or sex orientation. 

Official Language Rights

  • English and French both have equal status, rights, and privileges regarding their use in all Parliament and Government of Canada institutions. 

Minority Language and Education Rights

  • The Charter requires that provincial governments provide education to their citizens in the minority official language where numbers warrant.

Rights with Responsibilities

Knowing more about your rights as a citizen creates a list of responsibilities that go with these rights. For example, it is your right not to be discriminated against then; it is your responsibility to respect religious diversity and allow others to practice their own religious beliefs. 

Citizen Responsibilities

As a Canadian citizen, you should: 

  • Obey the law
  • Take care of oneself and one’s family
  • Help others in the community
  • Protect and Enjoy our Heritage and Environment
  • Vote in Elections
  • Serve on a jury
