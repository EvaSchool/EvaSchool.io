Everyday English: Who are you?

Lesson Description

 We will learn about Everyday English.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate the ability to understand, interpret, and evaluate spoken English for a variety of purposes;
A2. Use speaking skills and strategies to communicate in English for a variety of classroom and social purposes
A3. Use correctly the language structures appropriate for this level to communicate orally in English.
B1. Write in a variety of forms for different purposes and audiences;
D1. Use English and non-verbal communication strategies appropriately in a variety of social contexts.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 demonstrate comprehension of specific information in detailed, complex directions, instructions, and classroom presentations.
A1.2 demonstrate understanding of complex spoken English on a wide variety of topics in interactive situations.
A2.2 use a wide variety of conversational expressions to negotiate spoken interactions of all types.
A3.1 use correctly the grammatical structures of spoken English appropriate for this level.
B1.2 write short texts to express ideas and feelings on personal topics using a wide variety of forms.
D1.2 analyse and explain instances where different interpretations of non-verbal signals to lead misunderstanding in a variety of social, academic, and workplace contexts (e.g., role play scenarios involving intercultural misunderstandings of non-verbal communication, and discuss how communication could be improved) 

Learning Goals

  • Determine and use the appropriate language when introducing yourself.
  • Identify when it is appropriate to use humour in social situations.
    Use pronouns in describing yourself.
