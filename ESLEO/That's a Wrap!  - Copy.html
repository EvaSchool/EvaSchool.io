That's a Wrap!

  • PACTS: Plot, Atmosphere, Conflict, Theme and Settings
  • The plot is the main event of the story. It is made up of 6 parts: Exposition, Conflict, Rising Action, Climax, Falling Action, Resolution
  • The atmosphere is the mood of the story.
  • The characterization is the way by which an author reveals or describes the personality of a character.
  • There are four types of characterizations: Round, Flat, Dynamic, Static
  • The theme is the central message of the story.
  • The setting is the time and place that the story is set in. It initiates the mood of the story.
