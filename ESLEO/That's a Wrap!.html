That's a Wrap!

  • A symbol is a kind of picture that tells us something without using words. 
  • On March 24, 1975, the beaver was given the official status symbol of Canada’s sovereignty. This industrious rodent can be seen on coins and countless logos across the country. 
  • After the First World War, Canada adopted an official coat of arms and national motto as an expression of national pride. His Majesty King George V proclaimed this in 1921.
  • Mrs. Cathy Bursey-Sabouri drew the present design of the Arms of Canada.  
  • The Maple Leaf Tartan was declared an official symbol on March 9, 2011. 
  • The tartan’s colour pattern represents the changing colour of the maple leaf throughout seasons.
  • The Maple Leaf Tartan is a symbol of national pride and has been worn by Canadians from all backgrounds. 
  • Maple Tree: The Maple Tree was officially recognized as one of Canada’s symbols in 1996. 
  • NATIONAL ANTHEM: On July 1, 1980, “O Canada” was proclaimed as Canada’s national anthem. The music was composed by Calixa Lavallée, a well-known composer born in East Canada.
  • NATIONAL FLAG: Canada’s flag is red and white. In the center of the flag is a red maple leaf. The background is white. There is a red bar on each side of the flag. The bar on the right stands for the east coast of Canada. The Atlantic Ocean is on the east coast. The bar on the right stands for the east coast of Canada. The Pacific Ocean is on the west coast.
  • The colours of red and white represent France and England. These two countries were the first European countries to explore and settle in Canada.
  • Iced hockey is the national winter sport, while lacrosse is the national summer sport.
