Lesson 1 - PACTS

 Elements of Storytelling: Analyzing Plot, Atmosphere, Character, Characterization, Theme, and Setting.

Plot is the backbone of any narrative, serving as the sequence of events that drive the story forward. Atmosphere refers to the emotional and sensory environment created by an author or filmmaker. Characters are the heart of a story, driving the plot and providing readers or viewers with relatable figures to connect with. Characterization is how authors or filmmakers reveal a character's traits, beliefs, and motivations. The theme is the central idea or message that a story conveys. It often explores universal concepts like love, power, identity, or morality.
