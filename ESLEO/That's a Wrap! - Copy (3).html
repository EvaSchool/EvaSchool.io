That's a Wrap!

  • Chekhov uses imagery and tone to create that image in the reader’s head.
  • Chekhov uses lines such as “The grey background was untouched by death,” which gives us a better understanding of Nellie and her background.
  • A looking glass, more commonly known as a mirror today, is used to portray the future. Aside from this, the mirror can also be described as Nellie’s subconscious. And this is shown throughout the story.
  • Chekhov uses the mirror to show Nellie’s fears, hopes, and feelings.
