Enough and Not Enough Part III

Let’s continue learning about appropriate ways of using enough and not enough.

“Too”, “Enough”, and “Not Enough”

Commonly, those three are commonly used in negative senses. But they could be used positively.

There are not enough seating inside the auditorium.
There are too many people inside the auditorium.
There are enough seating inside the auditorium.

How much is enough?

  • “Enough” could be absolute or relative depending on the people’s references and context.

We need 3 cups of rice for dinner because 2 cups is not enough - Exact context
Our rice is not enough, we should buy more. - Relative context

“So” and “Such”

  • When we emphasize something, we can use so and such.
  • Such is used with an adjective + noun. If the noun is singular we use a/an before the adjective.
    • Example:
      The dog is such a loyal animal.
  • So is used before adjectives
    • Example:
      That dog is so loyal to the owner.
  • But limit your usage of So and Such, because they are used to emphasize something
