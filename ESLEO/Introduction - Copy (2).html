Canadian-American Relations Since World War II

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will learn about Canadian-American Relations Since World War II.

Overall Expectations

B1 Read and demonstrate understanding of a variety of texts for different purposes.
B3 Use a variety of strategies to build vocabulary.
C1 Write in a variety of forms for different purposes and audiences.
C3 Use correctly the conventions of written English appropriate for this level, including grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation.
D2 Demonstrate an understanding of the rights and responsibilities of Canadian citizenship, and of the contributions of diverse groups to Canadian society. 
D3 Demonstrate knowledge of and adaptation to the Ontario education system. 
D4 Demonstrate an understanding of, interpret, and create a variety of media texts.

Specific Expectations

B1.2 Demonstrate an understanding of more complex authentic texts in a variety of ways.
B3.1 Use a variety of vocabulary acquisition strategies to enrich vocabulary.
C1.2 Write longer and more complex texts to express ideas and feelings on personal topics using a variety of forms.
C3.1 Use correctly the grammatical structures and conventions of written English appropriate for this level.
C3.2 Use a wide variety of spelling strategies to spell words accurately.
D2.2 Explain how government policies on equity and social justice apply to current social issues
D3.4 Identify school and community policies and resources that are provided to support learning and explain how to use them.
D4.1 View, read, and listen to media texts, and identify strategies used in them to influence specific audiences.

Learning Goals

  • Read various articles and watch media texts to understand the relationship between Canada and the United States.
  • Demonstrate your understanding by writing an opinion piece about Canadian-American relations’ current status under the Biden administration.
