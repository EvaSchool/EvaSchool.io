Defining and Non-Defining Relative Clauses Part II

Let’s continue learning about relative clause through the relative pronouns!

Shortening Relative Clauses

  • Sometimes, it is okay to not include the relative pronoun in a relative clause.

Examples:

  • This is the coffee that he made earlier. = This is the coffee he made earlier.
  • That was the car that she bought last year. = That was the car she bought last year.
  • That is the person who my brother helped. = That is the person my brother helped.
  • You cannot take out the relative pronoun if it is the subject of the relative clause. 

Example:

  • The boy who stole the car was a student at the school - Correct
  • The boy stole the car was a student at the school - Wrong

Removing the Relative Pronoun

Study the sentence carefully.

  • If it is a defining clause, the relative pronoun (e.g. that) could be removed.

Example:

  • The cruise ship that arrived at the port carried many passengers yesterday.
  • The cruise ship arrived at the port carried many passengers yesterday.
  • If it is a non-defining clause, the relative pronoun (e.g. which) cannot be removed.

Example:

  • The cruise ship, which is enormous, arrived yesterday.

“Who” and “Whom”

  • The word whom is not being used commonly anymore. But, you could still use it depending on the circumstances.

Example:

They are the family who lives next door - Who is the subject of this sentence
They are the family who I helped earlier - Who is the object of this sentence

  • Whom is used in formal circumstances.

Example:

The person whom I met yesterday, was cheerful and friendly.
The person, whom is the guest at the event, was cheerful and friendly.

“Which”

  • It could refer to the noun immediately before it or could also refer to the entire clause before it
  • Which and That can be used when referring to the noun immediately before it.
  • Which can refer to an entire clause. Oftenly informal use to give an opinion or the sum of the idea.

Example: 

My friends decided to meet at 10pm, which I do not think is a great decision.
My friend is confused and always repeating her question, which annoyed me.

When, Why, and Where

When is used to indicate time.   Why is used to indicate reason. Where determines the place of an action.

Example:

  • It was in high school when I met my wife.
  • The man looks like he is sleepy, Everybody does not know why he is like that.
  • I remember the arcade where playing video games is my passion.
