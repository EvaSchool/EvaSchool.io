Exploring Canada: Regions and Brief History 

 

Canada is the second biggest country in the world. It is divided into five regions: Atlantic Provinces, Central Canada, Prairie Provinces, West Coast and Northern Territories. 

In this lesson, we will spend some time exploring these regions.

Stylized Quote

Canada's Capital

The capital of Canada is Ottawa. It is a city located in Ontario. You will find Canada’s parliament, the Supreme Court, and the Prime Minister’s residence in Ottawa.

 

Stylized Quote

Provinces and Territories

Canada is composed of ten provinces and three territories. Each of them has a capital city.

Atlantic Provinces

There are four Atlantic Provinces.

They are: 
Newfoundland and Labrador
New Brunswick
Prince Edward Island
Nova Scotia

 

 

Fill in the Blanks

 

The capital cities of the Atlantic Provinces are as follows:

Newfoundland and Labrador: St. John’s

New Brunswick: Fredericton

Prince Edward Island: Charlottetown 

Nova Scotia: Halifax 

 

Stylized Quote

The Atlantic Provinces are known for fishing, farming, forestry, and mining.

Since the region is near the Atlantic Ocean, winters are cool, and summers are humid.

Central Canada

Central Canada is made up of the provinces of Quebec and Ontario. 

What are the capital cities of Quebec and Ontario? 

Fill in the Blanks

The capital cities for Central Canada include:

Quebec: Quebec City
Ontario: Toronto

 

Stylized Quote

The Central region of Canada is the country’s industrial and manufacturing heart.

The Prairie Provinces

Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and Alberta make up the Prairie Provinces region of Canada.

What are the capital cities of The Prairie Provinces for Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and Alberta?

Fill in the Blanks

The capital cities of The Prairie Provinces are as follows:  

Manitoba: Winnipeg
Saskatchewan: Regina
Alberta: Edmonton

 

Stylized Quote

The Prairie Provinces are known for their rich energy resources and some of the world’s most fertile farmlands. Mining and hydro-electric power generation thrive here. It is also considered the country’s breadbasket, with agriculture as one of its main industries.

West Coast

Canada’s West Coast region consists of just one province, British Columbia. 

What is the capital city of British Columbia?   

Fill in the Blanks

The capital city of British Columbia is Victoria.

 

Stylized Quote

The province of British Columbia is known for its forestry products and its mining, shipping, and fishing industries.

The Northern Territories

The Northern Territories consist of the Yukon Territory, the Northwest Territories, and Nunavut. 

What are the capital cities of The Northern Territories: Yukon Territory, the Northwest Territories, and Nunavut?  

Fill in the Blanks

The capital city of The Northern Territories is as follows:

Yukon Territory: Whitehorse
The Northwest Territories: Yellowknife
Nunavut: Iqaluit

Stylized Quote

The region’s rich natural resources include gold, lead, copper, diamond and zinc.
