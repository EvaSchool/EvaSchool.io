Let's have a quick recap from the previous lesson.

Nouns can be either countable or uncountable. Countable nouns (or count nouns) are those that refer to something that can be counted. 

They have both singular and plural forms (e.g. cat/cats; woman/women; country/countries). In the singular, they can be preceded by a or an. Most nouns come into this category.
