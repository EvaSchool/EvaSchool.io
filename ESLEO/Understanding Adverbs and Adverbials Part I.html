Understanding Adverbs and Adverbials Part I

In this lesson, we’ll look at adverbs and adverbials. 

Adverbs often modify verbs. 
This means that they describe the way an action is happening. 

Example: 
She can count quickly. 
Quickly is the adverb in the sentence. 

Adverbial

An adverbial is a word or phrase functioning like an adverb.

Take note of the position of adverbials.

Adverbials of manner, time, and some of frequency usually at the end of the clause. 

Example: 
Adverbials of manner: She spoke to me slowly.
Adverbials of time: I’ll see you next time.
Adverbials of frequency: We go to the farm every weekend.

Adverbials of certainty, some adverbs of frequency, and time usually go in the middle. 

Example: 
Adverbials of certainty: I am definitely attending the party.
Adverbials of time: I rarely go to sleep 8 hours in a day.
Adverbials of frequency: We just finished taking the quiz.
