Understanding Tag Questions

     

In this lesson, you will learn how to use tag questions. They will make your sentences clearer and will help you on how to become better in speaking and writing in English.

     

   

Let’s Begin!

Tag Questions

  • A special construction in English.
  • A statement followed by a mini question.
  • "Is that right?" or "Do you agree?" They are very common in English.

Examples:

  • The water is beautiful. It’s crystal clear, isn’t it?
  • The people are anxious, aren’t they?

       

You will see that most of the time, the auxiliary verb from the positive statement is repeated in the tag and changed to negative.

Compliments

  • Telling people that they are nice goes a long way, you could win a friend and gain their trust.
  • And telling people that they are good in something makes them more motivated. So, keep telling people that they are good!

       

Forming compliments involves using tag questions.

Tag questions are also involved in casual conversations regarding complimenting a person.

Examples:

  • The painting is really beautiful. It is dedicated to the people risked their lives curing the sick, isn’t it?
  • The dress is pretty, isn’t it?
  • She was the most beautiful girl in the ballroom, wasn’t she?
