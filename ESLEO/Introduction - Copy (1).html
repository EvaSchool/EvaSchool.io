Freedoms, Rights and Responsibilities of Canadians

Lesson Description

In this lesson, we will learn about Freedoms, Rights and Responsibilities of Canadians.

Overall Expectations

Read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of texts for different purposes.
B2 Use a variety of reading strategies throughout the reading process to extract meaning from texts.
C1 Write in a variety of forms for different purposes and audiences.
C3 Use correctly the conventions of written English appropriate for this level, including grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation.
D2 Demonstrate an understanding of the rights and responsibilities of Canadian citizenship and of the contributions of diverse groups to Canadian society. 
D3 Demonstrate knowledge of and adaptation to the Ontario education system. 

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Read a wide variety of more complex, authentic texts of increased complexity on a range of topics.
B2.1 Use a wide variety of reading comprehension strategies before, during, and after reading to understand texts.
B2.2 Identify and use a variety of features of texts to locate information and aid comprehension.
C1.2 Write longer and more complex texts to express ideas and feelings on personal topics using a variety of forms.
C3.1 Use correctly the grammatical structures and conventions of written English appropriate for this level.
C3.2 Use a wide variety of spelling strategies to spell words accurately.
D2.2 Explain how government policies on equity and social justice apply to current social issues
D3.4 Identify school and community policies and resources that are provided to support learning and explain how to use them

Learning Goals

  • Read and demonstrate your understanding of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.
  • Demonstrate your understanding of your freedoms, rights and responsibilities by responding to sample scenarios.
  • Write an analysis of a news article of your choice to demonstrate your understanding of your freedoms, rights and responsibilities.
