Canada-US relations during the Cold War

The Cold War began at the end of World War II and persisted through the 1980s. It represents a polarization of global forces into two camps: America and its allies (represented by NATO and sometimes the UN) and the Soviet Union with its supporters (represented by the Warsaw Pact and sometimes China).

Clauses

Clauses are combinations of words and phrases that give either a complete or incomplete thought. Usually, you know it is a clause when a subject or verb appears together. 

Example: I cannot remember what happened last Sunday. 
    
In this sentence, “what” is the subject and “happened” is the verb. Together these words form a clause.
