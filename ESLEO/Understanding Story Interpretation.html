When writing an analysis, you have to decide which element of the story you will analyze. 

There are two main questions you have to answer in analyzing short stories.

  • How does the story element I want to analyze contribute to the meaning or effect of the story?
  • Why do I have the response I have to the story--what did the author do to make me feel the way I did about the characters or the ending?

Connect this to the PACTS of the story, analyze the story by using what you know about the PACTS. Infer information from the short story by exploring the plot, atmosphere, characterization, theme, and setting of the story.

  • By analyzing “The Looking Glass”, what can you interpret from the story?
  • How does Anton Chekhov set the tone of the story?

We can interpret the story by answering the two main questions.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: What did you interpret from the first two paragraphs of the story? How did you reach that interpretation?

 

Let’s analyze Chekhov’s use of dreams as a main driving force of the story.

In the first two paragraphs of the story, Chekhov uses imagery to depict that Nellie is falling asleep. He carefully transitions reality into dreams, and by using Nellie’s dream, Chekhov relates to the reader that dreams can influence people’s decisions.
Chekhov uses dreams as a way of relating to the reader.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: What do you interpret from Nellie’s dream? What relates to you, and what do you understand from it? How did you get to that understanding?

The tone of helplessness is also evident throughout the short story.

  • In the story, Nellie is helpless in her dream. Let’s emphasize “dream” and understand that - generally - dreams imply that we can do whatever we want. And yet Nellie is still helpless.
  • Chekhov uses imagery and tone to create that image in the reader’s head.
    • Nellie’s cries for help.
    • Dr. Lukitch’s inability to help due to his sickness.

Participate in the Discussion Forum: How did Chekhov portray how dreams reflect the subconscious? What other tone do you observe in the story?

Chekhov uses lines such as “The grey background was untouched by death,” which gives us a better understanding of Nellie and her background.
With this, we know that:

  • Nellie fears uncertainty (death). This statement shows that her dreams (or reality) have never experienced the death of a loved one.
  • From this, we understand that Nellie fears the future.

Participate in the Discussion Forum:Are there other statements that show this? Explain your answer.
