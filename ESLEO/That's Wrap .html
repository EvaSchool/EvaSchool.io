  • Stereotypes are impressions or generalizations that people have about different cultures or groups.
  • Correct Usage of Prepositions
    • To - used to indicate a destination or direction
    • Toward(s) - in the direction of someone or something
    • Through - Movement from one side to another but "in something", such as long grass or a forest
    • Into - Movement that enters a space, usually with a verb that expresses movement
    • Over - Movement at a higher level than something else
    • Across - Movement from one side of something to the opposite site (road, river)
    • Along - Movement of something in a line that follows the side of something long
    • From - the place where someone or something starts
    • Around - Movement in circles or in the vicinity of something
    • Onto - Movement to a position on a surface
    • Up - Movement to a higher position
    • Down - Indicates movement to a lower position
