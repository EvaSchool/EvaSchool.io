Understanding Adverbs and Adverbials Part II

Let’s talk about a completed action. 

Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect tenses are used to talk about an action that was completed at an unspecified time or indefinite time in the past.

We use has/have + past participle form of the verb 

Example:

I have listened to this song earlier.

Past Perfect Tense

The perfect continuous tenses talked about an action that took place in the past before a specific point of time or another past action.

We use had + past participle of the verb.

Example: I had finished my homework before the quiz came up. 

Future Perfect Tense

The future perfect tense talks about an action that will occur in the future before a certain point of time or another action in the future. 

We use will have + past participle form of the verb. 

Example: I will have joined the club by the time I passed the test.
