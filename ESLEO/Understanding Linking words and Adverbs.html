Understanding Linking Words and Adverbs

  

Linking words

In the previous lesson, you learned about linking words that introduce new information and introducing examples.

Now, you’ll learn linking words that add/support and emphasize.

  

   

Let’s use the following sentences as examples:

  • Ancient empires like Greece, Persia, and Macedonia experienced a golden age.

The like in the sentence adds to the information on the subject of ancient empires.

  • Starting your day in a good mood is better for you, in particular, it also affects people around you.

The words in particular emphasize the subject and good mood.

     

    

Examples:

  • My cat waits impatiently for his food. - Adverb and verb
  • This book is more interesting than the last one. - Adverb and adjective
  • Phillip sings rather enormously too loudly. - Adverb and other adverb

(The problem is that it often produces weak and clunky sentences, be careful not to overdo it)

  • Fortunately, we got there in time. - Adverb and other sentence
