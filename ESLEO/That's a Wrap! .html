That's a Wrap!

Two Countries from World War II

Let's find out!

  • The Second World War transformed Canada from a quiet country on the fringes of global affairs, into a critical player in the 20th century's most important struggles. 
  • The Cold War began at the end of World War II and persisted through the 1980s. It represents a polarization of global forces into two camps: America and its allies (represented by NATO and sometimes the UN) and the Soviet Union with its supporters (represented by the Warsaw Pact and sometimes China).
  • Canada and the US economies are closely tied and mutually beneficial. 
  • Canada and the U.S. are each other’s biggest trading partners.
  • The relationship between Canada and the US continues to transform under each new leadership.
