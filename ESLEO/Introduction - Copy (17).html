Future Form

Lesson Description

 We will review and expand their understanding of future forms (going to, present continuous, present simple, future with will, future continuous). We will learn another future from about, to, be to, and due to. You will use future forms to talk about intentions, arrangements, decisions made at the time of speaking and for scheduled events.

Overall Expectations

C3 use correctly the conventions of written English appropriate for this level, including grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation.

Specific Expectations

C3.1 use correctly the grammatical structures and conventions of written English appropriate for this level

Learning Goals

  • Students will explain how various characteristics of Canada’s natural environment can be used to divide the country into different physical regions.
