COMMUNICATION

  • The act of conveying meaning from one person or group to another.
  • Usually follows a process.
  • Has five major types: verbal, non-verbal, visual written, and listening.

MEDIA

  • Main means of communication
  • Has 4 types:
    • Print
    • Broadcast
    • Outdoor, and
    • Online

Open All Panels

PRINT MEDIA

  • A form of publication where the materials are formed or made through printing that can be used for advertisement and spreading news, entertainment, and information.

BROADCAST MEDIA

  • A type of media that gives information to the public through television and radio and is also used for advertisement and entertainment purposes. 

OUTDOOR MEDIA

  • Media that is focused on advertisement outdoors. It includes posters seen on bus, bus stops, billboards, etc. 

ONLINE MEDIA

Media located on the internet. Examples would be Social media apps such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, and Messenger used in exchanging messages and entertainment.

DECODING

The process where the receiver begins to interpret the message is called decoding. The manner in which the receiver gets the message affects how he/she views the message.

RECEIVER

The receiver is the intended person to receive the message. Their background also determines how they will interpret the message.

FEEDBACK

This is the response of the receiver. When the receiver responds to the message, the communication process becomes dynamic. 

NOISE

This is anything that protrudes the success of communication.
