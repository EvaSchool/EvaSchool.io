Media, no matter the form, is not real life. It does not present reality. It represents it!

Media constructs people, places, events, issues, and more. This is not a bad thing, but it’s best to be aware of this because people consume media every day.

Constructing these takes effort: 
Which is called the Production Process.

WHAT IS A PRODUCTION?

Process of creating a media product.

MEDIA PRODUCTS INCLUDE BUT ARE NOT LIMITED TO:

  • TV Shows
  • Commercials
  • Books
  • Magazines
  • Movies
  • PSAs
  • Youtube Videos
  • Radio shows
  • Newspapers

CONTEXT CLUES: What do you think people do in each stage of Media production?

Three Stages of Media Production:

  • Pre-production
  • Production
  • Post-production

THREE STAGES OF MEDIA PRODUCTION:

  • Pre-production = brainstorming, planning the script, storyboarding, drafting, scheduling, and more.
  • Production = Shooting, recording, or printing of the final product.
  • Post-production = Editing, proofreading, and release.

IMPORTANCE OF THE MEDIA PRODUCTION PROCESS:

  • Saves the production time, money, and other resources
  • Brings order to a production
  • Refines the media product’s content, duration, and presentation

WHY DO WE NEED TO STUDY MEDIA PRODUCTION

  • Even if you are not a blockbuster Hollywood mogul, being aware of the media production process reminds you that Media Products are not real life.
  • The media affects our experiences, culture, gender, and preconceived expectations. It is best to know what it is made of and how it is made.
