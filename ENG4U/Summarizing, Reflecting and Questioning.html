Summarizing, Reflecting and Questioning

  

Why Are These Actions Important?

  

Very often in seminar style classes, you are expected to listen to a presentation and to be able to summarize, reflect, and ask questions.
  • Sometimes a portion of your marks depends on how well you participate in such a way.
  • It is also important to be able to understand a variety of accents and different presenting and speaking styles.

 

Writing a Summary Writing a Summary From an Article Writing a Summary from an Article, cont…

  • A summary answers this question: "What is it about?"

-A summary also answers: Who wrote it? And what is it called?
-A summary says the same thing but in fewer words.

  • A summary is a piece of writing that outlines the main points of another article or book.
  • The point is to be able to decide what is the most important information, and not to include any extra information or opinion.
  • Being able to write a good summary shows that you really understand what you read.
  • It is a very important skill to have and is necessary in many courses you may take in the future.
  1. Read the whole article for general understanding first. Make sure you understand the content, and the overall message being conveyed. Look closely at the topic sentence, the final paragraph, and key words that are repeated throughout the article.
  2. Print the article. Read the article again. Cross out information that isn't important, and underline or highlight the parts that you think are the most important.
  3. On a piece of paper (or on your computer) write the answers to the following questions:

What is the title?
Who is the author? What is the main idea?
What is it about?

  1. What are the main points of the article?
  2. Write a topic sentence for the summary - include the name of the author, the title of the article, and the answer to "What is it about?"
  3. Paraphrase the main points. Make sure you don't write your own opinion in the summary. A summary isn't for opinions, it is just to 'condense' the important information in the article into a smaller form.
  4. Write the summarized information in the same order as in the original.
  5. The summary should be about 15 – 20% the length of the original.
  6. Proofread your summary!

Jessi Arrington: Wearing Nothing New

Practice: Summary

This TED talk by Jessi Arrington was about how to be a conscious consumer and outfit junkie at the same time. The Brooklyn designer demonstrated how she could travel with nothing in her suitcase, find everything she needs to look great for cheap in thrift stores, and then give it all away again, practicing non attachment.

     

Practice: Reflect

I really found this talk inspiring. I think it is important to be conscious of how our consuming habits affect the world, both socially and environmentally. Not only does she look great and fun, but she hit on an important truth and tonic for our times – we don't have to conform or 'fit in' to be fabulous – it's all about confidence and believing in yourself.
