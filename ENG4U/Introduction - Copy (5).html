Summarizing, Reflecting and Questioning

Lesson Description

Students will look at the importance of summarizing, reflecting and questioning speakers after listening to speeches/oral presentations. The steps for writing a summary after reading articles will also be shown. Students will practice taking notes after actively listening to a short TedTalk video. From there, they will write a short summary, reflection, and two questions for the speaker. As a class, the various ideas will be discussed. The main task will be for students to engage in the discussion forum by writing a summary and reflection from another video of their choosing.

Overall Expectations

A1 Listening to Understand: listen in order to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations for a variety of purposes;
C1. Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience;
C2. Using Knowledge of Form and Style: draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 identify the purpose of a wide range of listening tasks and set goals for specific tasks
A1.4 identify the important information and ideas in oral texts, including complex and challenging texts, in a variety of ways
A1.6 extend understanding of oral texts, including complex and challenging texts, by making insightful connections between the ideas in them and personal knowledge, experience, and insights; other texts; and the world around them
C1.2 generate, expand, explore, and focus ideas for potential writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate
C2.1 write for different purposes and audiences using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.

Learning Goals

  • Students will understand the purpose of summarizing, reflecting and questioning messages presented orally.
  • Students will use active listening skills to identify main ideas from a presentation.
  • Students will make notes based on a presentation.
  • Students will use critical thinking skills to turn their notes into a summary.
  • Students will reflect on a presentation.
  • Students will ask questions to the speaker that show further evaluation of the content.

  • I can make notes based on a presentation.
