VOMITING

Emesis or Vomiting is the expulsion of stomach contents through the esophagus and the mouth

Nausea and vomiting are not disorders or diseases themselves; they are symptoms of some underlying condition. The signal for vomiting comes from the vomiting centre in the brain. The stimuli may trigger the vomiting centre to initiate vomiting.

  • Motion sickness
  • Ménière’s disease
  • Meningitis
  • tumours

INJURY

  • bacterial, such as food or water poisoning
  • viral, such as stomach flu
  • Concussion
  • hemorrhage


DISEASES

DRUGS

  • Cancer
  • ulcers 
  • Chemotherapy
  • alcohol

Vomiting may lead to several complications, especially if it is recurring over a period of hours or days. Some of the complications associated with repeated vomiting include:

  • Aspiration of the vomitus into the air passage and lungs
  • Electrolyte and water loss
  • Damage to the enamel of the teeth
  • Tear of the esophageal mucosa

DIARRHEA

  • loose or watery feces
