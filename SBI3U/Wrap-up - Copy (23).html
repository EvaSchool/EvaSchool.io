Wrap-up

Key points

Most fungi are multicellular heterotrophs that use external digestion.

The bodies of fungi consist of thin hyphae containing many nuclei, surrounded by a cell wall made of chitin. Fungi also have reproductive structures that produce spores.

The life cycles of most fungi involve haploid individuals that reproduce both sexually and asexually.

Most plant species depend on symbiotic relationships with soil fungi.

Fungi are important decomposers that cycle nutrients in ecosystems.

Fungi are extremely valuable economically, particularly in the production of breads, cheeses, and alcoholic beverages.

Fungi cause many harmful crop diseases and some human diseases.

The introduction of non-native fungi to ecosystems by humans has had serious environmental consequences.
