Wrap up

By the eighteenth century, scientists were beginning to gather evidence supporting the theory that life had changed over time.

• Lamarck was the first scientist to recognize that species evolved in response to their environment, but his hypothesis of how species evolved has proven to be incorrect.

• Fossils provide compelling evidence that species living in the past were different from those living in the present.

• Fossils show that organisms become increasingly complex over time, with the most complex organisms being found in only more recent fossil deposits.

• Cuvier proposed that species did not change but were eliminated by catastrophic events, only to be replaced by newly created forms.

• Lyell proposed that Earth’s geologic features can be explained by very slow changes occurring over very long periods of time.
