Abnormal Meiosis and/or Fertilization

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:
D3. demonstrate an understanding of concepts, processes, and technologies related to the transmission of hereditary characteristics.

Specific Expectations

D3.4 describes some genetic disorders caused by chromosomal abnormalities (e.g., nondisjunction of chromosomes during meiosis) or other genetic mutations in terms of chromosomes affected, physical effects, and treatments.

Learning Goals

  • Determine the non-junction disorders and how it is diagnosed.
  • Identify problems with fertilization.
  • Explain the implications of reproductive technologies.

Success Criteria

  1. Understand how to diagnose non-junction disorders.
  2. Enumerate the problems in fertilization.
  3. Understand the implications of technology in reproduction.
