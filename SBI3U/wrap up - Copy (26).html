Wrap up

12.5 Summary

  • Water and nutrient transport in the xylem tissue involves root pressure, capillary action, and transpiration.
  • Nutrients enter root cells by active transport; water enters by osmosis.
  • The Casparian strip forces water and nutrients to pass directly through the cells of the endodermis. It also prevents water and dissolved substances from moving back out.
  • Water and nutrients move up the xylem tubes by root pressure, capillary action, and transpiration.
  • Transpiration is the evaporation of water from the leaves; it is the force that pulls the column of water up the xylem tubes.
  • Sugars are actively transported from source to sink in the phloem.
  • Phloem sap moves along the phloem tubes as a result of a pressure difference between source cells and sink cells.
  • Movement of fluid in the xylem is always upward (or sideways). Movement in the phloem can be upward or downward (or sideways), depending on source and sink locations.
