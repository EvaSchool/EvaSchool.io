Natural Selection

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B1. analyze the effects of various human activities on the diversity of living things.

C1. Analyze the economic and environmental advantages and disadvantages of an artificial selection technology, and evaluate the impact of environmental changes on natural selection and endangered species.

C3. demonstrate an understanding of the theory of evolution, the evidence that supports it, and some of the mechanisms by which it occurs.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 analyzes some of the risks and benefits of human intervention (e.g., tree plantations; monoculture of livestock or agricultural crops; overharvesting of wild plants for medicinal purposes; using pesticides to control pests; suppression of wildfires) to the biodiversity of aquatic or terrestrial ecosystems [AI, C].

C1.2 evaluates the possible impact of an environmental change on natural selection and on the vulnerability of species (e.g., adaptation to environmental changes can affect the reproductive success of an organism).

Learning Goals

  • Differentiate the types of selection
  • Identify various evolutionary changes without selection
  • Enumerate the consequences of human influences
