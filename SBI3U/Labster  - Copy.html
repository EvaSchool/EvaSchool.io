In July of 1837 Darwin began writing his first notebook on what he called the “transmutation of species.”

Charles Darwin returned to England in 1836, ending his voyage on the HMS Beagle.

In the years that followed, Darwin stayed true to his words, continuing to consult experts, conduct numerous experiments, and spend long hours contemplating his findings.

  • Some of the most compelling evidence for evolution comes from biogeography, the observed geographic patterns of distribution of species.

  • Darwin’s observations of both living and fossilized species during the voyage of the Beagle laid the foundation for this new science.

Darwin was particularly surprised by the unusual assortment of species he found on the Galapagos Islands.
