Wrap-up

key points

Darwin’s theory of evolution by means of natural selection explains how
the environment acts to favour the reproductive success of individuals with
certain heritable traits over others.
• Darwin’s theory of evolution by means of natural selection is able to explain
how an adaptation can arise.
• Darwin’s theory of evolution by means of natural selection can be used to
make predictions about the future evolution of species.
• Darwin’s theory of evolution by means of natural selection is a testable
scientific theory.

The modern synthesis of evolutionary biology combined Darwin’s theory of
evolution by natural selection with the science of genetics.
• Genetic mutations provide a continuous supply of new heritable information
and variation within a species.
• Beneficial mutations are favoured by natural selection.
• Neutral mutations have no immediate influence on the success of
an individual.
• Harmful mutations are selected against—reducing their frequencies within
a population.
• Pseudogenes provide compelling evidence for evolution.
• The sciences of plate tectonics and paleontology have greatly enhanced our
understanding of evolution.
