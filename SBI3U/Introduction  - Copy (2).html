What and How Much We Need to Eat

Overall Expectations

E.3 Demonstrate an understanding of animal anatomy and physiology and describe disorders of the respiratory, circulatory, and digestive systems.

Specific Expectations

E.3.2 Explain the anatomy of the digestive system and the importance of digestion in providing nutrients needed for energy and growth.

Learning Goals

  • To define energy and nutrients, and explain why they are needed.
  • To identify sources of energy in the diet.
  • To understand how energy needs change throughout the lifecycle.
  • To understand the energy needs of different levels of physical activity.
  • To determine factors affecting energy requirements.
  • Explain the relationship between diet and health.
  • Distinguish among the six (6) classes of nutrients.
  • Identify the importance of each nutrient.
  • Determine some physical and physiological problems related to what, how much, or how little we eat.

Success Criteria

  1. Define energy and nutrients, and explain why they are needed.
  2. Identify sources of energy in the diet.
  3. Understand how energy needs change throughout the lifecycle.
  4. Understand the energy needs of different levels of physical activity.
  5. Determine factors affecting energy requirements.
  6. Explain the relationship between diet and health.
  7. Distinguish among the six (6) classes of nutrients.
  8. Identify the importance of each nutrient.
  9. Determine some physical and physiological problems related to what, how much, or how little we eat.
