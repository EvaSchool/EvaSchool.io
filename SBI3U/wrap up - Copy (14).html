Wrap-up

The story of John Snow's groundbreaking work during the 1850s cholera epidemic in England serves as a compelling example of scientific detective work, challenging prevailing medical theories. While the medical community believed in the miasmatic theory, attributing diseases like cholera to "bad air," Snow's meticulous research unveiled a different truth — contaminated drinking water was the actual culprit. His systematic approach and indisputable evidence revolutionized medical thinking, emphasizing a fundamental principle of science: theories are always subject to change based on new evidence. This narrative highlights the transformative power of scientific inquiry, setting the stage for modern medicine.
