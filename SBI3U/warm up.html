Warm-up

key points 

Alleles that determine the phenotype regardless of the presence of other
alleles follow a pattern of inheritance called complete dominance.
• A heterozygous individual with an intermediate phenotype between
the phenotypes of the two homozygous individuals follow a pattern of
inheritance called incomplete dominance.
• Codominance occurs when both alleles are fully expressed. Type AB blood is
an example of codominance.
• Blood type is an example of a gene with multiple alleles. The three blood type
alleles are I
A, I
B
, and i. Different combinations of the three alleles produce the type

A, type B, type AB, and type O blood.
