Course Overview

This course furthers studentsâ€™ understanding of the processes that occur in biological systems. Students will study theory and conduct investigations in the areas of diversity of living things, evolution, genetic processes, animals: structure and function, and plants: anatomy, growth, and function. The course focuses on the theoretical aspects of the topics under study and helps students refine skills related to scientific investigation. This course is intended for students planning to pursue careers in the life sciences and related fields.

With regards to Diversity of Living Things, Students will:

  • Classify organisms according to the principles of taxonomy;
  • Describe the characteristics of major groups of organisms;
  • Analyze the relationships between biodiversity and the sustainability of ecosystems;

With regards to Evolution, Students will:

  • Analyze the evidence for the theory of evolution and its mechanisms;
  • Explain the process of natural selection and its effects on populations over time;
  • Assess the social and environmental impact of evolutionary theory;

With regards to Genetic Processes, Students will:

  • Describe DNA structure and function, and the processes of replication, transcription, and translation;
  • Explain patterns of inheritance and predict outcomes using genetic crosses;
  • Assess the applications and implications of genetic research and biotechnology;

With regards to Animals: Structure and Function, Students will:

  • Describe the anatomy and physiology of human and animal systems;
  • Analyze the relationships between structure and function in organ systems;
  • Assess the impact of lifestyle choices and diseases on system function;

With regards to Plants: Anatomy, Growth, and Function, Students will:

  • Describe the structure and function of plant cells, tissues, and organs;
  • Explain the processes of photosynthesis, growth, and reproduction in plants;
  • Analyze the effects of environmental factors on plant growth and development;

Units of Study:

  • Unit 1: Diversity of Living Things
  • Unit 2: Evolution
  • Unit 3: Genetic Processes
  • Unit 4: Animals: Structure and Function
  • Unit 5: Plants: Anatomy, Growth, and Function
