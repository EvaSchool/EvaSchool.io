Measuring Energy

In 1918, J. Arthur Harris and Francis G. Benedict, nutrition researchers at the Nutrition Laboratory of the Carnegie Institute of Washington, published a paper based on their study of basal metabolism. The data from their research allowed them to derive a formula that is still the most commonly used method of estimating basal metabolic rate and energy expenditure.
