The Prokaryotes: Eubacteria and Archaea

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B2. Investigate, through laboratory and/or field activities or through simulations, the principles of scientific classification, using appropriate sampling and classification techniques.

Specific Expectations

B3.2 compare and contrast the structure and function of different types of prokaryotes, eukaryotes, and viruses (e.g., compare and contrast genetic material, metabolism, organelles, and other cell parts).

B3.4 explains key structural and functional changes in organisms as they have evolved over time (e.g., the evolution of eukaryotes from prokaryotes, and of plants from unicellular organisms).

Learning Goals

  • Give reasons why prokaryotes are important
  • Identify the domains of Eubacteria and Archaea
  • Identify the characteristics of bacteria
  • Know about the diseases caused by bacteria and antibiotic resistance.

Success Criteria

  • Explain the importance of prokaryotes.
  • Classify Eubacteria and Archaea in their domains.
  • Describe the characteristics of bacteria.
  • identify the diseases caused by bacteria and antibiotic resistance.
