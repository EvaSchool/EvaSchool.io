The Need for a Respiratory System

Overall Expectations

E.2 Investigate through laboratory inquiry or computer simulation, the functional responses of the respiratory and circulatory systems of animals, and the relationships between their respiratory, circulatory, and digestive systems.

E.3 Demonstrate an understanding of animal anatomy and physiology and describe disorders of the respiratory, circulatory, and digestive systems.

Specific Expectations

E.3.1 Explain the anatomy of the respiratory system and the process of ventilation and gas exchange from the environment to the cell.

Learning Goals

  • Understand the importance of a respiratory system to all living things.
  • Explain the need for oxygen in Aerobic Cellular Respiration, and Gas Exchange and Ventilation.
  • Identify the parts of the Human Respiratory System.
  • Explain the structure and functions of the lungs, Gas Exchange in the alveoli, and the Mechanism of Ventilation.
  • Understand the Respiratory structures of other organisms.

Success Criteria

By the end of this lesson, I can…

  • Understand the importance of a respiratory system to all living things.
  • Explain the need for oxygen in Aerobic Cellular Respiration, and Gas Exchange and Ventilation.
  • Identify the parts of the Human Respiratory System.
  • Explain the structure and functions of the lungs, Gas Exchange in the alveoli, and the Mechanism of Ventilation.
  • Understand the Respiratory structures of other organisms.
