Viruses, Viroids and Prions

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

D2. investigate the characteristics, growth, and spread of bacteria, and the effects of aseptic techniques and antibiotics.

Specific Expectations

D3.5 explains the differences between bacteria and viruses in terms of their size, structure, and reproduction, and the methods used to control their spread.

Learning Goals

  • Learn what are viruses, viroids, and prions and identify their different types.
  • Give reasons why viruses are important
  • Identify the characteristics of the virus
  • Know how they affect the host.
  • Describe the different reproductive cycles or viruses.
  • Explain how vaccines and gene therapy work to minimize effects.

Success Criteria

  • Define what are viruses, viroids, and prions and identify their different types.
  • Give reasons why viruses are important.
  • Explain the characteristics of the virus.
  • Discover how they affect the host.
  • Identify the different reproductive cycles of viruses.
  • Explain how vaccines and gene therapy work to minimize effects.
