Asexual Reproduction: Copies and Clones

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B3. demonstrate an understanding of the diversity of living organisms in terms of the principles of taxonomy and phylogeny.

Specific Expectations

B3.3 describes unifying and distinguishing anatomical and physiological characteristics (e.g., types of reproduction, habitat, general physical structure) of representative organisms from each of the kingdoms.

Learning Goals

  • Identify modes of asexual reproduction.
  • Understand cell division.
  • Learn about plant and animal cloning.
  • Know the applications and implications of cloning.

Success Criteria

  1. Define asexual reproduction and understand each type.
  2. Learn the cell divisions and their subparts.
  3. Understand how plants and animals reproduce and clone; and
  4. Learn how cloning works and its effects on biodiversity.
