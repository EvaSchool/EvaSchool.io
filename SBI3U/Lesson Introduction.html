Introduction to Biodiversity

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B1. analyze the effects of various human activities on the diversity of living things;

B3. demonstrate an understanding of the diversity of living organisms in terms of the principles of taxonomy and phylogeny.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 analyzes some of the risks and benefits of human intervention (e.g., tree plantations; monoculture of livestock or agricultural crops; overharvesting of wild plants for medicinal purposes; using pesticides to control pests; suppression of wildfires) to the biodiversity of aquatic or terrestrial ecosystems [AI, C];

B1.2 analyzes the impact that climate change might have on the diversity of living things (e.g., rising temperatures can result in habitat loss or expansion; changing rainfall levels can cause drought or flooding of habitats) [AI, C];

B2.1 uses appropriate terminology related to biodiversity, including, but not limited to: genetic diversity, species diversity, structural diversity, protists, bacteria, fungi, binomial nomenclature, and morphology [C].

Learning Goals

  • Learn about biodiversity and species.
  • Explain the three different levels of biodiversity.
  • Analyze different impacts of humans on biodiversity. 
  • Give reasons why biodiversity is crucial to the survival of life on earth.

Success Criteria

  • Define biodiversity and identify each type of species.
  • Understand the three levels of biodiversity
  • Identify the beneficial and harmful impacts of humans on biodiversity; and
  • Understand how biodiversity works in our lives.
