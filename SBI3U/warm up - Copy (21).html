Warm up

On May 18, 1980, Mount St. Helens, a volcano in the U.S. state of Washington, suddenly erupted, releasing huge plumes of volcanic ash. The thermal energy and ash from the eruption destroyed all life on the mountainside. Lava from the volcano eventually cooled to form a dome of new rock. Over the decades since the eruption, the bare rock and ash have slowly been colonized by plant and animal life. The changes in the community that are happening on Mount St. Helens are a dramatic example of succession.
