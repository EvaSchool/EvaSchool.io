13.2 Summary

  •  Asexual reproduction, also known as vegetative reproduction, produces genetically identical copies (clones) of an individual plant.
  • Structures used in asexual reproduction include bulbs, rhizomes, and scions.
  •  Benefits of asexual reproduction include the ability to reproduce rapidly.
  •  Asexual reproduction techniques are used in agriculture to produce copies of plants with desirable traits.
