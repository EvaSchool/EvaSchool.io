Wrap-Up

Key Points

The biological species concept defines species as a population of individuals
that are able to freely breed under natural conditions.

Biologists have identified and described more than 1.7 million species.

All species depend on other species in a variety of ways for their own survival.

Species evolve over time and space.

Biodiversity refers to the variety of species in an ecosystem but also includes
structural diversity within ecosystems and individual variability within species.

New species are still being discovered, but biodiversity is under threat from
many human activities.
