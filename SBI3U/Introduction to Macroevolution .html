Macroevolution

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

C3. demonstrate an understanding of the theory of evolution, the evidence that supports it, and some of the mechanisms by which it occurs.

Specific Expectations

C3.2 explains the process of adaptation of individual organisms to their environment (e.g., some disease-causing bacteria in a bacterial population can survive exposure to antibiotics due to slight genetic variations from the rest of the population, which allows successful surviving bacteria to pass on antibiotic resistance to the next generation).

C3.3 defines the concept of speciation and explains the process by which new species are formed.

Learning Goals

  • Discuss the tree of life
  • Explain the diversification and mass extinction.
  • Learn about cladistics and phylogeny.
  • Elaborate on the theory of gradualism and punctuated equilibrium.
