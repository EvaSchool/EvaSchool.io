Mendelian Inheritance

Overall Expectations

  • D.2 Investigate genetic processes, including those that occur during meiosis, and analyse data to solve basic genetics problems involving monohybrid and dihybrid crosses.
  • D.3 Demonstrate an understanding of concepts, processes, and technologies related to the transmission of hereditary characteristics. 

Specific Expectations

  • D.2.3 Use the Punnett square method to solve basic genetics problems involving monohybrid crosses, incomplete dominance, codominance, dihybrid crosses, and sex-linked genes [PR, AI, C].
  • D2.4 investigate, through laboratory inquiry or computer simulation, monohybrid and dihybrid crosses, and use the Punnett square method and probability rules to analyse the qualitative and quantitative data and determine the parent genotype [PR, AI, C].
  • D.3.3 Explain the concepts of genotype, pheno- type, dominance, incomplete dominance, codominance, recessiveness, and sex linkage ac- cording to Mendelian laws of inheritance. 

Learning Goals

  • Explain Gregor Mendel’s laws of inheritance.
  • Discuss the difference between genotypes and phenotypes.
  • Label the three types of genotypes.
  • Differentiate Monohybrid cross from Dihybrid cross.
  • Draw and label a Punnett square.
  • Predict outcomes of a Punnett square.
  • Explain the difference between incomplete dominance and codominance.
  • Understand the concept of multiple alleles and polygenic traits.
  • Interpret pedigree charts.
  • Know about sex-linked inheritance.

Success Criteria

  1. Explain Gregor Mendel’s laws of inheritance.
  2. Discuss the difference between genotypes and phenotypes.
  3. Label the three types of genotypes.
  4. Differentiate Monohybrid cross from Dihybrid cross
  5. Draw and label a Punnett square
  6. Predict outcomes of a Punnett square
  7. Explain the difference between incomplete dominance and codominance.
  8. Understand the concept of multiple alleles, polygenic traits.
  9. Understand the pedigree charts.
  10. Explain the sex-linked inheritance.
