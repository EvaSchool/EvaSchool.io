Blood: A Fluid Tissue

Overall Expectations

E.3 Demonstrate an understanding of animal anatomy and physiology and describe disorders of the respiratory, circulatory, and digestive systems.

Specific Expectations

E.3.3 Explain the anatomy of the circulatory system and its function in transporting substances that are vital to health.

Learning Goals

  • Have a deeper understanding about the Blood and blood Plasma.
  • Differentiate the cellular components of blood namely; red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.
  • Determine blood types.
  • Describe the Rhesus factor, blood substitutes, and artificial flood.
  • Understand the importance of Blood Vessels.
  • Describe the roles of arteries, veins, and capillaries in controlling blood flow in the circulatory system.
  • Explain blood pressure.
  • Understand the major roles of the lymphatic system in blood circulation.

Success Criteria

By the end of this lesson, I can…

  • Explain the importance and functions of Blood and Blood Plasma.
  • Differentiate the cellular components of blood namely; red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.
  • Identify the different blood types.
  • Describe the Rhesus factor, blood substitutes, and artificial flood.
  • Understand the importance of Blood Vessels.
  • Describe the roles of arteries, veins, and capillaries in controlling blood flow in the circulatory system.
  • Explain blood pressure.
  • Understand the major roles of the lymphatic system in blood circulation.
