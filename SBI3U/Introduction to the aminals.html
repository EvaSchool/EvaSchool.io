The Animals

                         

Overall Expectations

Throughout this course, students will:

B3. demonstrate an understanding of the diversity of living organisms in terms of the principles of taxonomy and phylogeny.

Specific Expectations

B2.1 use appropriate terminology related to biodiversity, including, but not limited to: genetic diversity, species diversity, structural diversity, protists, bacteria, fungi, binomial nomenclature, and morphology.

B2.2 classify, and draw biological diagrams of, representative organisms from each of the kingdoms according to their unifying and distinguishing anatomical and physiological characteristics (e.g., vertebrate or invertebrate organisms, vascular or nonvascular plants).

Learning Goals

Learn about Animals and its importance

  • Identify the characteristics and phylogeny of animals
  • Learn about the classification of animals.
  • Describe vertebrates, invertebrates, and deuterostomes.

Success Criteria

  • Understand the importance of animals in our lives.
  • Describe animals based on their ancestry and characteristics.
  • Classify animals in their kingdom and phylogeny.
  • Classify animals whether they are vertebrates, invertebrates, and deuterostomes.
