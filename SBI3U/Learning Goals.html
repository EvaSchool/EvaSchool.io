Phylogeny and Modern Taxonomy

Overall Expectations

By the end of this course, students will:

B3. demonstrate an understanding of the diversity of living organisms in terms of the principles of taxonomy and phylogeny.

Specific Expectations

B2.3 uses proper sampling techniques to collect various organisms from a marsh, pond, field, or other ecosystem, and classify the organisms according to the principles of taxonomy [PR, AI, C].

B3.1 explains the fundamental principles of taxonomy and phylogeny by defining concepts of taxonomic rank and relationship, such as genus, species, and taxon.

Learning Goals

  • Learn about Phylogeny
  • Interpret phylogenetic trees.
  • Learn about modern taxonomy and the
  • International Barcode of Life project.

Success Criteria

  • Define phylogeny
  • Illustrate, understand, and explain the phylogeny of living things; and
  • Understand modern taxonomy and learn the international barcode of life project.
