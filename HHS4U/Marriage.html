Marriage

Lesson Description

In this lesson delves into the institution of marriage, exploring its customs, traditions, and the impact of social norms and expectations. Students will learn how to record and organize information in various formats, analyze the influence of cultural and religious norms, and understand mate selection, courtship, and marriage customs across different cultures, religions, and historical periods. The lesson will also investigate the significance of recent demographic trends in intimate relationships. By the end of this lesson, students will have a nuanced understanding of the diversity of marriage practices and the impact of societal changes.

Overall Expectations

A3 Processing Information: assess, record, analyse, and synthesize information gathered through research and inquiry.

C2 The Effects on Intimate Relationships: demonstrate an understanding of the impact of norms, roles, and social institutions on intimate relationships.

D2 Trends and Challenges in Intimate Relationships: demonstrate an understanding of demographic and social trends and issues related to intimate relationships and of strategies for responding to challenges in those relationships.

Specific Expectations

A3.2 Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats (e.g., notes, graphic organizers, summaries, audio/digital records).

C2.1 Analyse ways in which social norms and expectations (e.g., cultural and religious norms; expectations of peers, friends, and parents) can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships.

C2.2 Explain mate selection, courtship, and marriage customs (e.g., arranged and free-choice marriages; use of matchmakers; civil, religious, and tribal marriage ceremonies; polygamy and monogamy) in various cultures, religions, and historical periods.

D2.1 Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to intimate relationships (e.g., with regard to adolescent sexual activity; cohabitation, marriage, and divorce rates; same-sex marriage; delayed age of marriage; the number of people living independently; serial monogamy; an increase in arranged marriages).

Learning Goals

  • Students will record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats. 
  • Students will analyze ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships.
  • Students will explain mate selection, courtship, and marriage customs in various cultures, religions, and historical periods.
  • Students will analyze the significance of recent demographic trends relating to intimate relationships.
