That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In conclusion, this exploration of Canada and its diverse family structures emphasizes a commitment to academic honesty by adhering to APA conventions for citations. We have delved into the varied functions of the family, from reproduction to socialization, maintenance, nurturance, social control, and production, examining how these functions manifest in diverse family forms such as single-parent, two-parent (both same-sex and heterosexual), grandparent-led, extended, and childless families. The analysis of historical evolution has considered complementary roles in different economic settings, while an assessment of current social trends highlights the impact of electronic media, climate change, health care, education, economic conditions, aging populations, and increasing ethno-cultural and religious diversity on family functioning. This comprehensive exploration offers a nuanced understanding of Canadian families, grounded in academic integrity and addressing both historical and contemporary dimensions.

Learning Goals

  • Describe the functions of the family, and explain the ways in which these functions are met in diverse family forms.
  • Analyse the impact of major social and economic changes on the historical evolution of the family.
  • Assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families.
  • Demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.
  • Clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources. 

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can describe the functions of the family, and explain the ways in which these functions are met in diverse family forms such as lone parent family, nuclear family, extended family, etc.
  • Can analyse the impact of major social, economic and legislative changes on the historical evolution of the family.
  • Can assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families.
  • Can demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.
  • Can clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.
