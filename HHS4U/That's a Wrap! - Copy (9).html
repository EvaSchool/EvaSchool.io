What have I learned?

In conclusion, an exploration of individual development across the lifespan integrates diverse theoretical perspectives from renowned theorists such as Sigmund Freud, Erik Erikson, Lev Vygotsky, Abraham Maslow, Charles Cooley, and Urie Bronfenbrenner, among others. These perspectives collectively illuminate the intricate processes shaping human growth, considering factors such as identity formation, cognitive development, basic needs fulfillment, social interactions, ecological influences, and evolutionary aspects. This comprehensive approach aligns with the expectation of explaining individual development throughout the lifespan, and the utilization of various recording formats, including notes, graphic organizers, summaries, and audio/digital records, proves crucial in capturing the multifaceted nature of these theoretical frameworks.

Learning Goals

  • Explain individual development throughout the lifespan, according to a variety of theoretical perspectives on development.
  • Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can explain individual development throughout the lifespan, according to the psychosocial theory, seasons of life, hierarchy of human needs, psychosexual theory, and symbolic interactionism.
  • Can record and organize information and key ideas using the Developmental Theories Template.
