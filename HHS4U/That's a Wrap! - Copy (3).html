What have I learned?

In conclusion, the social science lesson on data identification and interpretation emphasizes the pivotal role of data analysis in uncovering patterns and trends. It underscores the importance of distinguishing between qualitative and quantitative data and introduces the practical use of tables and graphs. The module highlights the need for researchers to pose thoughtful questions, going beyond surface-level observations to understand the 'why' behind observed patterns. This process aligns with specific expectations, including comparing results across different groups and effectively communicating findings using appropriate formats such as oral presentations, posters, or research reports. The interdisciplinary approach ensures that social scientists not only analyze data but also communicate insights in a compelling manner tailored to a specific purpose and audience.

Learning Goals

  • Analyse and interpret research information.
  • Use an appropriate format to communicate the results of their research and inquiry effectively for a specific purpose and audience.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can identify and interpret quantitative and qualitative data.
  • Can use the appropriate charts (e.g., bar graph, line graph) to communicate the results of your research and inquiry effectively for a specific purpose.
