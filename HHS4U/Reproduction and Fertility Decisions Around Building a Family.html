Reproduction and Fertility: Decisions Around Building a Family

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will explore the complex decisions surrounding family-building, considering factors like fertility, assisted reproductive technologies, birth control, religious beliefs, adoption, relationship stability, financial status, and cultural influences. They will also assess government policies supporting families, examine recent demographic trends in parent-child relationships, and analyze the impact of contemporary social trends and challenges on family life, such as electronic media, climate change, healthcare, education, and work-life balance.

Overall Expectations

C3 The Effects on Family and Parent-Child Relationships: demonstrate an understanding of factors that can affect decisions about whether to have and how to care for children, and of the impact of norms, roles, and social institutions on family and parent-child relationships.

D3 Trends and Challenges in the Family and in Parent-Child Relationships: demonstrate an understanding of demographic trends related to the family and to parent-child relationships and of the impact of social issues and challenges on family development.

Specific Expectations

C3.1 Explain a range of factors that can influence decisions about having children (e.g., fertility; access to assisted reproductive technologies; access to birth control; religious beliefs regarding birth control and abortion; adoption policies and practices; the stability of an intimate relationship; considerations regarding the number and spacing of children; age and health; financial status; educational, career, and personal goals; cultural/religious background; the influence of friends or family; concerns about over- consumption of resources).

C3.3 Assess government policy and legislation that is intended to support and protect families (e.g., parental and maternity leave benefits, protection from discrimination on the grounds of family status, protection against discrimination because of preg- nancy, the right to breastfeed in public, adoption rights for same-sex couples, subsidized childcare, grandparents’ rights).

D3.2 Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to parent-child relationships (e.g., with reference to family forms, birth rates, age at childbearing, child custody arrangements, caregiving responsibilities, life expectancy, employment status of family members, educational attainment).

D3.3 Assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families (e.g., with reference to electronic media, climate change, health care, education, economic conditions, the aging of the population, balancing work and family, increasing ethno cultural and religious diversity in Canada).

Learning Goals

  • Students will explain a range of factors that can influence decisions about having children.
  • Students will assess government policy and legislation that is intended to support and protect families.
  • Students will analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to parent-child relationships.
  • Students will assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families.
