Social Science Theories

Lesson Description

In this lesson on Social Science Theories offers an exciting journey into the intricate realm of family dynamics in Canada. Students will explore a range of topics related to Canadian families, such as the consequences of changes in marriage and divorce legislation on individuals and families and the impact of economic downturns on children, parents, and the elderly. The lesson aims to guide students in identifying potential research and inquiry topics, grasping key concepts, effectively recording and organizing information, and understanding the research process while fostering self-reflection and evaluation.

Overall Expectations

A1 Exploring: explore topics related to families in Canada, and formulate questions to guide their research.

A3 Processing Information: assess, record, analyse, and synthesize information gathered through research and inquiry.

A4 Communicating and Reflecting: communicate the results of their research and inquiry clearly and effectively, and reflect on and evaluate their research, inquiry, and communication skills.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 Explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada (e.g., the effects of changes in marriage and divorce legislation on individuals and families; the effects of economic downturns on children, parents, and the elderly) to identify topics for research and inquiry.

A1.2 Identify key concepts (e.g., through discussion, brainstorming, use of visual organizers) related to their selected topics.

A3.2 Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats (e.g., notes, graphic organizers, summaries, audio/digital records).

A4.4 Demonstrate an understanding of the general research process by reflecting on and evaluating their own research, inquiry, and communication skills.

Learning Goals

  • Students will explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada to identify topics for research and inquiry.
  • Students will Identify key concepts related to their selected topics.
  • Students will record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.
  • Students will demonstrate an understanding of the general research process by reflecting on and evaluating their own research, inquiry, and communication skills.
