Infertility

  • Given recent social trends, such as increased education levels, delayed marriage, and delayed childbearing, individuals and couples are often shocked to learn that they cannot have children or that having children is not as easy as they might have thought.

One in every four couples in developing countries was affected by infertility.


 - World Health Organization

Families that are faced with infertility have a number of options:

  • Families can choose to remain child-free. As you have discovered in previous activities, childfree families are becoming much more common.
  • Adoption is an option for some families. There are many different forms of adoption: international, private, or public agencies. Some families also choose the foster-to-adopt option as well. Families pursue the type of adoption that best suits their lifestyle and budget.

  • For other families, reproductive technologies provide solutions for infertility. This allows families the greatest chance of conceiving a child with the genetic makeup of one or both parents. It also affords families the option of carrying a child that may or may not have the genes of one or both parents, especially if there are maternal medical issues.
