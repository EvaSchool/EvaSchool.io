That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In summary, this lesson goes beyond honing skills in identifying main ideas and supporting evidence; it aligns with specific expectations crucial for effective research. By fostering a detective-like approach to reading, it teaches students to distinguish between topics and main ideas, promoting active reading and critical analysis. The lesson integrates these skills into the broader research process, emphasizing the synthesis of information and the formulation of conclusions. Additionally, it ensures alignment with expectations for selecting information from diverse sources, organizing findings, and demonstrating academic honesty through proper source documentation and communication using APA conventions. The exemplars provided illustrate skill progression and readiness to meet expectations related to research, synthesis, and effective communication in academic settings. 

Learning Goals

  • Locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources.
  • Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.
  • Synthesize findings and formulate conclusions.  
  • Demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.
  • Clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can locate and select the main idea and supporting evidence from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources.
  • Can record and organize information and key ideas using a summary organizer.
  • Can synthesize information by writing a summary.
  • Can demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of information generated through research (ex. Annotated bibliography).
  • Can clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources using annotations.
