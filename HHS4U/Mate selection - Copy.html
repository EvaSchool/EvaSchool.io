What have I learned?

In conclusion, the study of attraction, mate selection, and the development of intimate relationships incorporates a diverse range of theoretical perspectives, including psychodynamic, evolutionary, structural functionalist, conflict theory, feminist theory, and queer theory. Each offers unique insights into the complexities of human connections, addressing unconscious drives, ancestral influences, social stability, power dynamics, and inclusive perspectives. Comparatively, theories like evolutionary theory, filter theory, and social homogamy theory provide nuanced frameworks for understanding mate selection. Assessing these theories in light of current research adds a dynamic layer to our comprehension, acknowledging the evolving nature of the field. The integration of evidence-based evaluations equips us with a foundation for predicting and understanding future trends in attraction and mate selection.

Learning Goals

  • Explain the development of intimate relationships according to a variety of theoretical perspectives.    
  • Compare various theories of attraction and mate selection.
  • Assess various theories of attraction, mate selection, and the development of intimate relationships with reference to current research that supports or contradicts them.

Success Criteria
