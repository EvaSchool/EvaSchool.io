That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

The history of residential schools in Canada underscores the significant impact of government policies on families, particularly Indigenous communities. Assessing the dark chapter of residential schools reveals the profound consequences of cultural assimilation and abuse, prompting a critical examination of contemporary policies such as parental leave, discrimination protection, and childcare subsidies. Understanding the family as a socializing agent necessitates considering diverse cultures, historical periods, and religious backgrounds to shape inclusive policies that respect varying parenting practices, attitudes towards sexuality, and gender socialization. Comparative analyses of children's roles across cultures and historical periods inform contemporary issues like adoption rights for same-sex couples and grandparents' rights. Examining the broader influence of social institutions, including education, religion, economy, and politics, is crucial for crafting policies that support positive socialization experiences for all family members, acknowledging the lessons learned from the painful legacy of residential schools.

Learning Goals

  • Assess government policy and legislation that is intended to support and protect families.
  • Analyse the role of the family as an agent of socialization in different cultures and historical periods and among groups from various religious backgrounds.
  • Compare the roles of children in the family and society in various cultures and historical periods.
  • Analyse the impact of social institutions on the socialization of family members.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can assess the Canadian Residential Schools that was intended to support and protect families during its time of implementation.
  • Can analyse the role of the family as an agent of socialization during the period of the residential schools in Canada.
  • Can compare the roles of children in the family and society during the period of the residential schools in canada.
  • Can analyse how have government controls and interventions affected the lives of the indigenous people in Canada during the period of the residential schools.
