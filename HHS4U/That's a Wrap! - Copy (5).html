That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In conclusion, leaving home is a multifaceted journey deeply influenced by cultural and social norms, familial values, and societal expectations that shape decisions about education, careers, and relationships throughout one's lifespan. Balancing the diverse roles of student, volunteer, employee, friend, and caregiver often leads to personal conflicts, underscoring the complex nature of individual development. Social institutions, including family, media, education, and economic and political systems, play pivotal roles in shaping moral and ethical development, identity formation, and social mobility. Recent demographic trends and current social challenges further contribute to the evolving landscape of individual lives, emphasizing the need for a comprehensive understanding of the dynamic interplay between personal choices and external influences.

Learning Goals

  • Assess ways in which norms, values, and expectations influence individual decisions throughout the lifespan.
  • Explain how multiple roles can create personal conflict.
  • Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to the lives of individuals.
  • Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to the lives of individuals.
  • Assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges on individual development. 

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can ssess ways in which norms, values, and expectations influence your decisions in your transition to adulthood (ex. leaving home as a rite of passage for many young adults in Canada).
  • Can explain how multiple roles can create personal conflict in your transition to adulthood.
  • Can analyse the impact of social institutions on socialization throughout your lifespan, such as family, media, education, religion, economy and political systems.
  • Can analyse the significance of recent demographic trends in Canadian society and determine the norms that may influence the behaviour of Canadians leaving home by examining various patterns and trends.
  • Can assess the impact of technological revolution, sexual revolution, women’s movement and youth movement in the transition to adulthood of Canadians.
