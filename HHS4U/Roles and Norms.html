Roles and Norms

Lesson Description

This lesson focuses on understanding the roles and norms within Canadian families. It equips students with the skills to explore and research this topic while maintaining academic integrity. Students will learn to employ appropriate communication formats, utilize family-related terminology correctly, and effectively communicate their research results using APA conventions for source acknowledgment.

Overall Expectations

A3 Processing Information: assess, record, analyze, and synthesize information gathered through research and inquiry.

A4 Communicating and Reflecting: communicate the results of their research and inquiry clearly and effectively, and reflect on and evaluate their research, inquiry, and communication skills.

Specific Expectations

A3.4 Demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.

A4.1 Use an appropriate format (e.g., oral presentation, poster, multimedia presentation, research report, web page) to communicate the results of their research and inquiry effectively for a specific purpose and audience.

A4.2 Use terms relating to families in Canada correctly (e.g., family systems, life expectancy, replacement rate, primary and secondary data, socialization, and social policy).

A4.3 Clearly communicate the results of their inquiries (e.g., write clearly, organize ideas logically, use language conventions properly), and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources (e.g., generate a reference list in APA style, use in-text citations properly).

Learning Goals

  • Students will demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.
  • Students will use an appropriate format to communicate the results of their research and inquiry effectively for a specific purpose and audience.
  • Students will use terms relating to families in Canada correctly.
  • Students will clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.
