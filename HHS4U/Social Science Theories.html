Exploring the Social Sciences

Lesson Description

In this lesson on Exploring the Social Sciences, students will delve into the intricate world of family dynamics in Canada. Through a combination of discussion, brainstorming, and the use of visual organizers, they will unravel the effects of changes in marriage and divorce legislation on individuals and families, as well as the repercussions of economic downturns on children, parents, and the elderly. The primary objective of this lesson is to empower students to identify topics for research and inquiry, gain a deep understanding of key concepts, and formulate effective questions to guide their investigative journey. 

Overall Expectations

A1 Exploring: explore topics related to families in Canada, and formulate questions to guide their research.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 Explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada (e.g., the effects of changes in marriage and divorce legislation on individuals and families; the effects of economic downturns on children, parents, and the elderly) to identify topics for research and inquiry.

A1.2 Identify key concepts (e.g., through discussion, brainstorming, and use of visual organizers) related to their selected topics.

A1.3 Formulate effective questions to guide their research and inquiry.

Learning Goals

  • Students will explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada to identify topics for research and inquiry.
  • Students will identify key concepts related to their selected topics.
  • Students will formulate effective questions to guide their research and inquiry.
