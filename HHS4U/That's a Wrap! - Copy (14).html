That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In summary, this lesson thoroughly examines the myriad factors influencing decisions about family planning in Canada, encompassing fertility, access to assisted reproductive technologies, birth control, religious beliefs, adoption policies, relationship stability, considerations about the number and spacing of children, age, health, financial status, educational and career goals, cultural backgrounds, and the influence of friends and family. The analysis extends to government policies supporting families, recent demographic trends in parent-child relationships, and the impact of contemporary social trends and challenges, including electronic media, climate change, healthcare, education, economic conditions, the aging population, and increasing ethnocultural and religious diversity in Canada. This comprehensive exploration provides insight into the intricate dynamics shaping family decisions and their broader societal implications. 

Learning Goals

  • Explain a range of factors that can influence decisions about having children.
  • Assess government policy and legislation that is intended to support and protect families.
  • Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to parent-child relationships.
  • Assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can explain a range of factors that can influence decisions about having children such as fertility, access to assisted reproductive technologies, access to birth control, beliefs regarding birth control and abortion, adoption policies and practices, etc.
  • Can assess the Ontario Fertility Program that is intended to support and protect families.
  • Can analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to parent-child relationships with reference to birth rates, age of childbearing, educational attainment, etc.
  • Can assess the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges relating to the functioning of families with reference to health care.
