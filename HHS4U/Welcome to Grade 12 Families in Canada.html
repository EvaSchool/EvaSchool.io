Welcome to Grade 12 Families in Canada

This course enables students to draw on sociological, psychological, and anthropological theories and research to analyse the development of individuals, intimate relationships, and family and parent-child relationships. Students will focus on issues and challenges facing individuals and families in Canada’s diverse society. They will develop analytical tools that enable them to assess various factors affecting families and to consider policies and practices intended to support families in Canada. They will develop the investigative skills required to conduct and communicate the results of research on individuals, intimate relationships, and parent-child relationships.


Unit Title

Description

Unit One- Introducing the Family: Methodology and Theoretical Perspectives

Students will explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada to identify topics for research and inquiry. They will learn to formulate effective questions to guide their research and inquiry and record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats. Students will demonstrate an understanding of the general research process by reflecting on and evaluating their own research, inquiry, and communication skills. Students will also learn to locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources and assess various aspects of information gathered from primary and secondary sources.

Unit Two- The Transition to Adulthood

Students will learn to demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research and use an appropriate format to communicate the results of their research and inquiry effectively for a specific purpose and audience. They will learn to explain individual development throughout the lifespan, according to a variety of theoretical perspectives on development and record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats. Students will also learn to assess research on sex-based similarities and differences as well as gender-based similarities and differences, ways in which norms, values, and expectations influence individual decisions throughout the lifespan and the impact of current social trends, issues, and challenges on individual development.

Unit Three-Intimate Relationships and Marriage

Students will learn to locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources. They will record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats and clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.They will also analyse ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships. Students will also learn to outline strategies for avoiding and responding to various types of violence and abuse in intimate relationships.
Unit Four -Parent-Child Relationships

In this unit, students will describe the functions of the family, explain the ways in which these functions are met in diverse family forms and analyze the impact of major social and economic changes on the historical evolution of the family. They will also assess government policy and legislation that is intended to support and protect families and analyze the role of the family as an agent of socialization in different cultures and historical periods and among groups from various religious backgrounds. Students will also learn to compare the roles of children in the family and society in various cultures and historical periods and analyze the impact of social institutions on the socialization of family members.

Exam

Rich Task Summative 30%

 This is a summative task assigned at the end of the course that brings together many of the expectations covered throughout the course and is created to assess and evaluate a student’s ability to demonstrate their understanding of the expectations through not only the product, but through conversation and observation.
