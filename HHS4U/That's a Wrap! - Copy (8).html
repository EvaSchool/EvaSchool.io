That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In conclusion, this lesson not only engages learners in a thoughtful exploration of relationships but also prompts a nuanced analysis of how social norms and expectations, including cultural and religious influences, impact the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships. The visual representation of a safe space encourages critical reflection on societal expectations, emphasizing the need to navigate and challenge norms affecting communication, respect, trust, support, and equality. Additionally, the lesson equips students with strategies for recognizing and responding to various types of violence and abuse in relationships, promoting the creation of safe environments. In terms of information literacy, the lesson encourages the use of diverse primary and secondary sources, fostering a well-rounded understanding of healthy relationship dynamics and empowering students with critical thinking skills for real-world applications.

Learning Goals

  • Analyse ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships
  • Outline strategies for avoiding and responding to various types of violence and abuse in intimate relationships
  • Locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can analyse ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships
  • Can outline strategies for avoiding and responding to various types of violence and abuse in intimate relationships
  • Can locate and select information relevant to your investigations on domestic abuse and/or intimate partner violence from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources
