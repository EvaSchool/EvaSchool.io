That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In summary, this lesson on studying individuals, partners, and families introduces us to Social Science and the Research Inquiry Process. Our focus is on exploring diverse topics related to families in Canada, such as the effects of changing marriage and divorce laws or economic downturns on different age groups. To meet specific expectations, we engage in identifying key concepts through methods like discussion and visual organizers, allowing us to formulate effective research questions. This structured approach not only enhances our research skills but also contributes to a deeper understanding of the intricate dynamics shaping individuals and families amidst societal changes in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada to identify topics for research and inquiry.
  • Identify key concepts related to their selected topics.
  • Formulate effective questions to guide their research and inquiry. 

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can identify topics for research and inquiry related to families in Canada.
  • Can demonstrate a better understanding of key concepts and how to identify them in your selected topics.
