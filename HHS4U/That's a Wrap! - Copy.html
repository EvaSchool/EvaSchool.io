That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In conclusion, this introductory lesson on social science theories serves as a cornerstone for delving into diverse topics related to families in Canada, aligning with the expectations of identifying research topics and key concepts. The analogy of the blind men and the elephant underscores the importance of considering varied perspectives, mirroring the complexity of family-related issues in Canadian society. The lesson encourages students to apply these metaphorical insights to their research, emphasizing the need for a multifaceted approach in understanding complex family dynamics. The distinction between macro-sociological and micro-sociological theories aligns with the expectation of identifying key concepts, fostering a holistic understanding of family-related phenomena. The overview of social science theories provides students with diverse analytical lenses, encouraging critical thinking about the multifaceted nature of family dynamics. Furthermore, the lesson promotes various formats for recording and organizing information, aligning with the expectation of demonstrating a grasp of the research process and fostering critical thinking and research skills among students as they embark on their academic journey.

Learning Goals

  • Explore a variety of topics related to families in Canada to identify topics for research and inquiry.
  • Identify key concepts related to their selected topics.
  • Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the general research process by reflecting on and evaluating their own research, inquiry, and communication skills.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can explore the different social science theories that can be used to explain patterns and behaviors seen in Canadian families.
  • Can identify key points while applying a selected social science theory in your research and inquiry. 
  • Can record and analyze information and key points using the Theory Application Template.
  • Can demonstrate an understanding of the theory application process by reflecting on and evaluating your own research, inquiry, and communication skills.
