What have I learned?

In conclusion, our examination of patterns and trends in Canadian marriages reveals a dynamic landscape shaped by legal structures, cultural diversity, and changing societal attitudes. The information has been effectively recorded using various formats, including notes and summaries, providing a comprehensive understanding. The impact of social norms on healthy intimate relationships is evident in the legal aspects, such as the Marriage Act and the legalization of same-sex marriage in 2005. Analysis of mate-selection and marriage customs, from arranged marriages to modern consent-based approaches, offers insights into the influence of cultural and religious norms. Recent demographic trends, including increased cohabitation and delayed age of marriage, reflect evolving patterns in mate-selection and courtship. In essence, the multifaceted exploration underscores the importance of adapting to evolving norms and the balance between tradition and modernity in shaping Canadian marriages.

Learning Goals

  • Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.
  • Analyse ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships.
  • Explain mate-selection, courtship, and marriage customs in various cultures, religions, sand historical periods.
  • Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to intimate relationships.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you...

  • Can record and organize information and key ideas using the Marriage Patterns in Canada template.
  • Can analyse ways in which social norms and expectations can influence the establishment and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships.
  • Can explain mate-selection, courtship, and marriage customs in various cultures, religions, and historical periods such as arranged marriages.
  • Can analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to intimate relationships with regard to divorce rates in Canada.
