That's a Wrap!

What have I learned?

In concluding the research inquiry process in social science, researchers must adeptly navigate through a variety of primary and secondary sources, ranging from interviews and observations to peer-reviewed journals and literature reviews. This selection process is accompanied by a critical assessment of gathered information, evaluating aspects such as accuracy, relevance, reliability, inherent values, and biases. Upholding academic honesty, researchers diligently document their sources and adhere to APA conventions for citations. The ultimate aim is to clearly communicate research results, requiring a logical organization of ideas and proper language conventions. By following these principles, researchers contribute to the scholarly integrity and excellence within the academic community, ensuring the thorough and credible execution of social science investigations. 

Learning Goals

  • Locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources.
  • Assess various aspects of information gathered from primary and secondary sources.
  • Demonstrate academic honesty by documenting the sources of all information generated through research.
  • Clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources. 

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you…

  • Can locate and select information relevant to your investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources.
  • Can use the Information Evaluation Criteria (CRAAP) properly to assess various aspects of information gathered from primary and secondary sources.
  • Can use databases and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.
