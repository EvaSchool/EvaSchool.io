What have I learned?

In conclusion, the exploration of relationships in Canada draws on a diverse range of sources, including primary materials such as interviews, surveys, and census data, as well as secondary sources like literature reviews and articles. The use of various formats, such as notes and graphic organizers, ensures the organized recording of information, aligning with expectations. The communication of results follows APA conventions, demonstrating precision and transparency. Analyzing differences among roles in intimate relationships across social, historical, and ethnocultural contexts, and examining recent demographic trends in adolescent sexual activity, cohabitation, marriage, and other aspects, contributes to a comprehensive understanding. This synthesis of information and analytical approach offers valuable insights into the evolving dynamics of intimate relationships in Canada.

Learning Goals

  • Locate and select information relevant to their investigations from a variety of primary sources and/or secondary sources.
  • Record and organize information and key ideas using a variety of formats.
  • Clearly communicate the results of their inquiries, and follow APA conventions for acknowledging sources.
  • Analyse differences among roles in intimate relationships in various social, historical, and ethnocultural contexts.
  • Analyse the significance of recent demographic trends relating to intimate relationships.
